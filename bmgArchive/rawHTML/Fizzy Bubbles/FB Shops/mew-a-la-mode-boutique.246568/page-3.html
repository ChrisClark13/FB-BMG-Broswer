Couple of size changes

Ymir the Amaura will be increasing in height and weight by 2x (putting him at 2.6m tall and 50.4kg). The reason for this is somewhat unknown. His mother was the matriarch of an ancient migrating herd of Aurorus who came to the valley where Jayson's base is, to hatch their eggs. Ymir's egg was the largest of them all, Jayson assumed this was due to his mother also being large and being the leader of the herd. Ymir was clearly larger than the other hatchlings and continued to grow rapidly as he was able to feed from the trees more easily. His size eventually became an issue though as he began to get a little rough with the other hatchlings, unaware of the strength that came with his size. His mother was forced to abandon him in the valley.

Alexander the Nidoking will be increasing in height and weight by 1.5x (putting him at 2.1m tall and 93kg). Alexander, like many other Pokemon, was abandoned by his trainer at the Adoption Center. Enraged that his trainer would treat him in such a way, he began to rigorously train and bulk himself up, in hopes of finding a trainer that would appreciate him. Sure enough, Jayson instantly recognized the then Nidoran's potential and together they worked to grow Alexander into a bulky Nidoking.

Argo the Lapras will be increasing in height and weight by 1.5x (putting him at 3.75m tall and 330kg). Argo is a rather old Lapras, Jayson encountered the veteran Pokemon in the lake next to his base. He has no idea how the Lapras ended up in the lake, but it didn't seem to trouble the water type, who had grown larger over the years by feeding on the rich food source provided by the lake.

Ajax the Chesnaught will be increasing in height and weight by 1.2x (putting him at 1.92m tall and 108kg). Ajax was reluctant to evolve from a Chespin into a Quilladin, not wanting to turn himself into a derpy pudgemon. Instead he chose to store up energy, his body slowly swelling as he resisted the natural urge to evolve. Eventually he was put in a situation where he had to evolve to save a new found friend, but there was still no way he was being a Quilladin. He quickly grab ahold of a Rare Candy and started the evolution process again, evolving into a Chesnaught before anyone could even see him become a Quilladin. The sudden surge of energy from back to back evolutions caused him to grow larger than expected.

The weather fluctuated wildly over Mt. Neuf- blazing sunlight alternated rather irregularly with mild hailstorms. And for those who could see the pair of Pokémon wandering down the long, winding trail leading down the mountain and to the ground, the source was rather apparent.

Two Ninetales, each a different form from the other, were walking side by side. In all probability unrelated, yet they considered each other sisters. While one was a Fire-type and the other Ice/Fairy, they had one big thing in common aside from species- they both made the decision to leave their previous Trainer.

"Do you think we did the right thing, Sunny?" asked the Alolan Ninetales. "Leaving Keith, I mean. He took us both in from the Adoption Center, helped us grow and evolve..."

"I know what you mean, Ava," nodded Sunny. The past few days left Sunny nigh unrecognizable- one of her defining traits as a part of Keith's team was her coat of reddish-orange fur, for she had retained the color scheme of her Vulpix stage. A visual indicator that while she had evolved, she had not lost her childlike innocence and curiosity. But since traversing Mt. Neuf, since she and Ava tackled many adversaries and dangers, each having saved the other's life many times over, Sunny had matured considerably, taking charge as Ava's older sister, and this was reflected in her fur as well. Over the past few days, her coat had gradually changed color. Wherever reddish fur was shed, cream-colored fur grew in its place, and now, the Normal Form Ninetales had the same color fur as was usual for her kind. "But he did say- he always said- that if any of us, at any time, decided we wished to seek our fortunes elsewhere, then we could leave, without fear of hurt feelings." Sunny turned to establish eye contact with her Alolan counterpart. "Keith wants what's best for us," she stated. "Even if that means saying goodbye."

Ava sighed. "I know," she conceded. "And you're right... I, too, feel like there is a better Trainer for us out there. I just don't know how I feel it."

"It is in our tails," stated Sunny. "Each of our tails contains a different mystic power. We Ninetales, whatever form, possess great supernatural powers, all contained within our tails. Yes... Keith was good to us, and good for us, there's no denying that. I meant what I said when we said our goodbyes- that I hope we meet again. And I know he knows it, too."

"I hope so, too," said Ava. "I miss him. But... I'm really curious as to what Trainer we'll end up with," she added.

"We will soon find out, I'm certain," smiled Sunny.

Ava tilted her head. "How do you know?" she asked.

"Because Luna has been telepathically conveying directions to me ever since we started to exit this mountain," stated Sunny. "We are close."

And sure enough, they soon came across a campsite beside the mountain trail. A singular sleeping bag, covered in images of Unown T, Unown E, Unown R, and Unown I, was rolled up and resting on a tree stump. A young girl was tending to a fire that was cooking up some soup, while a rather familiar Starmie shot bubbles of pure heat at the flames to intensify them.

And in an instant, the pair of Ninetales were mobbed. An Inkay had swooped in, tackle-hugging the pair of them. A Swoobat was not far behind, and the Starmie approached as well. Ava and Sunny couldn't help but laugh and smile at this reunion.

Once they'd backed off, the young girl approached. She wore nothing on her feet at all despite being outdoors, and seemed quite unbothered by this. "You're Sunny and Ava, then, right?" she asked them. "Luna said you were coming."

"Indeed," confirmed Sunny. "Ava and I decided we wished to find a different Trainer. We feel that Keith, while a capable and competent Trainer in his own right, and a dear friend of ours, would not be ideal for helping us realize our full potential."

Ava shook her head. "Sunny, how is she supposed to understand what you just-"

"George, Luna, and Andromeda feel the same way," Terri said to Sunny, nodding understandingly. "I can try and do what you don't think he can, but I make no promises."

"...You can understand what we say?" asked Ava, astonished.

"Yep," smiled Terri. "So, do you two want to come with me?"

The pair of Ninetales exchanged looks. Just looks, not words- Ava wasn't completely certain, but her sister seemed to be, and ultimately, this was good enough for the Alolan Ninetales. They then reached back, sticking their snouts into their tails, and each pulled out her own Premier Ball and handed it over to Terri, who accepted them gladly. Then, she hugged the pair of Ninetales she was now glad to call part of her team. Making sure to not pull on any of the eighteen tails they had between them, of course- getting cursed was probably not the best way to get this started.
