Mew à la Mode Boutique

In a suburb town near Fizzytopolis, in a busy shopping district, lies a gargantuan yet quaint-looking shop. Its design is definitely taken from Kalosian architecture, complete with the stylish light pink wooden sign hanging on the wall with "Mew à la Mode Boutique" carved in, and the white and blue-striped awnings shading over the front. As you pull open the door, it hits the cute bell hanging from the ceiling, announcing your arrival over the sound of the soft, smooth jazz music playing through the facility's speakers. When you walk inside, the boutique's impressive capacity is more understood, as it is adapted to welcome Pokémon of any size so they can freely shop through the aisles of clothing, dyes, changing rooms, trimming stations and other specialized departments.

“Bonjour!” a young woman greets you with a warm smile while subtly sliding her 3DS behind her counter. “Bienvenue to Mew à la Mode Boutique, where uniqueness and style truly shines! whether it is through accessories, dyes, or some other little je-ne-sais-quoi, we guarantee to transform your Pokémon just as one-of-a-kind on the outside than they already are on the inside! We also offer special services, including, item customization to fit them with your own personal touch, and spriting to capture your Pokémon's or your items' new look!”

“Oh 'scuse-moi,” she chuckled to herself softly while offering her hand to shake, “I forgot to introduce myself! I'm Mélina Loiseau, owner of this boutique! Now then, where shall we begin our tour?”


Pokémon-Related Services

Special Characteristics Registration
We all know that all our Pokémon are unique, whenever in obvious, subtle or unusual ways. As the main attraction of our shop, we are able to officially keep on record some physical aspects your partners may have!​

Some aspects of Pokémon can different from the usual: coloring, birthmarks, scars, and alternative height and size... all should be registered here!

General Special Characteristic Rules:
(Applies to all Special Characteristics)
  • All special characteristics requires a justification/origin related to it. No need of an essay – just a short summary will do! It's not just a paperwork thing - we love to hear the stories!
  • You can put as many characteristics as you want at any time (ex.: Add one scar then add a different one later), as long as it does not overwrite an already registered characteristic (ex.: After setting an altered height/size, you cannot set a new one after; after choosing colors, you cannot set new colors)
    • If you want to replace a characteristic, you need to use a Natural Spray to remove the old one before adding the new one. (See information on the Natural Spray below for details.)
  • By default, none of the special characteristics, including weight/height, does not give anyadvantage or disadvantage to the Pokémon. (All moves and actions are considered as if done by an unmodified Pokémon.)
    • That said, it is at the updator's discretion to choose otherwise, but this should be considered as the exception and not the norm.
  • Trading does not get rid of any of the special characteristics.
    • Note however that when an altered Pokémon is given for adoption, it loses all of its special characteristics. As unusual Pokémon are more difficult to be adopted, we have the pleasure to help the Adoption Center by sending them constant donations of Natural Sprays!
  • If, for any reason, your Special Characteristic request has been denied, you will not be charged.

Available Special Characteristics and related rules:

Coloring ($100): Color(s) of the fur, skin, scales, feathers, etc. that varies from the normal colors. We are the only place in the world able to register this particular and unusual trait, so it's no surprise it is the most popular characteristic to register!​
  • Colored Pokémon that looks too similar to their shiny form are not allowed, therefore any request to register such a coloring will be denied.
    • As colors and whatnot can be subjective, the decision of denying or accepting colors in terms of the shiny coloring can be reversed, if the original judgment/decision has been proven incorrect.
​
Birthmarks ($50): Includes freckles, spots, and stripes.​
​
​
Scars ($50): Includes scratches, burns and other lasting marks made by injuries. Life as a Pokémon can be really rough. It is no surprise to see a scar here and there.​
  • Deformities and handicaps are strictly not allowed.
​
Size ($100 + $50 for disproportion): Different sizing (height and/or weight) of the Pokémon compared to the average height and weight stated in the Pokédex. I mean, Pokémon comes in all shapes and sizes between species, but also within the same species!​
  • All new sizes must be registered as a proportion (a multiplier or a percentage) comparing to the Pokédex sizes. (No feet/inches, no meters, no kilograms, no pounds, etc.)
  • Different proportions can be applied to weight and height. However, there is a $50 deformity fee if the proportions between weight and height are different.
  • A size of a Pokémon can be, at its lowest, a third (0.33x or 33%) of its average Pokédex size.
  • A size of a Pokémon can be, at most, double (2x or 200%) of its average Pokédex size.
  • If a sizing is judged to be too exaggerated, unbelievable or unhealthy for the Pokémon's species, the resize request can be denied.
    • If the decision of the acceptance or denial of the resizing is later deemed unfair or incorrect, it can be reversed.
​
Auras (Unavailable): A special glow that surrounds a Pokémon.​
As the accessibility and purpose of auras are still in discussion, it is currently only available when your Pokémon have a maximum Bond.​

For $25, we can transfer the data of your current Pokédex into a brand new, basic red Pokédex from any region (including regions from spin-off games) of your choice!

We can also custom design your Pokédex, for a fee of $50. Just say what color/design you want, and we'll take care of the rest!

Spriting: General Rules

Whether you change your Pokémon's appearance or get new accessories, we can commemorate the moment with a portrait! What's the use of changing your appearance when you can't show it off?

​
When changing a Pokémon's appearance, buying a non-official accessory or customizing an item, you can request us to create a small sprite of them, just for you! However, due to the unusual nature and work needed of this service, special rules are applied:

  • Each sprite request has a fee of $100 (in addition to the price of the characteristic/item/accessory).​
  • Sprites have to be requested; Not requesting a sprite will not get you one. Seems obvious, but our lawyers ask us to remind you, just in case!​
  • A member can only request 4 sprites every 30 days. Art takes time! You cannot rush art!​
  • Due to various reasons (time constraints, changes not visible on sprites, sprite too complex, etc) it is possible a sprite cannot be created. Whenever it is the case, you will be notified as such in your confirmation, and you will not be charged for the (nonexistent) sprite.​
    • After such “no sprite” notification, feel free to ask someone else to do the spriting!​
    • There may be cases where only a partial sprite can be created. (For example, when a sprite of a Pokémon with special characteristics can be made, but their accessory(ies) couldn't make it into the sprite.) Whenever it is the case, you will be contacted (via PM/VM/Discord/Skype) to warn you about it and to let you decide if you want to proceed with your sprite request or not.​
  • Due to time restraints, creating and giving the sprite may be delayed. Again, if it's the case, we will tell you in your confirmation, and we will let you know (via PM/VM/Discord/Skype) when those sprites will be made and edited in the confirmation post!​
    • Please, do not request someone else do the sprite when its creation is delayed.​

Notes:

Boutique Zone Rewards: General Rules
​
As a reward for an adventure in a zone, it is possible to get a specific but free use of a service or an item from the Boutique.

All rules above concerning those services and items are still applied. However, there is rules that applies only for zone rewards, and some existing rules are relaxed specifically for zone rewards:

  • Your updator has to clearly state that the Boutique-related reward is given and that it has to be registered/picked up in the Boutique. Otherwise, it's Boutique business as usual.​
    • Please make sure you specify in your post that it is a zone reward registration/pickup; Otherwise a normal request will be assumed.​
  • While registration/collect of a Boutique-related zone reward is free, (I mean, it's a reward, jeez) spriting fees are still applied if requested.​
  • When registering zone-acquired special characteristics, the justification/origin needs to be a detailed but abridged version of where/how the special characteristic was acquired in the adventure. A link to said adventure is not required, but highly recommended.​
  • If a zone-acquired special characteristic should overwrite an existing one, the Natural Spray to remove only the existing, overwritten special characteristic will be free.​
  • As zone-adventuring often gets you in unusual places and situations, some Boutique rules concerning special characteristics can be more flexible, depending on how fitting it is to the scenario of the adventure:​
    • Size: The Pokémon's proportions can go beyond the minimum/maximum limits (so smaller than 33% or bigger than 200%), and more leeway can be given for different weight/height proportions.​



FAQ
​
My Pokémon is really, really special-looking. I have a hard time what would fit in which special characteristics so I get the prices right. Is there a trick?
Wow, your Pokémon must be quite the unique fellow if you don't know which detail would fit in which characteristic! Well you can simply contact me through your favorite contact thingy (not posting in the thread) and describe me what you want to do, and I'll help you get an estimate! Just make sure you still write your justification/story behind the characteristics!

Does special characteristics carry over after my Pokémon changes form (evolution, Mega Evolution, Dynamax, Pokémon-specific forms, etc)?
Unless you specify that they don't (either the form have the normal species' characteristics, or have special characteristics different than normal and the previous special characteristics), yes they do!
Sidenote: If you do specify that a special characteristic changes does not carry over the different form(s) (ex.: one form has a one coloring and the other form has another coloring), this counts as the "same" characteristic, therefore only needs one registration/payment!

I just got a Boutique Zone Reward that gives a special characteristic! I do love it, but I got an idea that the same special characteristic changes after changing form. What do I do?
You would think this would fit in the sidenote mentioned in the previous question, but sadly it does not. Unless the reward includes a specifiation the different form(s) have a different special characteristic, the reward's special characteristic will carry over all the forms. That doesn't mean you can't do your idea though! When you pick up your (free) reward, you can also purchase a Natural Spray and register a tweaked special characteristic to include the change on that other form!

I'm in a bit of an awkward situation: it has just been discovered the species of my Pokémon with special characteristics has a new form they can change into! Do you know what will happen whenever he turns into that new form?
I'm no Pokémon Professor, but I am an expert in special characteristics! In general, it'll follow the answer of the previous question: Your Pokémon's special coloring will carry over! However, if you discover that your Pokémon in their new form doesn't carry over their special characteristics in any way, please quote your Pokémon's characteristic registration and state the differences of that new form to register it.

My Pokémon have the same visual characteristics as the sprites/models of an older/new Pokémon game/generation. Would it be considered a special characteristic I have to register here, despite those characteristics being different from those of other games/generation?
Nope! If the characteristics (ex.: colors, fur patterns, etc) of your Pokémon matches those of the (non-shiny) sprites/models from any Pokémon game/generation, you don't need to register here! You could say they're all simply official color variants of the same species. To make things clear, here's some examples:
  • Vaporeon, in recent generations, has generally a light blue color. However, Gen 3 Vaporeon are more of a cyan color, and in Gen 2 they have a dark pastel blue hue. All these are official color variants. Therefore, a Vaporeon in Fizzy Bubbles can be of any of these colors, without the need to register here.
  • From Gen 1 to 7, Eevee had no physical differences between female and male. However, the Let's Go series introduced female Eevee with a heart-shaped tail tip pattern. Due to this, a female Eevee's tail in Fizzy Bubbles can have either the normal jagged pattern or the heart-shaped pattern.
If you're still not sure if you need to register your Pokémon's characteristic(s) or not, feel free to ask me for clarifications!

I'm a bit worried about my color registration being denied for being too close to a shiny. Any pointers?
Aw, no need to worry about it that much! Just do what you wanna do, and if I turn it down, I can tell you how you can make it right! But if you really want to know, here's some tips:
  • Just look over at the shiny form sprites in the Pokédex source of your choice (Bulbapedia, Serebii, etc). Does the coloring of your Pokémon look like that? No? Then you're likely safe! If it does... well, let's look over the specifics below.​
  • Is that shiny form already looks like the normal one? Those frustrates me too... Well you don't have to worry much, because it would be very very silly to be denied for looking like a shiny when the normal form already does...!​
  • Is it just small part of your Pokémon that looks like a shiny's? If that's the case, you're likely ok! If it's a pretty big part (or main part) of the Pokémon though... be careful with that.​
  • Your Pokémon shares the same colors as a shiny... but said "shiny colors" are placed on different parts of the body compared to an actual shiny? Oh you're definitely safe!​
  • Your Pokémon has the same color as a shiny, but not the same shade? Eeeeeeeh now we're getting on the suggestive side of things. I say that if the shade of your Pokémon is noticeably different than the shiny's (like, your Pokémon is light pink when its species' shiny form is dark pink), then it'll pass. If the different is not very big... your registration will likely be turned down.​
My Pokémon has freckles/spots/stripes. Do I have to pay for each freckle/spot/stripe?
Whoa, no! That would be totally unfair to you, especially if your Pokémon has a gazillion of them. Just pay once for each kind of birthmark (once for freckles, again for stripes, etc.) If you registered, say, freckles on one part of your Pokémon's body and later (ie another post) add more freckles on a different part of the body though, well unfortunately you'll have to pay the characteristics registration fees again.

I'm a bit confused about the sizing. What is the "size"?
Size is just a catch-all term, used for simplicity's sake, for the height, the weight, or both!

What about the length and/or the width of a Pokémon?
Unfortunately, the Pokédex doesn't really keep track of that information, so we can't really have a say in terms of that ourselves, including registering it... but as a good estimate of sorts, the height and length of a Pokémon are linked (since Pokémon that are longer are able to "stand" taller), while the width and the weight of the Pokémon are linked (as weight means muscle mass and fat (among other things), which usually results being apparent in terms of width).

I don't fully understand the "Different proportions" and "deformity fee" rules with sizing. How does it work?
Basically, your Pokémon doesn't need to have one proportion (multiplier/percentage) to be applied to both height and weight; You can choose the size of the two independently! However, when that proportion is different between the height and weight, the Pokémon will be considered as deformed (as the general proportions of it isn't normal), so an extra fee has to be applied along the sizing fee. As examples:
  • One trainer wants to register their Pokémon with a height and weight x2/200% the normal size. As that proportion is used for both height and weight, the Pokémon isn't considered as deformed; therefore the trainer only pays the sizing fee.​
  • Another trainer wants to register their Pokémon with a height of x0.5 and no changes in the weight (x1). Since the proportions of the height and weight are different, the Pokémon is considered as deformed; therefore the trainer must pay a deformity fee along the sizing fee.​
Now I'm worried that the sizing I want to register is too unbelievable for my Pokémon. What should I do to make sure it doesn't get denied?
Awwww, we went through the same thing with the colors! Don't worry about it much, if your request is denied, I'll be telling you why and how to change things to make it pass! But ok ok, I'll tell you some ideas on how to make sure you get a green light on your first try:
  • Your Pokémon can be either bigger or smaller, but wouldn't it be a bit strange and worrisome if a Pokémon that's already very small gets even smaller, or that a huge Pokémon gets even more gargantuesque? Yup, you guessed it, it's two ways to get turned down in terms of sizing. So keep in mind the general size of the species of your Pokémon, and try not to go too far in the side that it is already in!​
  • Although it is possible for your Pokémon to be taller yet weighting less than normal, or to be smaller yet weighting more than normal, but as a general rule of thumb, be careful around going on opposite "directions" with the height and weight. We take seriously the health of the Pokémon that come here, and that includes with their BMI!​
  • If your Pokémon happen to have a "real world" counterpart and you are trying to match it in terms of size... then that goes into your favor, especially if you tell me in your explanation of the sizing!​
I already have a custom Pokédex/key stone accessory/Z-accessory, but the style doesn't really fit me anymore. Can I customize it again?
Of course! You'll have to pay for the customization fee again, unfortunately.

With my Pokémon, do I have to pay for their accessories and special characteristics for every evolution?
Nope! I mean, you're not buying two or three of the same accessory, nor are registering the same Pokémon's characteristics more than once! The only thing you'll need to pay for every evolution though is the sprites (as requested).

I want a custom accessory that holds both my Key Stone and my Z-Crystals! What shall I do?
Well, since the Z-Power Ring in the games already can hold both Z-Crystals and a Key Stone, just customize your Z-Power Ring into whatever you want, and you're good to go with your Key Stone! (And if you're buying a new Z-Accessory, it automatically supports and includes a Key Stone.)

Shoot, I bought myself a new Z-Power Ring, but now I want two seperate accessories for my Key Stone and my Z-Crystals. What now?
Just buy a new Key Stone accessory!
