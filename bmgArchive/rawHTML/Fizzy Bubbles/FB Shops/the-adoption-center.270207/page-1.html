The Adoption Center​


Adopting a Pokémon from the Adoption Center!
Adopting a Pokémon is easy -- you just need to pay the adoption fee, state which Pokémon you would like, and provide a Poké Ball to house it in. A trainer may adopt a Pokémon once per calendar month.

Dropping off a Pokémon at the Adoption Center!
A Pokémon may be dropped off at the Adoption Center at any time, free of charge. Pokémon dropped off here receive one of two types of care: Basic Care or Advanced Care. A Pokémon receiving Basic Care will be fed and watered, but otherwise left to its own devices. They will revert back to Lv. 01, forget any special moves they know, and may even return to a previous stage of evolution. A Pokémon receiving Advanced Care will continue to receive basic training by the staff. They will maintain their level and evolutionary stage, as well as any special moves they know.

Rules & Regulations!
★ When adopting a Pokémon, here is a flat fee of $200, as well as additional fees per each special move ($20) and additional level ($10) the Pokémon has.
★ When adopting a Pokémon, please quote the list of available Pokémon. Please include only the Pokémon you are adopting in your quote.
★ When adopting a Pokémon, please link to the bank withdrawal to confirm you have the needed funds. Linking to a Pokeball is not required.

Willa wandered throughout the Adoption Center, Scatterbug on her shoulder. She looked over the various Pokémon in there, wordlessly contemplating what she saw. The Voltorb and the several Darumaka stood out right away as being obviously stronger than their fellow occupants, and there was something almost endearingly familiar about how they seemed to act. She supposed she might mention this to Keith later on, but right now, she had a decision to make. She softly chuckled at the playful Skitty, she regarded the focused Meditite with interest, she considered the considerable potential in the small Larvesta...

Something didn't feel right. Willa stiffened, Scatterbug starting to tremble nervously on her shoulder. And then, on the spot, she wheeled around, just as a pair of long beaks were ready to pluck the Scatterdust Pokémon off her shoulder. The Doduo froze, caught in the act, and withdrew their heads, regarding Willa with interest.

"Sc-sc-scatterb-bug," shuddered Scatterbug, still reeling from her close call.

"Do do," cawed both heads simultaneously. There was something weird about how utterly coordinated their actions and movements seemed to be. Willa produced her Pokédex and pointed it at Doduo.

"Doduo, the Twin Bird Pokémon. A Normal and Flying-type," droned the device. "Doduo's heads contain identical brains linked by telepathy, and both heads will never sleep at the same time so that one may keep watch. On rare occasions, an uncommon mutation will cause one Doduo to have two different brains."

"I see," murmured Willa. "You two are in constant sync, then, it would seem. I'm impressed," she stated. "I would like you to come along with me if you like," she offered. "I have but one condition, though," she added before Doduo could respond. "You are not, under any circumstance, to try and eat my Scatterbug, or any Pokémon owned by myself or by my boyfriend," she said in a quiet yet ominous voice that said quite clearly that she meant business. "Is this acceptable? I assure you there'll be no shortage of food for you if that's your concern."

Doduo contemplated this. Once again, she impressed her by getting straight to the point, right down to the promise of alternative options for food aside from that Scatterbug. She had impressed the Twin Bird Pokémon even more upon correctly deducing someone was sneaking up behind her, too. Moreover... this made a very refreshing change from the constant onslaught of prospective adopters who were unnerved by her behavior. Evidently most Trainers were under the impression that it was normal for a Doduo's two heads to have different brains or something like that.

"Do duo!" cawed Doduo, both heads nodding simultaneously. Willa gave a faint smile and held out an Ultra Ball. Both heads moved in at once, and the beaks touched the button simultaneously. Once the ball stopped shaking, Willa nodded and moved on to the counter, intent on paying for her new Doduo.

It had been a month or so since Alice had adopted Dora, her Skitty, but the Pokemon had settled in fantastically with the rest of them and the idol had made a final decision after deliberating on if she would adopt another and who to choose. She'd had her eyes on one of the two Doduo in the new Adoption Centre for a while now, and knew that another trainer had been contemplating the other. It was best to swoop in before someone else revealed desire or whimsy for the species and she lost out. While it wouldn't be too crushing as long as the Pokemon found good homes, Alice still wanted one of the twin-headed birdies.

After greeting the new staff at the desk, she went in, walking past the enclosures until she came across the one housing the Doduo. Alice wondered how much of a nightmare it was day to day. They weren't known for being a particularly peaceful species, their heads often arguing. With four heads in there together, did they often bite and snap and squawk amongst themselves? That didn't seem to be a problem anymore, though, because there was only one Doduo left. Alice really was lucky for getting there when she had.

“Doduo~?” Alice called over in a sweet sing-song voice, waving her hand a little in front of the fence. She didn't dare put her fingers too close, lest one of the Pokemon decide that they looked appetising. “My name's Alice! I want to adopt you, if you'd be happy to come home with me? I have lots of friends for you to make and space to run and fly around in! I promise it'll be lots of fun and we can go on lots of cool and scary adventures!”

Scary was about right given how the majority of them turned. The fact they hadn't been arrested for murder or had their career tanked from some of what she had experienced was a miracle by now. But hey, with the spotlight still favouring the Fizzytopian defender, she easily had the $200 and Poke Ball required for the adoption process. That was, assuming the Doduo took to her, because if they didn't, well... That'd be sad and embarrassing, wouldn't it?

Shadow entered the building, listening to hopeful and occasionally mournful cries. His heart went out to all of the Pokemon here. There was nothing that saddened him more than the thought of unwanted Pokemon. He wanted to gather them all up in his arms, give every single one a place to be loved.
Problem is he was only one man. He couldn't possibly give ALL of the Pokemon here the love they rightly deserved. As his eyes locked on a drowsing Shuppet he knew he could save at least one from potentially years waiting for someone, and it was on his list of desired Pokemon anyway.
He held out his arms in welcome. "Come here you!"
The Shuppet approached. Thankfully they were less moody than the Evolved form. "Shup?"
Shadow tried to hold it before getting flustered. "Ghost type, right. Anyway, I'm taking you home! I got the cash and a Pokeball! I mean, it's nothing special, I am a new Trainer... But rest assured I'll take care of you!"
The Shuppet seemed delighted with him. What with having a temperamental and rarely liked Evolved form, he'd expected to be stuck there forever, although admittedly he'd been well fed thanks to plenty of dark emotions to devour. He briefly wondered if things would suddenly get worse at the shelter but shrugged it off.
Shadow placed the Shuppet in one of his five Pokeballs, and put the requisite $200 Pokedollars on the counter. Gazing at his new companion's Pokeball, he vowed to give his new friend the love he rightly deserved. Pausing a moment, he thought about nicknames and Abilities and such.
"I think I'll call you... Bane."
The Pokeball wiggled ever so slightly in response. Shadow guessed the Shuppet would have... Frisk? Yes, that was it. Shadow paused a moment, then nodded.

It had been a long time since Alice Hope had visited the Adoption Centre, but here she was again, for her first visit of the new year. It was a cold day, and the gusts of wind only made things worse, but at least Fizz City's ground wasn't ruined with sheets of ice or overwhelmed by snow. Instead, the citizens were treated to splatterings of rain, which wasn't ideal given who she was coming to get, but such was life.

She stepped over the threshold to the building, greeting the staff cheerily as she pulled her hood back from her still-damp blonde hair. Nothing special was done with it. In fact, the idol hadn't even bothered to put make up on for the trip. As a result, her Aipom, Amina, looked fancier than she did, wearing a bright raincoat with a special glove and sleeve for her oversized tail. The monkey dipped her head back, too, making a show of shaking droplets from tail and fur. Alice laughed and flinched, apologising for the mess made before making her intentions known.

There had been a male Larvesta in the Centre for a while now, and its presence had been weighing on the trainer's mind for almost as long a period. She had come along way since her adventures in Fizzytopia began, and with the help of Pokemon like Blaze and Hela, Alice's fear of fire wasn't quite as awful as it was. It wasn't fully eliminated, though, and on top of that, their family was enormous. Though, thinking of acquaintances like Gary and Keith made her feel better about the monster den of a base she had back at the Cloud Garden, and really, they managed just fine. The bug was probably worse off here than if they at least tried to make things work, and so Alice had set herself a condition: if she could think of a perfect name for the Larvesta, they would come to collect it, and here they were.

The assistant smiled politely as Alice explained herself, setting two hundred pokedollars on the desk and filling out paperwork as she did so. It was always reassuring to know that the idol put such thought and consideration into the Pokemon she adopted given the great numbers she had taken away since its grand reopening just under three years ago. She was practically a meme in the place, in fact, though not out of maliciousness. Soon enough, one of the staff members was leading her through to the Larvesta's enclosure, Amina's big eyes somehow managing to sparkle with greater excitement at the sight of the fluffy fire bug; her trainer's stomach knotting a bit from adrenaline. Acquiring a new Pokemon was no small thing, after all, even after all this time.

“Hi, Larvesta...” Alice began greeting sweetly as the door to the place was unlocked. It was the beginning of his new life, but did he truly know what that could mean? Would he be okay with it? “I hope you don't mind that me and Amina were hoping to take you home. We've got a big space in the Cloud Garden, and a lot of adventures with your new name on it. Everyone else seems to love it, so...”

Goodness, she was out of shape at this for sure. The past year hadn't been so good, but she was becoming her best self again, a little bit at a time.

We'll do our best together, won't we, Veritas?
