His breathing was heavy, he was sweating buckets. More importantly, the pain his left leg wouldn't go away.

Why the hell are they still giving chase?!"

It made no sense. Althea had given up pursuit - why wouldn't the rest of them? And why were they using that much force to bring him in, anyway? If he hadn't dodged when he had, that goddamn Houndour would've burned a lot more than just a limb...

He had no choice; he had to find help. On the verge of passing out, Ray burst into the Pokémon Center and stumbled his way over to the counter.

"Welcome to the Pok... oh my!"

He knew Pokemon Center staff only treated Pokemon, but he was nowhere near any other medical facility. Besides, checking into a hospital was the easiest way to get identified and caught. Struggling to even speak, he locked eyes with the nurse, and mustered to say only a couple of words.

"Please... help..."

------

When he came to, he was lying down. The room was foreign to him, but not the person standing over him, looking relieved - the Joy from earlier.

"Oh good, you came to... I was starting to fear the worst."

He weakly lifted his head off the bed and looked at his leg: a Chansey was dutifully tending to the bandages, smiling all the while.

"I... assume you ran into trouble. Don't worry, nobody came looking for you," the nurse continued, as if sensing his concern. "But you know you really should seek proper medical help afterwards - we don't routinely treat Trainers, as I'm sure you know..."

Ray's head fell back on the pillow. More so than in pain, which had eased considerably thanks to Chansey's expert ministrations, he felt exhausted. But even at that moment, his mind raced, thinking up the next course of action.

And his eyes fell on the nurse's helper once more, as he realized he could never risk such a compromising situation again.

I can't resort to public institutions like this while I'm on the run. I need... my own healer.

His lips curled into a faint smile before he drifted off to sleep again. When he woke back up... it was time to get to work.

~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Adopting Chansey and placing her into Premier Ball 1/1.

Payment = $200

Rise, Link… The time has come for you to awaken… You are fated to have a hand in a great destiny, and it will soon find you…

The time has come for you to awaken…

Now, open your eyes.

Link blinked awake from a fitful slumber, lifting his head immediately from the cushion upon hearing a strange voice infiltrate his dreams. Lately, it seemed his head had been haunted by nightmares each eve, ominous visions of darkness and dread encroaching. Despite the growing sense of foreboding though, something about the mysterious message felt oddly... familiar. As if a forgotten friend calling out to him.

Like a trance, he raised up on all fours and wandered to the entrance of the Base, sniffing the salty air as the moon shone down on calm ocean waves. A single pink petal drifted down from the sky, landing briefly on his snout before being blown away again by the sea breeze. Like a lost child; starkly out of place amidst the coastal climate, yet appearing to know its destination as its mystical movements beckoned to the befuddled mutt. Casting one last glance back down the tunnel towards where his trainer and teammates were still fast asleep, Link stepped cautiously out from the cavern onto sand and took off sprinting solo down the beach, chasing after the cherry blossom guide as it weaved gleefully back and forth on the wind.

Unbeknownst to him, two sets of eyes were watching the wolf-like pup make his way along the coast as well. One stood high above the cliff's rockface, shimmering cobalt coat illuminated by majestic lunar light, almost glowing gold. The other was bathed in shade, curiously following the canine out of the cave as she tagged along closely like a shadow.

"Now what's that dumb dog up to at this hour..."

Midna murmured to herself as she tailed the Poochyena just past his periphery. Meanwhile, Sheik simply observed the pair travel along the shore until they were out of sight, before vanishing as well with a graceful leap behind the boulders...

-

"Your master will come for you. Until then, you shall rest safely here."

100 years had passed since she heard those words spoken to her, before being laid to sleep deep within the woods. How long she had waited for the "chosen one" to find her again... She had nearly given up hope, when she perceived a faint presence step forth into the forest. Padded paws drew steadily nearer the pedestal as if spellbound, and she brightened softly to their approach. Warm fangs clamped firmly onto her hilt, yet try as the holder might, he did not possess the strength to pull her fully from the seal. Perhaps, he was not the one after all... Or he simply wasn't ready yet.

Time... We need more time.

But the Calamity was coming.

And not a moment more could be spared.

A burst of brightness shot forth, engulfing both weapon and wielder in such brilliance it nearly blinded the nearby observer, who hissed and retreated into the gloom. When the intense glare faded and she recovered enough to glimpse back, she gaped to witness a Honedge hovering before a magnificent beast, as the former addressed the other formally in a melodic, yet robotic tone:

"I ask of you... Are you my master?"

-

It had been nearly a whole day since Link went missing, and Jess was worried out of her wits after waking up this morning to discover the dog had abruptly disappeared in the middle of the night. She had spent hours searching high and low for signs of the pooch, and as evening started to fall she finally circled around with her Togekiss and touched down back at Base, hoping he had managed to make a return in the meantime. Just as she arrived at the hollow, she spotted a dark shape in the distance, and her heart leapt as she rushed excitedly to meet what she assumed must be her companion coincidentally coming back as well. She stopped short however upon realizing the form was much larger than anticipated, and adjoined by what appeared to be a sword suspended in mid-air, who floated forward to introduce herself.

"Greetings, young one. According to your social customs, I should provide you with my personal designation. Fi is the name I was given. I shall be in your care from now on."

She bowed deeply, and Jess awkwardly reciprocated the gesture.

"Er, pleased to meet you..." She glanced towards the huge hound beside, towering proud beneath the twilight. "Link, is that you?"

The mongrel nodded mutely, and Jess sighed in relief as she petted his now long midnight mane.

"You've grown up, huh? I hardly recognized you at first."

...It might've been her imagination, but his expression seemed even more inscrutable than before, staring seriously straight at her without saying anything. No, past her- as Jess realized Sheik was standing behind, quietly surveying the scene. The Houndour studied the Mightyena's mature build in silence for a moment, before turning and stalking off into the sunset, still as aloof as ever. Yet, there seemed to be a sadness surrounding both as Link watched the other leave with an almost inaudible whine, tail drooping slightly despite the lack of emotional change on his face. Meanwhile, Fi remained entirely unfazed by the encounter, gazing expectantly at the confused human girl as she rubbed the back of her neck.

"Well... I guess let's go inside and have you meet everyone then, shall we?"

As they proceeded within, Midna popped out once more in the backdrop as dusk descended, grin deepening to a sinister sneer as she snickered to herself at this development.

"Not exactly what I had in mind, but... I guess this'll do."

They had seen this before, back when the strange girl had come in and left with a Bronzor. And now, it came to be that Terri Alph was once again in the Adoption Center, in a seemingly endless staring contest with one of the Pokémon there. This time, it was a tiny insect, allowed by the staff to roam free as she tended to stick around on her own in any case. Nobody was quite sure exactly when she'd arrived, either. Never showing much interest in prospective adopters, until today- she seemed to sense something as soon as Terri walked in through the door, and flew right over to her. And Terri, far from being surprised by this, simply fixed the diminutive creature with a curious look, and took out her Pokédex.

"Cutiefly, the Bee Fly Pokémon. A Bug and Fairy-type," droned the device. "Cutiefly can sense Aura, allowing it to identify flowers that are about to bloom, or what an opponent's next move will be."

After the silent staredown that lasted for far longer than any of the staff thought was strictly necessary, Terri spoke up. "So, what do you sense in me?" she asked.

"A remarkable soul," the Cutiefly responded. "Unusual, yet kindhearted. Like nothing I've ever sensed before. It intrigues me," she buzzed. "Far moreso than anyone else who's come through these doors since I arrived. For one thing... you can understand what I'm saying perfectly, can you not?"

Terri smiled her mysterious smile. "I can," she confirmed. "You're very perceptive. Why would anyone drop you off here?"

"Oh, they didn't," Cutiefly buzzed. "I made the conscious choice to come in with other Pokémon. A place like this, abuzz with the comings and goings of people... I figured it would be a good way to find that which I seek."

Terri tilted her head. "Which is?" she asked.

"A Trainer, of course," replied Cutiefly. "One who could help me realize my full potential. And it seems as though this was the correct choice, as now I have found you. If you'll have me?"

Terri's smile widened. "I'd be delighted to," she replied, holding up a Poké Ball. "Would you accept Find as your name?"

Cutiefly buzzed thoughtfully. "An unusual name," she remarked. "And yet... I like it. Very well, then- Find I shall be from here on in," she decided. Saying no more, Terri gently tapped the Cutiefly with the Poké Ball in her hand, which opened up and sucked in the Bee Fly Pokémon. It barely wobbled in her hand before falling still- Find had put up no struggle whatsoever. Pleased with this, Terri approached the counter to pay the bewildered staff for her newest Pokémon.

Adopting the female Cutiefly, naming her Find, putting her in Poké Ball #4 of 10, and declaring her Ability to be Shield Dust.

Payment: 200
