Toxidize (90 BP, 90 Accuracy, 10 BP, Poison, Physical.
The user whirls its body round and slams the target with rust and dust. This attack is super-effective on Steel type pokemon.

Design Commentary: My thought process here was simple. "What's the most iconic symbol of an amusement park, after the rollercoaster?" The observation wheel. Taking into account Phantom Isle's history, it would make sense that like with many amusement parks there would be spare parts laying around, and the idea of a ferris wheel pokemon intrigued me, but finding a suitable reason for one being there wasn't easy, neither was the potential design, so I took to inspiration from real life, and that's when I remembered probably the most infamous abandoned amusement park.

Pripyat, in the Chernobyl exclusion zone.

Mokwurk and Ferustwiel's shell design is very closely based off the famous ferris wheel there, with its yellow frilled pods, and both are based heavily off them. Mokwurk is based off the drive mechanisms of many rides, with damaged and rusted cogwheels protecting its squishy body, and is a little bt heavier than regular Pineco due to the extra metal, with it sticking to the dark to avoid falling prey to its evolved form. Ferustwiel meanwhile is closer to the wheel itself, having evolved to expand its cannon arms length to ensure the toxic rusty wheel won't affect it. Due to this, it's notably taller than regular Forretress, though the inner core remains around the same dimensions. In order to maintain full inertial power, it developed itself to become vertically inclined, and its cannons evolved to not only prevent its wheel from slipping off, but also to build on the gears it once held and to allow it to spray its rust around it. The poison subtyping refers, unsurprisingly, to Chernobyl and its irradiated landscape, and Ferustwiel's inner core is thematically leaden to reduce radiation.

With regards to abilities, Mokwurk's form is somewhat fragile, with those cracked and rusted gears weighing it down, hence the weak armour and stallish nature due to the heavy metal, whereas Ferustwiel utilises, its metal rusting properties and naturally long reach to strike at foes, hence Corrosion and Long Reach. I can imagine that Ferustwiel keeps itself afloat, hovering just above ground level in levitation so as to stand out above the crests of many of the park's buildings, as any ferris wheel would.

In terms of stats and movepools, they follow their Johtonian predecessors, but trade physical bulk for more offensive power. While Mokwurk bears a somewhat shallow movepool, it gets exclusive access to Gear Grind, while Ferustwiel instead learns Shift Gear, to demonstrate its improved mobility. Toxidize is effectively Freeze-Dry in that it makes an otherwise ineffective move SE, and fits with the whole metal corrosion aspect, while the plethora of fire moves are in memetic reference to how Chernobyl ended up as it was, through meltdown. Their egg moves remain similar, though they lose on Revenge and Counter in exchange for mobility enhancements. Shell Smash is fitting as neither of them seem to be built around having strong and stable defences.
