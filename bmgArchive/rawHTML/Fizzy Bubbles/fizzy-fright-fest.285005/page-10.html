The sounds of music resonating from theme park attractions, winning bells dinging, the whoosh of a rollercoaster clacking down a track, and the screams of thrill seekers echoed across the island throughout the duration of the evening and deep into the night. Shortly after the stroke of midnight as the majority of the visitors had gotten their fill of fun and excitement, people began to gather around the tent that the film crew had set up as their base of operations.

The film crew was hard at work compiling all of the footage they had captured throughout the evening. Images of Sableye watching and giggling at failed attempts of various guests, a flock of Drifloon tethered to some sign posts, a Purrloin stealthily slinking between visitors’ feet looking for something to pilfer, the haunted faces of Gastly camouflaged in the dark haze, and the ominous silhouette of an Absol watching from the distance atop a Ferris wheel car reflected off of various television monitors as they reviewed the film. “Looks like we’ve got some great material here… There’s still hours more of footage to sift through, but no sign of any of these ‘regional forms’ you keep hyping up, Professor…” Dawn Hattenbrough noted skeptically.

Professor Palm seemed unfazed as his focus remained fixated on the task at hand and addressed the trainers who had gathered. “Thank you all for your help- we’ve generated a ton of excitement here tonight and it’s really drawn out a lot of the natural wildlife! There’s still a lot of work to be done to go through all of our observations, but I suppose now is as good a time as ever to reward you for all of your hard work!” The disheveled professor directed everyone’s attention over to a makeshift counter that had been set up and stacked with an array of prizes. “I hope you all enjoyed yourselves tonight, so feel free to cash in your Carnival Coupons for some of these prizes! Additionally, for those of you who have submit your observations in the search for a Fizzytopian Regional Variant, I’ve got an extra 5 Carnival Coupons as thanks for all of your hard work!”

“These little party favors are nice and all, but where’s my big scoop?!” Dawn interjected, breaking up the revelry. Professor Palm adjusted the frame of his glasses and stared down at the clipboard in his hands. Attached to it were countless sheets of observations gathered by the trainers who had participated. “Well, we’ve gathered tons of data here tonight, and while not all of them can be proven if they exist or not, the findings here are invaluable to our research. It’s possible that any special lifeforms were just waiting for the right conditions to present themselves to—” Dawn quickly cut him off with a dismissive wave of her hand. “Oh spare me the idealism, Palm- if you think the viewers will accept some secondhand reports as tangible evidence then you’re a bigger hack than I—”

Dawn Hattenbrough is interrupted as something pulls on her pant leg. She looked down and jumped in shock as a strange looking face stared up at her, inciting a shriek. A grin crept onto Professor Palm’s face as he flipped through the sheets on his clipboard. “Like I was saying, maybe this creature felt like you could use a little prize to go home with. Now let’s see… ah, here’s the data on this species.” He landed on the page that had the observation report for the creature before them and held it up for all to see.


“This little creature looks to be a Fizzytopian Trubbish, also known as a Trubbfish. First observed by the trainer Emp! Thank you for your contribution; this has been invaluable to my research!” Dawn scrunched her nose and gingerly picked it up by the tip of the bag and handed it to Emp. “Then you take it! That dead fish creeps me out! And you guys, why aren’t you getting this on film yet?” she snipped at the cameramen.

Congratulations to empoleon dynamite! It was an incredibly close race that came down to the wire. Thank you all for voting, and an even bigger thanks to all of you who submitted your wonderful artwork! For coming in first place, empoleon dynamite is awarded a lv.1 female Trubbfish!

With the area’s wildlife and the regional variant properly captured on camera and the prizes given out, it was time for the festivities to come to a close. “Well, I’d say that was another successful expedition- thank you all so much for coming and helping me out! I can’t stress enough how critical it is to progress my research. Until next time, I hope that I can work with you all again in the future!” Professor Palm made some final observations of Trubbfish and wrote them down in his notes and began to pack up his equipment. “Palm wait,” Dawn called out as she approached him. There was a look of remorse on her face. “I owe you an apology. Your research was spot on. If you’d allow me, I’d like to interview you for a segment on the program to highlight your research further. You’ll be the top researcher in your field in no time!” Professor turned around with a warm smile on his face. “Sounds fine by me, but remember, we’re all first!”

It wasn't long until the Pokémon Trainers of Sludge Wave Coast met back up, each of them clutching a number of Carnival Coupons. "Alright, how'd everyone do?" Keith asked.

"Nuzzle put on a wonderful performance that the crowd loved," stated Terri, brandishing her five Carnival Coupons.

"Dodrio... got the Rollerquaza moving well enough," Willa murmured. Keith put an arm around his wife at this point as she showed her handful of Carnival Coupons.

"Yeah, I, uh, kinda heard the screaming," Keith said. "You OK?"

Willa nodded. "I am fine," she confirmed, with a small yet genuinely grateful smile in response to Keith's concern.

"Good," Keith nodded back. "Meanwhile, Artemis didn't miss a single target at the shooting gallery," he added, holding up another bunch of Carnival Coupons.

"Salemence had more than enough strength for the hammer game," said Maribel. "Just... our approach wasn't quite right," she added sheepishly, holding up fewer Carnival Coupons than the others had held up so far.

"Eh, Wes wasn't as graceful as he could've been, but he still did a damn fine job in the circus tent," Gavin reported, holding up his share of the Carnival Coupons.

"And Homer, of course, navigated us flawlessly through the Hall of Mirrors," said Eddie. "On an entirely related note, when we're headed back to Sludge Wave Coast, I will need to stop at Donphan Donuts for a minute."

Once the good-natured chuckling at this statement died down, Keith spoke up. "Alright, so, seems like we all rocked this place," he grinned. "Now let's see what we can see!"

And so they did. In the process, Keith was slightly disappointed to learn that his findings about the Fizzytopian Form of Sunkern had not been the star of the show, but said disappointment was quite short-lived as he saw what was being called Trubbfish- the Fizzytopian Form of Trubbish. Did it hold a candle to Normal Form Trubbish? In his opinion, no. Was it still adorable in a strange way? Hell, yes.

Following this was the prize counter. With the Carnival Coupons pooled together, earned by Sludge Wave Coast's Trainers, including the extra seven Keith was given for his efforts on researching a potential new regional form, they had a good amount to work with here. The decisions were tough ones, but eventually everyone was able to agree on the best uses for them.
