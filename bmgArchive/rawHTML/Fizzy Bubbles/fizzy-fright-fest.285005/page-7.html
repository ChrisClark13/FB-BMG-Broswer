Fizzy Fright Fest​

● CARNIVAL RULES:
- You may only participate in one activity at a time and you may only partake in an activity once each.
- As you participate in each activity, it is encouraged that you enjoy it with the company of your Pokemon. A maximum of 1 Pokemon may assist you with each activity and they may use up to a maximum of 3 attacks.
- Based on how much you and your Pokemon are able to generate excitement at each activity, you will be scored and given Carnival Coupons to cash in for prizes at the end of the event!
- You have until the end of the art contest to participate in all 6 attractions!
- List of Attractions:
- Sideshow Alley: A freak show where the star attraction is you! Can your performance attract and appeal to the highest number of passersby?​

Carnival Coupons: +4, +5, +5, +4, +5

Zephyr swooped and dove, twisting and turning as she navigated the obstacle course with ease. She ended with a twirl, zipping past the goal before flaring her wings and circling down in a lazy spiral. Wildfire and Melissa clapped for her, while Ore darted over to the ticket booth currently printing out their coupons.

"Nice job, Zephyr," Melissa said, raising her arm so Zephyr could land on it. "Amazing flying." She scritched Zephyr's head, the Fletchling chirping her agreement.

"Cyndaquil cynda," Wildfire suggested, which had Zephyr straightening up and whistling excitedly.

"Sounds like a plan." Melissa accepted the Carnival Coupons from her Sableye and then led the way out of the Circus Tent. "Alright, last one is...Sideshow Alley, I believe." The most difficult one, in her opinion, which was why she'd left it for last.

They reached a relatively empty spot along the path with enough space for a routine and then paused as Melissa wracked her head for ideas. She'd overheard that some good moves for this activity included some kind of method of attracting attention, like Follow Me or Sweet Scent, and then moves of escalating intensity. So...maybe ending with a Z crystal move? Maybe Bloom Doom, if someone on her team had Sweet Scent? She pulled out her Pokédex to check. Nope, apparently none of her Pokémon had Sweet Scent. Huh.

Welp. Can't be helped. Might as well go for the fire-themed one then.

She shrugged the shoulder Wildfire had been using as a perch. "Well, guess you're up, buddy."

Wildfire leaped to the ground, tilting his head to the side as he looked up at her. "Quil?"

"Start off with several Flame Bursts aimed up--like fireworks?--to attract attention. Then Fire Blast and round it off with Eruption."

Wildfire glanced dubiously at the ground and then at their surroundings. "Quil quil?"

Melissa shrugged and started backing up. "We'll find out in a sec, right?"

Wildfire sighed and turned to trot to the center of their makeshift stage. She couldn't say he hadn't tried to warn her.

Clown Jr., the Abandonment Pokemon​


Mime Jr. were one of may Pokemon frequently used in the children's battling arenas on the Isle before the park was shut down, but with the closure being nothing shy of a rush due to the accident causing increasingly bad PR for the park by the day, may of thes Pokemon were left behind. After some time, the Mime Jr. on the newfound Phantom Isle turned away from their happy lifestyle to one of trickery, focusing their Psychic powers to hide their presense so that the more malicious threats on the Isle would have a difficult time finding them, all the while allowing the newly christened Clown Jr. to sustain itself on the scraps of joy it once had by playing a prank or two on the occasional visitors. The hat atop Mime Jr.'s head has drooped down, slinking along the ground, Psychic powers allowing the Clown Jr. to magnetize the orb at the end to collect any stray cans of food or food wrappers as it walks around, as well as eliminate any footprints it may otherwise leave by stirring up dust.

Type: Psychic

Ability 1: Prankster
Ability 2: Magnet Pull
Hidden Ability: Infiltrator

Evolution: Mr. Clown (level up while holding Metal Coat)

Mr. Clown, the Mascot Pokemon​


Some say the Park that has now become Phantom Isle never had a mascot. And perhaps that was once true, but brave adventurers that have entered the gates of Phantom Isle after the shutdown of the park know better. As Clown Jr.'s magnetized orb slowly recovers enough pieces of metal from the collapsing park, the small Pokemon evolves into a Pokemon much more suited to survival and hunting within the Park, Mr. Clown. It gains a pristine shell with a white metallic hue, the otherwise red pieces of Mr. Mime having become a shimmering mirror, distorting the world around it to any would-be viewers. The same reflective coating shines over Mr. Clown's hair as well as hands and feet, its hands having developed into sharp claws with pointed knuckles to allow it to rip into any ride or wall it so chooses for a quick meal, or to horrify those that may find it lurking within its favored lairs. Its feet have developed similarly, no longer boots that would make loud noise when traipsing through an area but instead opposable claws themselves, allowing for climbing up the steel beams that make up the abandoned rides or even scaling the walls and ceilings within the now run down buildings to get a surprising drop on its would-be victims. A bright green light emanates from within its eyes and mouth, the closest adaptation Mr. Clown could achieve to proper night vision, and the only warning one might get of its presence.

Type: Psychic/Steel

Ability 1: Tinted Lense
Ability 2: Steely Spirit
Hidden Ability: Infiltrator

Signature Move (upon evolving to Mr. Clown):
Malicious Rend (Steel, 60 BP): The user makes a sudden swipe at the foe with sharpened claws. Because of the ferosity and quick speed of this attack, it will break through and destroy and Light Screen, Reflect, Aurora Veil, or Safeguard currently affecting the target.
