    Name: Silver or Sil (nickname)
    Gender: Girl
    Age: Young adult (between 17-21 years old)
    Appearance (cold climate clothes):

    Background (will be expanded on as I develop the character):
    A young adult from Sinnoh, Sil caught her partner Pokémon, an Alolan Sandshrew (whom she named Hailstone), as a child while staying with some relatives in the more northern part of Sinnoh (a small group of the Pokémon had accidentally been introduced to the area). Over the years, she slowly leveled him up to level five, though she never took a serious interest in battles, or the wider Pokémon world, until she became a bit older. Now she and Hailstone have decided to head to Fizzytopia to learn more about Pokémon, as well as train Hailstone further, perhaps teaching him powerful and difficult moves such as Aqua Tail or Triple Axel, for example.
    Name: Selene
    Gender: Girl
    Age: mid to late 20s
    Appearance:

    Background:
    A dual Galar-Alola citizen, Selene was one of the scientists that worked on the Aether Foundation's Beast Killer Project. She became obsessed with the Pokémon the lab had developed, seeing them as the pinnacle of modern technology and science. When the project was cancelled, she was distraught. Unable to steal the Type: Null without arousing suspicion, she downloaded several digitized research notes and stole her lab notebook instead with the help of her Pokémon, Prism, and gave copies to a friend working at Marco Cosmos in the hopes that she could continue the project there. However, Marco Cosmos's more profit orientated structure turned her away from the company, and she fled to Fizzytopia. Now she journeys the far off region to research Pokémon types so that she can create a Type: Null on her own.

    Note: All entries will be written by me
    Entries

    An extremely rare variant of Bulbasaur first discovered in the Fizzytopia region. The light from the jack-o-lantern flickers as if it were candlelight, but no candle lies within the gourd's hollowed out cavity. Some say that the light is actually the soul of a lost child.

    It is theorized that Pumpkasaur hibernate all year long underground until fall arrives, which is why they are so rarely seen. It is said that inside the pumpkin is a trapped spirit, and that the carving on the squash is its face.

    These small Ice types slam into rocks at high speeds to break them apart. They then lick the shards in order to absorb the metals needed to replenish the steel inside their bodies.

    While their steely backs deflect the sharp claws of Sneasel, their soft underbellies offer no such protection. They burrow in snowdrifts to avoid being flipped over and clawed.

    Despite its reputation as the weakest and most pathetic Pokémon, the tail muscles of wild Magikarp are usually highly developed. Magikarp capable of leaping over 5x their height out of the water have been found; their jumping ability can serve as both a defensive and offensive technique.

    The fin atop their head looks like a crown. In a Galarian fairy tale, a fraudster managed to convince a king to wear the crown-like fin in exchange for the real crown, tricking the king into believing the fin was the golden scales of a legendry Dragon Pokémon.

    The scales of their zigzag pattern lack the specific cells required to change color. Kecleon carefully conceal their stomach whenever they are attempting to remain camouflaged, sometimes wrapping their tail around their body if needed.

    Kecleon sometimes change their color depending on their mood. Which colors correspond to which emotion varies between groups of Kecleon; wild Kecleon living in Hoenn's Route 118 have different colors for joy, for example, compared to Kecleon from the Nature Preserve in Unova. Kecleon that live with humans tend to follow human conventions for color-emotion associations, learning these from human media.

    According to legend, Woobat's peculiarly shaped nose and affectionate habits are what led to the iconic becoming the ideographic symbol for a heart.

    Woobat are very affectionate Pokémon, often seen grooming, nuzzling, and touching each other's noses when resting in caves. This behavior, along with their soft fluffy fur, have made them popular pets with people, especially among the lonely.

    In the wild, Red-Striped Basculin are very hostile towards their Blue-Striped cousins. However, Red- and Blue-Striped Basculin raised in captivity without influence from wild specimens do not exhibit such behavior, suggesting that it is learned and not as a result of a fixed action pattern.

    Basculin are highly aggressive and will violently mob almost all other aquatic Pokémon in their environment, oftentimes to secure food sources. While these fish Pokémon are nutrient rich, their aggression makes them risky meals for predatory Pokémon.

    Decades long research on Zorua's illusion-creating abilities led to the creation of holographic technology common throughout the Pokémon world today. Further studies are being conducted to see if the Tricky Fox Pokémon can create illusions using light outside the visible spectrum.

    Due to their ability to take the form's of other creatures and create illusions, Zorua and its evolution Zoroark are commonly used in assassinations, espionage, and spy missions, though infrared technology and DNA testing have limited their usefulness in modern-day endeavors.

    Despite the thinness of the membrane surrounding its body, it is highly effective at protecting the Solosis from its outside environment. Research has shown that it functions similarly to a cell membrane, just on a larger scale, and that psychic impulses control whether or not certain particles, such as atmospheric oxygen, can pass through.

    The coiled tissue structure connected to its head functions as a psychic radar from which high-energy psychic waves can be emitted. It uses this to find other Solosis, Duosion, or Reuniclus as well as scramble the minds of Psychic Pokémon that compete with it in its natural habitat.

    According to a Kalosian legend, Binacle were born from the severed hands of sailors that were cut off as a punishment for theft and then thrown into the sea. It is believed that some of these Binacle retained their previous owner's kleptomania, hence why Binacle's hidden ability is Pickpocket.

    Highly adept at manipulating objects with their hand-like heads, these Pokémon sift through sand near their rock for food. As Binacle near their evolution, they release pheromones to attract other Binacle pairs that are needed to form a Barbaracle.

    These Pokémon have the temperament of a toddler. If anything upsets them, they'll spew weak toxins and sparks everywhere around them until they've calmed down, usually by the sound by music.

    If they hear a steady beat, they'll bob their heads to the rhythm. Due to their weak and underdeveloped muscles and heavy head, this typically results in them falling flat on their face.

    Leukocyte was first caught by Aspen's close friend and friend from college, Selene, at Loch Bygone after returning home from a successful raid at the local Power Spot. Taking advantage of the fog summoned by the Ghost types in the area, the albino Woobat was able to turn her normally harmful coloration into an advantage, camouflaging almost perfectly with the pale white mist. She even was taught Shadow Ball by the resident ghosts who took pity, learning by watching them manipulate the ethereal energy emitting from the Power Spot.
