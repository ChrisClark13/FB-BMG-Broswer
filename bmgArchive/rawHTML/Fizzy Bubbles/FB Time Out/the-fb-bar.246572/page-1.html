Keith entered the bar, glancing around. It was built quite like the one in Yupien City, though at the moment, business looked pretty slow.

...Also not unlike the one in Yupien City, if Keith was being honest with himself.

"Well, it looks like dis place ain't quite done bein' a ghost town," quipped the Meowth on Keith's shoulder.

"I say give it time," Keith shrugged. "If the city can improve the way it's done, it's surely only a matter of time. C'mon out, everyone!" he added, throwing an assortment of Poké Balls in the air. From the Christmas Ball emerged a Cryogonal; from the Premier Ball emerged a Druddigon; from the Great Ball there appeared a Seismitoad. From the Violet Cyber Ball appeared a Ditto. And from the regular Poké Ball there appeared an adorable Ivysaur. Keith led this odd group over to the counter so they could all get something to drink.

Keith, Meowth, Winter, Pomona, Aletri, Mint, Celeste:
Keith sipped the chocolate milk the bartender had served him- booze had never been a particular favorite of Keith's, whose presence at bars was usually more about the socializing than the beverages- as another Trainer entered along with her Chatot and her... sentient floating Pokédex? That was a new one on Keith.

"Hey," Keith greeted the newcomer. "Haven't seen you around before, you a new Trainer? I'm Keith. Keith Masters." As he spoke, Winter the Cryogonal slowly floated behind Keith, shy in the presence of an unfamiliar face.

In stark contrast, Pomona seemed thrilled at the prospect of making a new friend. "Saur! Ivy~" she said sweetly as she approached Mint. Aletri followed his mother over to Mint as well, giving a friendly wave as he did so.


Jack, Sprocket:
"WHOA WHAT THE-" Jack exclaimed, jiggling in stunned surprise as the Rotom Dex unexpectedly snapped his picture. "Wh- whoa," he added, upon getting his first proper look at Sprocket. "I've seen Rotom possess fridges, toaster ovens, fans, lawnmowers, and washing machines before, but this is a new one on me. You possessing a Pokédex of some kind there? Oh, yeah- name's Jack," he added.

Mint, Celeste, Keith, Winter, Meowth, Pomona, Aletri:

The bartender served Mint her drink just as the Trainer from before came over to her, some of his Pokémon in tow.

Mint couldn't help but chuckle a little as she saw the Trainer's Cryogonal hide behind him-Cryogonal had always struck her as a more stoic type of Pokémon more likely to cause others a little discomfort, rather than the other way around, after all.

"Yeah, I'm pretty new in town." Mint said. "I'm Mint Earhart, and-"

Jack, Sprocket:

"You've got that right!" Sprocket replied to Jack's asking about his body. "I've got all the featurezzzz and then some! I can take picturezzzz, show the map and flag destinationzzzz, and, well, help Mint stay organizzzzed!" the Rotom Dex cheerfully explained. Just then, Mint spoke up behind him.

"Sprocket, have you been taking pictures without permission again!?" she asked, and Sprocket floated back, a guilty look on his face. "Come on, Sprocket, you know better than that..." Jack chuckled at Mint's amazement, and nodded as she mentioned not knowing that Ditto were quite as capable with speech in a transformed state. "It depends on the Ditto, honestly," Jack responded. "Some can be more thorough with their transformations. Something to do with transforming the vocal chords or something like that- I don't really know the specifics," he shrugged. "All I know is, I turn into a human, I speak like one."

"Yeah, Jack's not your average Ditto," Keith chimed in. After which the subject turned to Sprocket, and Mint explained that a Rotom Dex was not built for battle. Sprocket still had his species's default typing. "Ahh," Keith nodded, following the whole explanation. "That's kinda cool, honestly. I mean, I've never had any complaints about my own Pokédex- well, there was one, but that turned out to be nothing- but this sort of thing would've been awesome when I was just starting out." As he spoke, he took out his own Pokédex- the classic Kanto Pokédex, model HANDY505, though instead of red, his Pokédex was Koffing purple. It even featured a skull and crossbones on the cover, exactly like what one would see on a Koffing. He then opened it up and scanned Sprocket with it.

"Rotom, the Plasma Pokémon. An Electric and Ghost-type," droned the device. "Rotom is capable of entering electronic devices and power them from within. Recent breakthroughs in Alola have led to a new Pokédex model specifically designed to be powered by Rotom."

"Cool," Keith repeated, before putting his Pokédex away. "So, Mint, where are you from?" he asked. "I'm from Lavaridge Town in the Hoenn region."

While Keith talked, Winter slowly slid out from behind his Trainer's back. "Hello..." the shy Cryogonal quietly greeted Celeste.

Will was no stranger to bartending. Hell, he had done it for years while working his way through school. He wasn't a drinker himself... for obvious reasons, but he certainly didn't hold it against anyone else. He was dressed well, not that that was anything out of the ordinary. Wearing a plaid shirt with a nice grey jacket over it, as well as some matching dress pants. He had to admit, being around bar patrons made him feel a little on edge. The smell of booze made his stomach churn, especially seeing people that looked like his father.

"Horsea!" Abigail called from behind the bar, she was resting in a shallow bowl of water, watching as the patrons passed. She knew her trainer wasn't wild about the environment here, but at least he seemed chipper enough. Will smiled to himself, turning to his Horsea and giving her a pat on the head. He tossed a few Poketreats in the bowl, causing the seahorse to squeal with delight.

A young woman approached the counter, smiling at Will as she approached. Her demeanor was friendly, polite, and she was certainly something to look at. Will smiled, walking over to the sink to wash his Horsea's scent off his hands.

"What can I get for you?" He asked, shaking off his hands over the sink. The woman folded her arms on the counter and gave a smile, Will could smell the booze on her. The smell coming off of her was heavy, with a clear attempt made to mask it in perfume. Will took a deep breath, hoping to clear his sinuses of the extreme smell that had overwhelmed his station.

"Yeeeeeeeeeah, can I get a... motherfuckin'.... uhhhhh," Her speech was slurred, it was clear she had had a few too many. Will couldn't remember serving her prior to this, so he had to wonder if she had been sipping on her own drinks. He was sure there was a bouncer outside, why had he not stopped her on the way in? Her acting certainly wasn't passable, it was obvious from the moment she approached that she was under the influence.

"I would love to, but I think you've had a few too many." He turned away, trying to make it clear to the woman that he had no intention of serving her. He was startled upon hearing some clattering, causing him to turn around. A large Machamp stood next to the woman, demanding it's trainer be served. All four of it's fists were on the counter, surrounded by pieces of broken glass from the tip jar it had shattered. Will felt his breath hinge in his throat, this was certainly not what he signed up for with this job.

"Hor!" Abigail shrieked, jumping out of her bowl and blasting the trainer and her Pokemon with a Blizzard. Upon hearing the commotion, a bouncer came from the door over towards Will.

"Is there a problem here?" He asked, looking at the angry looking Machamp and it's icy trainer. Will nodded, making hand motions to signal that the woman had had a few too many and summoned a Pokemon to attack. The bouncer nodded, escorting the woman away and putting the Machamp in handcuffs.

Will hoped that the rest of the night would be less interesting than this... or he might actually lose his sanity.
