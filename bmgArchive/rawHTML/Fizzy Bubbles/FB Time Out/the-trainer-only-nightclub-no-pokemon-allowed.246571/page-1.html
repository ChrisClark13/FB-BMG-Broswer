Not far from the mysterious Tree Grove is a giant building with bright lights and enticing music, advertising a place for trainers to relax and socialize with other trainers without the stress of having to watch over Pokemon and keep them out of trouble. Perhaps in direct reaction to the ban on trainers, or perhaps out of a potential business opportunity, the proprietors of this establishment have a strict no-Pokemon policy. It is rumored that the owner, a gentle but insistent young man in his late twenties, once owned a club that was Pokemon-friendly before a Swalot not only ate all the food he had, but ate the entire club, as well! Unfortunately, that particular Swalot just*loved the taste of his last business, and proceeded to eat the next three establishments he built until he finally created this one. It is also rumored that the mere mention of Swalot sends the owner into panic-mode, so it is advised that you avoid discussion of that particular Pokemon.

The Trainer-Only Nightclub is the perfect place to dance the night away, and the DJ is open to nearly all music requests. Of course, if dancing is not your thing, why not sit down and enjoy some food and drinks at the appropriately-named Wallflower Pub that's taken residence directly inside the Nightclub? With a perfect view of the action on the dance floor as well as a lovely window to the beautiful forest outside, you can escape from the madness with a nice beer (or soda if underage) and a bite to eat, or simply fuel up before another round of dancing.

A tall figure in a long, well-ironed frock coat looms in the entryway of the Nightclub, surveying the scene. A brand new establishment, recently opened - without a trainer in sight. The figure steps forward with a heavy gait, revealing a woman in her mid-30s with long, brown hair and an intense gaze shielded by full-rimmed glasses. The relative lack of patrons suited her fine. She could enjoy her espresso in peace.

Making her way to the Wallflower Pub, she flags the bartender, who fills her order promptly - no-nonsense. As she sips her espresso, she removes two decks of cards from the pocket of her coat, sifting through the decks to remove the aces, before idly shuffling what remains. She places 28 cards in seven diminishing columns with the bottom card facing up on each, and seven cards face up to the right of the columns, before placing the remaining cards face-down. A rousing game of Seven Devils and a strong surge of caffeine to keep her mind sharp. What could be better?

The Trainer-Only Nightclub. A famed institution in the Fizzytopia region, located in the very heart of Beeyemgee City. Following its grand reopening post-Fizzbylution, the place was starting to regain a bit of popularity. Sometimes this was a good thing. Other times... not so much.

But this was why the Nightclub management took the time and money to hire bouncers. Usually the applicants were easily identifiable by their shirts' complete lack of sleeves, and the bulging muscles said shirts exposed.

So one can imagine the shock and surprise that was felt all around when Gavin Golurkson walked in and applied for the bouncer job. The Orre-native Shiny hunter was not impressive from a physical standpoint. The black t-shirt and dark green jacket he always wore still had their sleeves, and were in no danger of losing them to one muscle flex too many. Indeed, the only thing that kept them from laughing Gavin out of their office was the look in his eyes, a look of toughness. A look that somehow got them thinking, hey, maybe this guy's got what it takes after all.

And so it came to be that that very night, one Gavin Duking Golurkson stood outside the entrance to the Trainer-Only Nightclub. Since he was technically outside and therefore off the premises, he was allowed to keep his Pokémon on his person, though in the spirit of the place, he was still encouraged not to resort to them unless circumstances absolutely warranted it.

For the most part, his first night was a quiet one. Not a lot of riffraff. Several Trainers of varying degreeds of rowdiness, but none too rowdy to warrant denying entrance. One such Trainer was none other than Keith Masters, a friend of Gavin's, and the original Trainer of Gavin's Slowbro.

After that, however, things started to get interesting. A rather large, imposing man was making his way to the entrance, and a number of Poké Balls were clearly visible on his belt.

"Hey, hey, whoa, whoa, buddy, hold up there," Gavin said, barring the man's way. "Those Poké Balls empty or what?"

The man groaned and rolled his eyes. "You telling me these fuckwads still have that stupid 'no-Pokémon-allowed' rule here? Yeesh. You get your place eaten by one Swalot, and suddenly the whole Pokédex is off limits!

"Hey, believe me, I hear you," said Gavin. "But rules is rules. We got a PC right over there you can use, or you can leave your team at the Tree Grove. Not like you don't got options here."

The man glared at Gavin, his eyes finding the pair of Poké Balls on the Shiny hunter's belt. "Ohhh, I see how it is!" he roared. "Nightclub staff get to carry Pokémon, but oh, no, Helix forbid the patrons get to even just bring the Poké Balls!"

"I'm only allowed mine because I'm not actually working inside," said Gavin, keeping his tone as calm as possible, annoyed as he was with this man's demeanor. "You want in, your Pokémon can't come with. Them's the rules, and I'm no exception from them-"

"Tauros shit!" interrupted the man. "I'm going in, and no weakling like you is gonna stop me!" And with that, he took a step towards the doorway, at which point Gavin, arms crossed, stood in front of him.

"I don't want to have to stop you," said Gavin. "So save yourself the embarrassment.

"OK, that tears it!" roared the man. Making a fist with his right hand, he took a swing at Gavin, who easily ducked under it. "HAH!" roared the man in response to this as Gavin kept evading his punches. "What's wrong, puny little insect? Not gonna fight back?"

"Oh, you want me to fight back?" said Gavin. "Is that really what you want?" He shrugged. "OK," was all he said. And the next punch that came his way, Gavin did not dodge. Instead, he caught the fist with one of his own hands, and to the man's astonishment, he couldn't seem to get his fist free from Gavin's grip.

"What the-" he began, before Gavin used his free hand to yank on the man's shirt collar, and pull him close, until their faces were but inches apart.

"Now you listen to me and you listen good, you little shit," Gavin said, his voice barely qualifying as a whisper, yet the man caught every syllable nonetheless. "My name is Gavin Duking Golurkson. I am no weakling. I grew up in Arceusdamn Pyrite Town. My father is former Cipher agent Zook Golurkson, a man known for his terrifying temper and formidable strength. And I assure you, I take after my old man in all the ways you really don't want me to." As he spoke, the large man's trembling caused Gavin's jacket sleeves to expose his arms a little bit, revealing definite muscle definition. Even if the arms themselves were in no danger of shredding through the clothing, it became abundantly clear to this man- Gavin was no wimp.

"I... But... You..." spluttered the man.

Gavin yanked the man's face a little closer. "Now get. The fuck. Out of here," hissed Gavin. After that, he let the man go, and he did not squander this opportunity whatsoever. He stumbled backwards, and once he was sure he was well outside of Gavin's reach, he wheeled around and fled like a Wimpod that took a Super Fang.

Gavin gave a sigh and leaned up against the building following this. Judging by the stunned looks he was getting from bystanders as well as the scattered applause which told him that this man was a problem for others as well, he had a feeling the rest of the night might not be so eventful. He might not even need to resort to using his Pokémon. Kind of a shame- Miror and Dudley could use the training. Oh, well, he figured. Easy money was better than nothing.

It was Gavin's second night on the job as the Trainer-Only Nightclub's newest bouncer. He gave a small smirk as he leaned up against the building- his first night was largely uneventful, save for one idiot who thought he could muscle his way past Gavin. How very wrong he was proven to be.

As Gavin gave a nod, allowing one man to walk in, he stopped. "Hey," he said. "Saw you put that moron in his place last night, just wanted to say thanks. He gets drunk enough, he'll send out his Pokémon and just start trashing the place."

"Had a feeling he was trouble," replied Gavin. "But yeah, you enjoy yourself, alright?" he added. The man nodded in response and walked into the Nightclub.

By and large, this night was quite uneventful. It seemed as though Gavin was getting himself kind of a reputation, as some rather suspicious-looking people, upon spotting Gavin outside the Nightclub, seemed to abruptly change their minds about approaching. Each instance of this got a good chuckle out of Gavin.

A few hours in, Gavin decided it was time for a snack. He produced the food he'd packed- a single Duking brand Poké Snack, and a bottle of Cacnea Cola. He grinned as he unwrapped the Poké Snack- these things were awesome, as far as he was concerned. Fuck everyone who said those things were only for Pokémon. Gavin had acquired a taste for them after all those years of placing these things at Orre's three Poké Stops in the vain pursuit of a wild Shiny in the Orre region.

After his first few bites of Poké Snack and his third or so sip of Cacnea Cola, however, he heard footsteps. And judging by them, they were coming from someone who was not walking at any sort of consistent pace. He turned, and sure enough, a man was stumbling towards the nightclub. The only thing that could have more clearly telegraphed his obvious inebriation would be if he was carrying a bottle of booze in his hand. Of course, given his current state, Gavin supposed it was entirely possible he'd been carrying one and dropped it by accident a while back.

Gavin took out his P*DA, started to compose an email to arrange for a ride home for this man- even if he wasn't going to enter the nightclub, he was in no fit state to be out in public.

"Hey. Excuse me," Gavin spoke up as the man approached.

"Heeey, get offa my case, person man thing," slurred the man. "I know ya don't let stinkin' drunk peoples inta yer nightclub thingy, so I ain'tn't goin' in yer nightclub thingy. I'mma mindin' my own Beedrillswax, and I's thinkin' you do the same."

"You're right- when I don't let drunks in here, smart thing to do is not to go in," agreed Gavin. "Now, having said that, you know what the even smarter thing to do would be? Get you a ride home."

"Aaaahhh, I's fiiiine," scoffed the man, making no effort to suppress a burp which blasted his horrible breath into Gavin's face. "Ya don't need ta ride me a offer home. I gots three legs what ain'tn't broked."

"Yeah, and what good do those legs do you when you're so damn wasted you're thinking I'm trying to 'ride you an offer home'?" retorted Gavin, still typing away on his P*DA. With one last button press, he put away the P*DA. "A taxi's gonna be here in a few minutes."

"Aww, come the fuck on, you joy buzz kill," the man slurred, stumbling where he stood. "Now listen you to me- I mighta had a couple drinkses today, but my sharp's as mind as ever! Wa- *burp* Watch me. I'mma walk a straight line like the cops make ya do."

Gavin was torn between disapproval and amusement- the man couldn't have walked a more crooked line if he was some sort of Zigzagoon/Spinda hybrid. And he rounded off the whole fiasco by walking face-first into a lamppost.

"Dude, you're drunk as a Skuntank," insisted Gavin. "Just sit down and wait for the taxi, OK? And you, no, no, hell, no," he added, turning to a violent-looking man with way more than six Poké Balls attached to his belt, who had clearly been attempting to sneak in while Gavin was distracted by the drunk man. "Don't think you can pull one over on the new guy. The management already told me you earned your ass a lifetime ban from the Nightclub, so clear the fuck out."

"Pfft. Make me, why don't you, rookie fuckwad?" sneered the newcomer. And so, with one precisely aimed kick... Gavin made him.

"Eheheh," the inebriated man chuckled drunkenly as the banned man hobbled away, clutching the very sensitive area Gavin had aimed that single kick at, and squeaking out feeble insults and threats as he left. "I see'd that guy give bouncers here all kindsa trouble before. I likes the ways ya handleds him." He was now sitting down on the sidewalk.

"Heh," grinned Gavin. "Sometimes that's how you gotta deal with people giving you shit like that- a good kick in the Sludge Bombs."

"Y'know what, you's alright, kid," said the drunken man, attempting to stand back up and failing miserably. "I'mma take the offer home yer ridin' me, a'ight? I ain't normally all drunken like this, y'know," he added. "Juss got a big promotion at work, went a little crazy cuckoo to celebrate, I ain't thunk I drunk too much, and suddenly I's the human Spinda."

"Well, just be more careful with your drinks, yeah?" said Gavin, as he could see the taxi approaching. "And little tip- drink some water between each drink. No hangover that way."

"Yeah?" slurred the man- now Gavin was helping him stand back up. "A'ight, I'mma try and 'member that." With that, Gavin helped him get into the taxi, and once the man managed to convey to the driver exactly where it was he actually lived, they were off.

Following that, the night was pretty uneventful. Gavin had to inform a few other people that they couldn't bring their Pokémon in, but they were pretty cool about that, and very agreeably deposited their Pokémon in the nearby PC. "Pretty cool gig," Gavin remarked to no one in particular, as it was approaching the end of his shift. "Too bad it's only a three-day deal. I could see myself doing this on a regular basis."

"Alright, last night on the job," Gavin murmured, leaning up against the building once more. "Let's end on a high note."

Indeed, it was the third night of Gavin's three-night gig as a bouncer at the Trainer-Only Nightclub in Beeyemgee City. The first night, he had to deal with a belligerent individual who had no intention of respecting the rule about Pokémon not being allowed in. The second night, Gavin found himself calling for a ride home for a very drunk man, while simultaneously keeping out someone who had been outright banned from the Nightclub. Decent bit of multitasking, if Gavin did say so himself.

Tonight seemed to be off to a fairly mild start. The first couple of people to come to the nightclub raised no red flags whatsoever. Gavin caught a glimpse of the banned man from the previous night, though only for a moment- upon spotting Gavin, the man turned tail and fled.

To Gavin's surprise, the first thing of note to happen that night did not involve any sort of trouble. A man approached him- Gavin recognized him as the drunk from the previous night, but to his surprise, the man was now not drunk at all. "Ah, hello, there!" smiled the man. "My memory of last night is fairly hazy, but I believe you were the individual who secured me a ride home?"

"Yeah, that was me," nodded Gavin. "Nice to see you ain't drunk tonight."

"Eheheh, yes, yes, I... may have overindulged a bit last night," the man admitted sheepishly. "Anyway, I wanted to thank you- I was probably a bit adamant in not accepting help, I'm sure, so I'm quite grateful you didn't stand for any of my nonsense. Here," he added, handing Gavin a wad of Pokédollars. "The least I can do as my thanks is pay you back what you paid the taxi driver to take me home."

"Oh, hey, you don't gotta do that, dude," said Gavin.

"Oh, I insist, sir," persisted the man.

"...Oh, alright, fine," relented Gavin, accepting the money. "So are you going inside?" he added.

"I think I will, actually," nodded the man. "First, though, I do have to deposit my Pokémon in the PC, yes?" he added.

"Or leave them at the Tree Grove," nodded Gavin. "Either way, can't bring 'em inside."

"Naturally," the man agreed, already accessing the PC. Once he'd deposited his whole team, he walked inside.

Following this, nothing too major happened for a while. People came and went. Some were allowed inside, others had to be turned away, whether due to being overly rowdy, or just refusing to go inside without their Pokémon on hand. And then...

"Hey! You!"

Gavin turned to look. It was the banned man from last night, yet again. And this time, he wasn't alone. Following behind him was a large, muscular man wearing a red t-shirt that did next to nothing to conceal his buff stature, and his blond hair stood straight up, making him seem even taller.

"Yeah, not so tough now, are you, you little shitstain?" sneered the man. "Want to try and kick me again? Just try it- my associate here'll kick your ass if you so much as make a move!"

Gavin smirked and chuckled. "You sure about that?" he challenged the man. "Somehow I don't think you're getting in here."

"Pffft. You realize who's with me, right, kid?" chuckled the man. "This guy's a-"

"-a former member of Cipher," Gavin interrupted. "His name is Zook Golurkson, and he's known for his fearsome temper and amazing strength. That about the gist of what you were gonna say?"

The man looked stunned. "H-how did you-"

"Because you've made a big-ass mistake in choosing your backup," smirked Gavin. "I'm Gavin Golurkson. And the very large angry man right behind you, whose son you just threatened, is my old man."

"...Fuuuuuuck," groaned the man, just before Zook grabbed him and forcefully turned him around, lifting him up off the ground so their heads were level with each other.

"Hey!" roared Zook. "You can get me to help you rough up all sorts of people, but when you mess with the son of Zook, you mess with the dashing and terrific Mr. Zook!" He turned to Gavin. "This guy giving you trouble, Gav?" he asked.

Gavin smirked, even moreso as he heard the man's desperate pleas for mercy. "Entirely too much trouble, Dad," he replied. "Let him have it."

And boy, oh, boy, did Zook let him have it. All it took was one power-packed punch from one of Zook's massive fists to get the man running off as fast as he could at the first possible opportunity.

"Good shit, Dad," grinned Gavin. "Y'know, you should consider being a bouncer yourself. Had to get my skills from somewhere, after all."

"I just might, my boy," smirked Zook. "Shoot me an email when you're done for the night- we'll grab a bite to eat at the local McDeino's and catch up, yeah?"

"Sounds like a plan," nodded Gavin. And after the rest of his shift passed without any further incident, that's just what Gavin did.

She lets Gavin change the subject - unfortunately for her, he asks about her name.
