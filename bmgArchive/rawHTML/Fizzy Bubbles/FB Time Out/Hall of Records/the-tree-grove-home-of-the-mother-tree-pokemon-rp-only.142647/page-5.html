August, Durandal, Seath - Despite Seath's initial reluctance to engage his powerful mother in battle, he was even more reluctant to disappoint her. His trust in the dragon that birthed him overcomes any fear that he may significantly harm her, and as a display of this trust, he fires off an Ice Beam at August. Certainly, the move was a good choice if he wanted to inflict significant damage and earn himself the advantage... But to open with one's trump card too early was nearly always a costly mistake, and Seath needed to learn that lesson before he made the blunder with a less forgiving opponent. Quick to react, August shields herself with Protect. She then breathes in deeply, letting the air turn caustic in her lungs, before unleashing her Dragonbreath. If Seath had been training well, the amount of time it took for August to prepare the attack (and to control the amount of breath so as not to completely obliterate her son) would be enough for him to spring out of the way when the spray of breath came... If not, it would certainly be a painful lesson that he needs more agility training. Not allowing herself to give her son too much time to escape the attack, August could only hope that she was calculating the right amount of breath. She assumed a creature of her own flesh and blood could take more than the little spit of breath that sent Lizzie writhing in pain, but certainly not as much as she would breathe in the face of his father, were she to battle him again. Indeed, in sharp contrast to the bloodlust and mania that she displayed in her passionate battle with Duran, August is far more calculating and precises in her movements and actions this time around.


Hedwig, Sangre - Hedwig had made her kill with the precision and devastating efficiency that Sangre expected of one who belonged to his species. Of course, it would take more than taking down a single, noisy Mothim to impress Sangre, but at the very least, Hedwig had shown herself to be capable in contrast to her blunder in the Park. Sangre finds it vaguely amusing that Hedwig seems to be avoiding the flock of Venomoth. Was she full already? No, she had to still be thirsting, at the very least. Was she intimidated by the numbers? ...Or did she, perhaps, have some moral compunction against killing Venomoth? Well, Sangre had no such compunctions, and if there was one thing Sangre knew about flocks of insects, it was that such gatherings could work to his advantage just as easily as they could work to his detriment. A well-organized, powerful group could work together to ensure his downfall... but a disorganized group that easily falls into disarray could be used to his advantage. Besides, if the group happened to have a straggler... Well, he wouldn't want to disappoint Mr. Darwin, now, would he?

Sangre takes a turn toward the group of Venomoth, eager to scope out their group. Though he remains focused on the task at hand, in the back of his mind, he can't help but wonder how Hedwig will react.


Lizzie - A young Axew runs as fast as her little legs will carry her toward the Tree Grove entrance, all while being pursued by a man with a green bandanna and a standard-issue daycare uniform, complete with an apron covered in advertisements for the Fizzytopian Daycare - UPN Branch. Though the man's plastic name tag proclaims that he is "happy to take care of your Pokemon," he does not seem quite so happy to be chasing down this impish little dragon as she high-tails it to the Tree Grove. As he runs, his arms and legs trail droplets of blood, and the jagged scars that are visible through his uniform look as if they've been inflicted with a sharp, thin blade.

"LIZZIE! LIZZIE, GET BACK HERE!!" The man tries to recall Lizzie using the Pokeball her trainer provided, but each zap of the red beam barely misses the dodging, weaving Tusk Pokemon. The blood loss seems to be getting to the increasingly pale man, who stops briefly to scarf down a Sitrus Berry and pull out his phone. He attempts to break into another sprint, but before he can go any further, a burly-looking Machoke stops the man dead in his tracks. He points to the sign, proclaiming the entrance to the Tree Grove, and gives the Daycare Worker a stern look. Laughing nervously, the worker backs up, as he sheepishly describes the situation on his Xtranceiver;

"We have a situation, here. Lizzie made another break for it. Yeah, yeah, she unfortunately made it out this time. Jumped clear through the window. Not a scratch on the little bugger... I did follow her out! I got the scars to prove it, man! ....Er, well, y'see, there's a little problem. I-I... yes, I know. Yes, but the thing is, she's gotten all the way to the Tree Grove, and I can't - okay. Okay. Look, I have no idea what to tell the trainer, okay? She probably knows her Pokemon's a total - Yes. Yes. I understand. We'll let Marion take care of it. Yes. Okay, okay - Okay, bye." The man ends the call and sighs heavily, trudging his way back to the Daycare Center.

Meanwhile, Lizzie smirks to herself as she explores the outskirts of the Tree Grove. Made it to Sanctuary, she thinks to herself with a smug sense of satisfaction. Lock ME up in that miserable shop, will she? HAH! Serves her right. Now she has to take me home, Lizzie muses, though her eyes suddenly flash with the slightest bit of panic. I hope she won't be too mad at me...

The little male Cleffa would have been there himself if it hadn't been for the height of the tree and his incapability to climb up the branches - the poor thing had been left crying in the stroller prepared for him and his "sister" as Francis made a run for less baby-talk-filled areas, only to be followed by the balloon-like Aisling.
