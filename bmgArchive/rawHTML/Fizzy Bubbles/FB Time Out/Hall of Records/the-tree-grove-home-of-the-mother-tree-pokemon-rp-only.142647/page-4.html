August, Durandal, Seath: Seath asks suddenly if greed was really such a terrible thing. August chuckles a bit at the mention of Durandal's closely guarded stash of gold and other precious items. As a young Dratini, August had engaged in similar behavior herself; Marion would notice shiny necklaces and baubles going missing from her drawers, and ending up in piles hidden around the house. Though Marion was tolerant of August's behavior, given what she had read about dragon nesting instincts, Minerva was far less forgiving. If any of her sewing needles or, Giratina forbid, if the needle went missing, Minerva would stealthily steal all of August's painstakingly collected treasure and bury it somewhere where the poor dragon couldn't find it. After five or six instances of this, Marion decided to redirect August's instincts for shiny things into items of deep personal value. In fact, the book that August had given to Seath was from that very pile, which Marion helped August hide from Minerva and the others. As August grew older, she became responsible for finding suitable hiding places as she and her trainer moved around. She wondered idly if Seath had started hoarding things yet... Perhaps books would become his "treasure". Only time would tell.

Seath speaks of an insatiable hunger, and wonders what the dragon of Sir George's tale was like as a hatchling, and what events in his life shaped him into the wretched soul he ultimately became. August gives a firm nod in agreement to Durandal's response, showing solidarity with her beloved in his ideas, and in his vow to protect little Seath from whomever might try and attack him. After her mate's explanation, August makes a statement of her own;

Greed for knowledge, huh? Sounds like curiosity to me... August chuckles a bit. Curiosity will lead you down some very strange roads, indeed. I do not believe you have met my trainer, Lady Marion... but she shares a similar hunger to you, my dear. Not content with the limited knowledge books provided about the world of the dead and the spirits of the living, she left home to search for her own answers. The hunger runs deep within her veins. It leads her to dangerous places where no mortal would dare tread... but she has loved ones who keep her safe in spite of her insatiable curiosity. I will be interested to see what burning question takes you on the road... but there is time, and you have much to learn before you start your own journey.

Durandal decides to lighten up the mood by asking about Seath's training, and the little Dratini is eager to relate what he has learned to his mother. Apparently, Lady Orchrist had taught Seath about suffocation, and had taught Seath this lesson by choking the little Dratini to near- wait, what?

A protective growl forms low in August's throat; she had never really trusted that miserable snake, though she had done her best to be cordial... and she had the NERVE to choke her baby?! Before the low growl turns into a full-on explosion of rage, however, August flashes back to the "lessons" that she had learned at the hands of Minerva when she was a young Dratini herself, such as "which of these angry Shuppet about to viciously attack you is not an illusion" and "how to tell if that horrifying shadow in Marion's room is actually a threat and not just Minerva trying to mess with you". To Minerva's credit, August had developed a real sixth sense regarding illusion and trickery, and had an uncanny knack for recognizing deception from Minerva's constant illusions and (often painful) pranks. Much as August absolutely hated the thought of her baby being harmed in any way, she knew that these tough lessons were often the most influential for a growing baby. If she coddled her boy and shielded him from all harm as a child, he would not be well-equipped to face the world as an adult dragon. At least Orchrist seemed to be showing some restraint, after all. Better he learn at the hands (or snakelike coils) of a teammate than to learn from the enemy... often a lesson learned too late.

Coughing loudly as a way to hide the growl, she gently rubs her baby's head, smiling down at him gingerly. "I think there is more to that lesson that simply how long it takes to choke someone, Seath. Think about it... What was Lady Orchrist really trying to teach you?"

Sangre, Hedwig: The twilight hours fell upon the Tree Grove softly, as the golden sun sank towards the verdant horizon, kissing it tenderly before sinking beneath the world. The serene dimming of the sun's light and the rising of the moon served as a call to the Pokemon of the Tree Grove to slow down, relax, and begin thinking of sleep; not so, however, for Sangre and his new hunting partner. Where the dawn of night brought sleep to most creatures, it was this time of night when Sangre began to feel truly invigorated and alive. The hunt was imminent, and he was ready to strike.

Sailing towards a tree on the outskirts of the Grove, the Crobat swoops silently into the foliage, stopping on a vacant branch. Dangling upside-down from his perch, wrapping his wings close to himself, he perks his ears and listens for the slightest sound. A gentle snore, the pitter pat of small Pokemon feet, a fallen twig or rustle in the grass; if prey happened to be about, Sangre would more than likely hear it. Much as he focused on his listening, however, he could not help but wonder if he and Hedwig would be able to register the same noises, or if they would each go after different prey. He had to admit that he was intrigued by the thought of hunting with another, and it would be interesting to see if Hedwig's presence would be a detriment or a help. The hunt itself would be the true test.

Hedwig, Sangre: As night fell upon the Tree Grove, a pair of Crobat silently approached. As Sangre hung upside-down from a branch of a tree on the outskirts of the Grove, Hedwig did this as well, her ears open, listening for any sort of sound at all. Naturally, so silent were her wings that not even her own sensitive ears heard them flap. She also didn't hear as she grasped the tree branch with her small feet. This was what Hedwig did best- she loved to hunt. She lived to hunt. When she was this focused, not a damn thing escaped her notice. Not the rustling of the grass as a gentle breeze blew through, not the quiet creaking of some of the weaker tree branches as the wind blew through them, not the fluttering of a Mothim's wings as he flapped his way back to his nest-

The Mothim. Hedwig slowly grinned as she silently let go of the tree branch and was airborne once more, her four wings flapping in complete silence. She gave a look to Sangre as though inviting him to follow her, then took off, flying in the direction from which she heard the Mothim. She made sure she was approaching the Bug/Flying type from behind, so there was no way he would see her coming. She started following the Mothim from a distance- not so far that it would be easy for him to lose her, but not so close that he would notice. She was totally silent as she waited for the perfect moment to strike. If she wanted to, she could devour the Mothim in a single bite, but it all had to be timed perfectly.

Her experience with Lizzie certainly taught her a great deal about the gentle exertion of strength as a corrective measure, though there were a few bumps and scratches along the way. The more August sparred with Lizzie, the more confident the Dragonite had become in predicting the little Axew's movements, and exerting force in such a way to teach her when she made a mistake and not harm her too terribly. August's heart hurt at the thought that she did not have the same intuition and experience with her own son; after all, it should have been her job to teach him how to fight as his biological mother, seeing as in the Pokemon world, the mother generally shares a body type (or the experience of said body type) with her offspring. Perhaps now she could make up for lost time, and dote upon her baby's growing aptitude in the process.

Taking on a battle stance, the mother Dragonite's eyes flicker with anticipation to see how Seath will engage her in battle. Don't hold back, Seath, August growls with an oddly reassuring smile. Mama can take it.

Sangre, Hedwig - At first, Sangre does not appear to react to Hedwig's look to follow him. The bat-like Pokemon remains perfectly still on the tree branch, unmoving and unblinking, until there is sufficient distance between himself and Hedwig that he can follow her without feeling himself to be an imposition. At that point, he silently launches himself from the branch, following Hedwig at a reasonable distance. Certainly, he had heard other insects and small creatures moving about in the darkness, but there was always the opportunity to veer off course for a meal of his own. At that moment, he was curious to see when and how Hedwig would take down this Mothim. After all, timing and strategy were what differentiated the satiated Crobat from the starving one. Perhaps he could learn some new tactics from this female. After all, Sangre was not so self-absorbed as to think that he knew everything there was to know about hunting. After all, there was always something new to learn about the art of subduing prey.
