"I always act strangely when I am in a trance, and apparently this time was no different. Do you not have any business to do with that team of yours?"
"Team Flash Fire? Nope, Silas said we could have the day off, so I was hoping we could hang out."

"Aw, but I was hoping we could-"
"Just...please...leave me alone." Carla caught herself about to snap at Happy and continued walking to the entrance.
"Okay..." the Flareon said dejectedly, his ears and fluffy tail drooping as he padded slowly towards the basement so he could sit and watch television with the others.

"I'm home, everyone!" Hyrem's voice came just as Carla had stopped in front of the entrance. She knew from her recent visions that Hyrem would be coming at this moment to open the door and walk inside, so she stood ready to greet him as he stepped through the stone door. "Hi, Carla!" Hyrem greeted his Espeon warmly, not expecting her to be there as she doesn't do that usually. He reaches his hand down to rub the side of her face, and she gave a soft, happy mew as she enjoyed the affection. "You're actually just the Pokemon I wanted to see," he added before taking out a small sack. "How would you like to go on an adventure with me? One where we wouldn't have to leave the base at all?" Carla then stared at him as if she had realized something; in her vision, she saw the two of them in a beautiful place, encountering multiple kinds of creatures, though all of them hidden from her view at this time so she had no idea of their identity, and all of this came after she saw herself staring at her trainer's face, both of their heads resting on the pillow she has seen on his bed, with what appeared to be some pink, powdery substance all over. With the vision in mind, she nodded, going along with what she saw of a possible future. "Okay, then it's settled! But we'll have to wait for nighttime to do it, OK?" "Espeon!" she gave another soft mew in acknowledgement. Hyrem was, of course, trying to keep it a surprise, but there wasn't much he was going to hide from a Pokemon that could see the future.

After he gave dinner to all of his Pokemon that hadn't already gotten it themselves, Hyrem was certainly exhausted enough to go to sleep, so he went to his bedroom where Carla was sitting on the edge of the bed waiting for him, as well as Happy and Marion who were on the floor and Zatch, Bubbles, and Juvia swimming in the aquarium. He got out the small sack again, reached in, and pulled out a pile of pink powder. "See this? This is called Dream Dust," he explained to Carla. "This is how we get to the Dream World. We just sprinkle it on our pillows, go to sleep, and that's all we need to do! I don't know about you, but I'm ready for some shuteye, are you?" A big yawn from his Espeon was sufficient enough to respond. Without further ado, he poured the dust on his pillow, spreading it out so it covered the entire pillow. Then he took another handful and spread it across his bed so Carla would be exposed to it no matter where she slept on the bed. Finally, he got under the covers and felt the Dream Dust already beginning to work, and he smiled as Carla joined him at the pillow, curling up so her head was on the pillow. He took his hand and ran it across Carla's side, feeling her soft fur and hearing her purr from the touch. Tonight would give the two of them a very good night's sleep as they drifted off into...

Not between the doors themselves, but what lies beyond. From beneath the door to your left, a gentle breeze blows at a constant rate, as if the area that lies ahead is for some reason windy. And from beneath the door to your right a subtle but notable heat wave escapes the chamber ahead, as if something burns behind the gate.

Knowing that you might be close to awakening, choosing one of the two doors might be all that remains.

The path goes on and so does time. What will you do?
