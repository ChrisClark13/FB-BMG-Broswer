The Pokémon Dream World
A place where your dreams can come true...



A place where fantasy and reality unite to the point it's difficult to tell what is real and what is not, the Pokémon Dream World will keep you guessing with every step you take. Whether you are strolling through the Pleasant Forest, soaring across the Windswept Sky, swimming in the Sparkling Sea, exploring the Spooky Manor, trekking up the Rugged Mountain, navigating the Icy Cave, or lazing around the Dream Park, this fantasy world will stretch your imagination well beyond its limits.

Sprinkle some Dream Dust on your pillow(s) before you sleep and you and your partner Pokémon will wake up on the Island of Dreams. Follow the rainbow bridge stretching out before you and you'll end up in one of the following seven locations:-



The Pleasant Forest is an area of the Island of Dreams in the Pokémon Dream World. Many trails wind their way through the thick shrubs and tall trees in this area. Bug, Electric, Grass and Poison-type Pokémon are often found here.

The Windswept Sky is an area of the Island of Dreams in the Pokémon Dream World. These beautiful cloudy skies are never marred by ugly storm clouds. It's only the fluffiest of big white clouds you'll find way up here where many a Flying-type Pokémon can be found.

The Sparkling Sea is an area of the Island of Dreams in the Pokémon Dream World. Sunlight glitters off the surface of this beautiful deep blue sea. The waters are mostly calm, inhabiting all sorts of Water-type Pokémon.

The Spooky Manor is an area of the Island of Dreams in the Pokémon Dream World. This creepy old manor is said to harbour some of the scariest Dark, Ghost, and Psychic-type Pokémon.

The Rugged Mountain is an area of the Island of Dreams in the Pokémon Dream World. The tough and unwelcoming terrain attracts many Fire, Fighting, Ground, Rock and Steel-type Pokémon.

The Icy Cave is an area of the Island of Dreams in the Pokémon Dream World. Nobody knows exactly how deep it goes as most cannot handle the sub-zero temperatures. Ground, Ice, Rock and Steel-type Pokémon can be found here.

The Dream Park is an area of the Island of Dreams in the Pokémon Dream World. A large park showcasing professionally trimmed hedges and a large beautifully manicured lawn, many Normal and Fairy-type Pokémon can be found here enjoying the wide open space.



Special Area
The Pokémon Café Forest is an exceptionally rare area of the Island of Dreams in the Pokémon Dream World. How it is accessed is still a mystery but should you find the Pokémon Café - home of a Pansage, Pansear, and Panpour - you will uncover the secret path leading to this special forest where some of the rarest Pokémon are found.​

Zone Guidelines
Currently, this zone is the only way members can obtain Pokémon with Hidden Abilities. As such, there are restrictions placed upon it. Please ensure you read the following guidelines before entering the zone.

- Each member may enter this zone twice per calendar year. Both introduction posts must be made between 1st January and 31st December to be considered a part of that year. For example, if I enter the zone on 31st December 2014 but the majority of my adventure is in 2015 it's still considered a 2014 adventure. If I then enter in April of 2015 it will officially be my first for the year and then October will be my second. I will have had three adventures in one year, but only two of them are 2015 adventures. I must then wait until 1st January 2016 before I can post another intro.

- All adventures will be from 10-12 updates in length so don't enter this zone expecting an epic adventure. These updates are designed to be rapid-fire. Updaters will also need to curb themselves here as this is not your usual adventure zone so please try and stay on track!

- Purchase some Dream Dust from your local Pokémart and sprinkle it on your pillow as well as your partner Pokémon's sleeping area. The mysterious qualities of this special powder will have the two of you falling into a deeper than usual sleep where mysterious things are said to happen.

- When entering their dream state players may envision up to three Pokémon on the Island of Dreams, but there is only a 50/50 chance of these three Pokémon being encountered during your adventure. During your first annual run through the zone you may only choose common and/or rare Pokémon (please refer to the Egg House rarity schedule). During your second run only two of the three Poémon you choose may fit the super rare or ultra rare categories, the third must be either a common or rare Pokémon.

- Upon reaching the Tree of Dreams you will have the option of choosing to leave with a Pokémon you have met, or of changing your partner Pokémon's ability to a Hidden Ability.

- Only one Pokémon will come out of the zone with a Hidden Ability, whether that's your existing partner Pokémon or a new one you meet in the zone.

It was nighttime in the Fizzytopia region, the bright blue sky of daytime replaced with a vast, dark expanse peppered with bright, shining stars all across the region, and the grounds of the old, abandoned Hidden Mansion were no exception. While the ghosts in said mansion were just becoming active for the night, it was just the opposite in one of the Secret Bases built on its inexpensive property.

In one such base, many Pokémon were either asleep or close to it. A dark grey Scolipede was curled up into a ball larger than a truck tire outside, snoring softly as he slept. Inside, a Meowth had fallen asleep on one of the couch cushions, a Pawniard had fallen asleep by the front door, though would awaken in an instant to the sound of someone breaking in, and so forth. Some Pokémon, however, weren't even close to being ready to sleep. A Shuppet and Banette were still wide awake, due to their Ability of Insomnia; a Crobat had just flown off to go hunting; and a Swalot, clad in a unique red outfit, had snuck away under the cover of night to fight crime as Captain Stomach. But the reasons these Pokémon had for staying awake weren't all as innocent as those...

Deep, deep underground, in a small, dark room illuminated only by the numerous monitors on the wall which displayed the goings-on inside the base above, a room accessible only by pressing a secret, hidden button in the elevator to make it go down, much further down than the base's owner knew it ever went, a very much awake insect was engrossed in whatever it was he was doing with his computer. The Shadow Ledian's four hands worked the mouse and keyboard as he watched the monitor intently. And then, as his piercing red eyes fell upon a certain piece of information, said eyes widened.

"Hidden Ability?" murmured Stewie. "What the deuce?"

Working furiously, Stewie typed and clicked away, his eyes scanning the various websites he was pulling up. At last, he seemed to be satisfied with what he had discovered, as he beat his wings rapidly and was in the air, pacing around his secret lair in midair.

"So," he murmured to himself. "It seems I need not settle for Swarm, then, as I've been doing all this time. Iron Fist- it's perfect for the future ruler of the world. Yes, yes, haven't I always wished to someday rule the world with an iron fist?" he chuckled evilly. "So all I would need is to access this Dream World... but how?"

As he pondered this point, he glanced at the monitors on the wall, and at that moment, he spotted his Trainer walking into the library on one of the monitors. He held a small bag of some sort in one hand, and seemed to be looking around warily. Intrigued, Stewie pressed a button on a remote control, and he began getting sound from the room as well.

"OK," Keith was saying as he hid the bag behind a book on one of the bookshelves. "The Dream Dust will be nice and safe there for the night. I'll decide who to take with me to the Dream World tomorrow."

"Well, well," Stewie said, his evil grin growing wider and more ominous by the second. "So the hat man's got his hands on some Dream Dust, hmm?" His plan coming together in his twisted little mind, the megalomaniacal Shadow Ledian flew down to the center of the room, where a skull sat on the floor, for seemingly no purpose other than decoration. Landing before the skull, Stewie jabbed the left eye socket with one of his fists, then the right, then the left again, then the right two times, and then one more time in the left.

As he made that final jab, the skull began to glow light blue. "Password accepted," said a soft voice emanating from the skull's mouth, as the glow spread to Stewie, who then promptly vanished into thin air.

In the entrance hall on the first floor, one of the hidden closet doors slowly opened, and Stewie poked his head out cautiously, glancing warily in all directions. It was by no means the first time he used the teleporter skull to exit his secret lair, and he had yet to be caught emerging from the closet where the teleporter would warp him to, but still, better safe than spotted. Indeed, all the Pokémon on the floor seemed to be either asleep or too busy to notice anything. Satisfied with this, the Shadow Ledian swiftly and silently flew up the stairs. Judging by the sounds coming from the bathroom, he could guess where his Trainer was. And this worked out quite well for Stewie- in fact, his plan depended on it. The entrance to the library was directly across from where he emerged from the staircase, and so he flew straight ahead. Once in the library, he wheeled around and made a Beedrillline straight for the book Keith had hidden the Dream Dust behind. He quietly pulled out the book, which seemed to go into detail on the history of evolution stones, and grabbed the small bag that had been hidden behind it. Replacing the book, he then flew into Keith's bedroom, smirking his satisfaction at hearing the sound of running water still coming from the bathroom.

Stewie hovered over Keith's bed, carefully opening up the bag. Then, he gently sprinkled a nice, even coating of Dream Dust all over the bed. And as he finished, he heard the sound of footsteps just outside. Thinking quickly, he dove under the bed, just as Keith entered the room, yawning loudly. The Shadow Ledian listened intently as Keith climbed into bed, and soon enough, all was quiet. Slowly, carefully, quietly, Stewie crept out from underneath the bed, and flew up onto said bed. Keith was lying on the left side of the bed, very close to falling asleep. Smirking his satisfaction that his plan thus far was working, Stewie lied down next to his Trainer and quickly felt himself falling into a deep sleep, as he thought of nothing but gaining his most fitting Hidden Ability of Iron Fist.

...

"Zzzz... huh... wha?" Keith murmured groggily. He was just waking up... or was he? He couldn't be sure. He didn't know where he was, it seemed like some kind of small island. And was it his imagination, or did he see some Pokémon floating around every time he blinked? Experimentally, he closed his eyes and tried to focus. It was kinda blurry... but one of them was small and blue, with long ears and numerous spikes. It almost looked like how Godric, his Nidoking, looked before he had even evolved into Nidorino- kind of like a female Nidoran would look. Satisfied with this assessment, he moved on to the second vision- this one was yellow, with a very large head, and what appeared to be a pair of exceptionally baggy pants- a Scraggy, he suddenly realized. Moving on to the third one, he focused- it was red and beige, and somewhat human-shaped, albeit with a long tail, large ears, and a curious tuft of fur on the forehead. This one didn't look too familiar to Keith, but he knew he had seen a picture of something like this before... oh, yeah, it was something called Pansear, wasn't it? Now satisfied with his assessments of what these mental pictures were of, Keith opened his eyes, only to find he was still on that strange island. Oh, and there was now also the minor detail of the Shadow Ledian hovering over him, staring intently at him.

"WAH!" Keith exclaimed, jumping up. As he did so, he vaguely registered the fact that he seemed to be fully dressed in his usual attire, complete with his cowboy hat. "St-Stewie?! What the- what are you- what is-" he stammered as he looked around. And then, as he saw what appeared to be a bridge that looked just like a rainbow, it sank in. "...this is the Dream World?" he murmured. "I- but- wait a second!" he exclaimed suddenly. "I didn't put that Dream Dust on my bed- wha-" And then, as he looked back at Stewie, he realized what must have happened. "You did this, didn't you?" Keith sighed. "Arceus damn it, Stewie! And the only reason you'd do this is if it benefited you in some way, so I'm guessing this has everything to do with your Hidden Ability." As he spoke, he dug out his Pokédex- mainly out of habit, though he was surprised to find that his Pokédex was indeed now in his hand. Remembering that most anything can happen in dreams, he shook it off, pointed the device at his Shadow Ledian, and pressed a few buttons.

"Ledian, a Five Star Pokémon. An evolved form of the Ledyba," droned the device. "Its possible Abilities under normal circumstances are Swarm and Early Bird. This specimen's Ability is Swarm. Its Hidden Ability is the Iron Fist."

"Iron Fist, huh?" Keith muttered, pressing another button.

"Iron Fist- Ledian's Hidden Ability," the device continued. "Ledian's punching attacks are more powerful than usual."

Keith sighed. "Well, that explains it, then," he said. "You want your Ability changed to Iron Fist, don't you, Stewie?"

"Led, Ledian," snapped Stewie, nodding at the same time.

"Ho, boy," groaned Keith. "OK, look, Stewie, here's how it's gonna work. We'll go through this Dream World place, and by the time we get to the end of it, I might allow you to change your Ability, but this depends a lot on your behavior. If you listen to me and do what I say, give me no problems, don't try to scare away any of the Pokémon we find, I will consider changing your Ability. So, we have a deal?" he asked.

Stewie folded both pairs of arms defiantly. "Led Ledian, Ledian led Ledian," he replied. Keith knew what this meant- of course, he couldn't understand a word of Ledian language, but Stewie's tone of voice and body language made it unmistakable to him- Stewie was telling him that he would make no promises. Basically, there was no guarantee either way. Keith nevertheless sighed, knowing this was the best he'd get out of Stewie, and led the way towards the rainbow bridge. He had a feeling Stewie might end up getting what he wanted even if he didn't behave like Keith wanted him to, but only time would tell...

The chirps of Kricketot echoed through the valley. Trees dotted the scene here and there, reaching toward the brightening stars with welcoming boughs. In the midst of these, a lone tent sat propped up, just above a large boulder. If a passerby were to approach, they might see the varied silhouettes of a trainer and her Pokemon within, conversing in the way such beings tend to do.

“Pax, you know how this goes.” Pest held a bag just out of reach, though she couldn’t keep a smile from her face. “Finish your food, and then we’ll see what this stuff we got from the Pokemart is about. I’m curious too, but first things first.”

The slender Sylveon didn’t quite pout — she was evolved now, and a fairy besides — but her mouth still drew a little to the side. Big blue eyes stared up at the trainer, breaking only briefly to cast pointed glances at the half-eaten food in her bowl. In keeping Pest’s attention on her face, Pax sneaked a feeler around and behind the human; the feeler wiggled for a second, anticipating, before seizing the bag with an audible yoink!

“Pax!” Grabbing futilely after it, Pest burst out laughing. “Okay, okay. We can do this now, if you want. It’s getting late, and I shouldn’t have gotten the discount feed anyway.” She took the bowl and dumped its contents outside, much to Pax’s delight. “You ready?”

Pax nodded vigorously.

Taking the bag back, Pest reached in and grabbed a pinch of Dream Dust. The mysterious substance sparkled in the lamplight as she sprinkled it across her pillow. Another pinch of dust was scattered next to the sleeping bag, and Pax leaned in to sniff at it; her nose wrinkled, and she sneezed adorably.

“All right, let’s do this.” Pest slipped into her sleeping bag, resting her head on her pillow. By her side, Pax circled her area a few times before settling down. “Let’s get some sleep. I’ll see you soon, Pax.”

The lamp clicked. Gradually, in the growing darkness, their breathing grew even.



Shapes swam across Pest’s mind. Dimly, she could recognize their forms, even as they passed from one to the next seamlessly. The wide, mad eyes of a Darumaka … the swishing tails of a Vulpix … the scuttling legs of a Venipede, skittering back into the darkness —

With a gasp, she stumbled. Arms flailed wildly, and eventually, she regained her balance. Taking a few deep breaths, she calmed herself, and took in her new surroundings. A bright blue sky stretched overhead, dotted with puffy clouds. Around her, vivid green grass spread beneath her feet. What caught her attention, though, was the broad path in front of her, reaching up into the sky. Translucent and striped with attractive colors, it bore the appearance of a rainbow. A smile broke out on her face as she thought of the Cloud Garden; this sight would fit right in there.

A light trill rose at her side. Pax stood attentively, taking in the sight of this strange place. Her feelers waved lazily in the hint of a breeze, and her tail wagged slowly, evenly, like the ticking of a clock.

“This place … it’s amazing. And we haven’t even really seen it yet!” Pest scratched Pax behind the ears. “This is really going to be great. Let’s go!” And with growing optimism, she set foot on the rainbow path.

Levin sat on the bed, lost in thought, holding the vial of Dream Dust firmly in his hand. The substance held special meaning - he knew more about its properties than most people, thanks to Zion's pioneer research into the matter. That it was approved and available for use in every Pokemart in the land was already a meaningful achievement, but to be able to use it himself was a frightening prospect at the same time. There was a world of difference between the Dust's theoretical properties and what could happen for real when he tried it out. He knew for a fact that it opened a gateway to the Dream World in his slumber... but he knew nothing about getting out.

Zion assured me the Dust's effects were temporary and that waking back up was practically a statistical certainty. But as Zion itself also said, it doesn't sleep.

It wasn't just the fact that he might be trapped in a dream-like coma that scared him - it was the thought of condemning someone else to that same fate. He knew he'd need protection inside the Dream World, because his dreams very rarely went smoothly from start to finish, and he had no idea whether the Pokemon in that realm would be hostile. Going in alone was dangerous, but bringing someone along was selfish.

He briefly contemplated storing the Dream Dust away, knowing it'd vanish in just 72 hours... until something started lightly pecking his leg repeatedly. Looking down, he saw his beloved red bird, one of his closest companions - Solair the Fletchinder. His sense of honor and bravery were second to none, knightly virtues he made sure to uphold at all times - or, according to Mazo's translation, "as long as the Sun shines". Levin was quick to realize what Solair wanted, and couldn't help but smile as he picked the bird up and sat him on his lap.

- You don't have anything to prove anymore. We'll face Vesper again sooner or later, and that's all the retribution you'll need. I can't put you through this, not knowing if we'll make it back... especially not you.

But Solair was relentless. He chirped loudly, clearly arguing his point; it was obvious he disagreed and wanted to travel to the Dream World, but Levin couldn't understand the Fletchinder's stubbornness on this particular matter, when he'd always been flawlessly obedient.

His wings. He needs to prove a point, master.

Mazo was at the door, smiling at the scene. As always, he had it all figured out... but this time, Levin was quick to follow.

- Your wings... Vesper had them pierced and rendered useless. That's what this is about then... You want to face him with even stronger wings than before.

With the strongest wings in the world. Solair needs to show Vesper he's unbreakable. And you too.

The Fletchinder nodded affirmatively, spreading his mended wings as he did.

- You don't need to show me anything Solair. But I owe you a rematch against that Mega Beedrill... and I want to see Vesper's face when we show him the true results of his actions! You win... let's do this.

Mazo smiled and left the room to resume his training with Aya. Levin hesitated briefly but finally opened up the vial, sprinkling the magical dust on his pillow. He grabbed Ivan's coin for luck and laid down, Solair by his side. And while it usually took him hours to fall asleep, this time he found himself drifting off almost instantly...

- I can't believe it...

They made it. Somehow, it actually worked; there was no mistake about it. Lush grass surrounded them and the sky above was clear blue. A gentle breeze touched his skin and it felt warm, although the Sun itself wasn't visible. Solair, flying close by, looked equally astonished. And if any further proof was necessary, a wide rainbow road stretched before him into the unknown. Maybe the Heiml myth of the Bifrost bridge had a layer of truth to it after all...

Before he could address Solair, his vision went blurry for a moment and his head felt excessively light; he brought a hand to his forehead, as if about to faint, and looked ahead to find three creatures crossing the rainbow path towards the sky... From what he could make out, the first one was limber and much faster than the other two, running across the rainbow road as it it were in a hurry; his blurred eyesight had trouble adjusting to its speed at first, but it soon became apparent that it was a Sentret. Farther back, two other creatures walked lazily side by side but not acknowledging each other's presence.
