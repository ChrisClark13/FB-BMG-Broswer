As Keith and Stewie continued down the path, the Cottonee seemed to vanish. This got Keith wondering- when the Pokémon he encountered in the Dream World were no longer within his sight, did they still exist or not? Or anything else in this realm, for that matter? Were they even real at all, or were they simply illusions, figments of his own imagination? And if it was indeed the latter, then how was he to realistically expect anything he found in here, effectively in his own subconscious, to become real when he woke up?

"Led. Ledian!" Stewie snapped. He noticed that Keith had slowed down somewhat, and was trying to spur him to move faster.

"Hm- oh, sorry, Stewie," Keith muttered. "I dunno, I was just thinking- I mean, this whole thing, it's a dream, right? So who's to say that what happens in here is gonna have any effect on anything once we wake up? I mean, if someone told me that their own Pokémon originated in their dream and just somehow became real, I'd think they're crazy..."

The Shadow Ledian rolled his eyes and smacked Keith in the back of the head with one of his fists. "Led Ledian ian Ledian Ledian!" Stewie said, and though Keith couldn't understand a word of it, he figured he might at least be getting the gist of it.

"...You're saying I'm being stupid, right?" Keith asked. "I mean, true enough, there are confirmed reports of Pokémon coming from the Dream World... and that Dream Dust stuff, that's real enough, too. That what you're trying to tell me, am I close?" he asked. Stewie gave a curt nod in response. Of course, he had been saying stuff along those lines, just with a bit more profanity in the mix. Keith nodded back. "Right... You're right," he nodded. "Thanks, Stewie, I needed that. Let's keep moving."

And keep moving they did,and before long, the path seemed to widen before them, eventually coming to an end in another clearing. This one featured taller grass and pleasant aromas. Keith breathed in deeply, enjoying the scents as he began to register a familiar sensation... light-headedness, dizziness... it was almost as if... as if he was just waking up! His eyes widened as he realized that his time in this Dream World was drawing to a close, and thus, he needed to haul ass over to the Tree of Dreams! The Tree of Dreams- how did Keith even know to call it that, anyway? Not long ago, he wasn't even aware of the existence of such a tree, and yet it came to him so naturally, like it was a fact he had known all along. No doubt the Dream Dust had some sort of hand in that, Keith realized- it must have been designed to help the affected Trainer to know at least the general idea of what to do in the Dream World, if they wanted anything to get done by the time they were to awaken.

As Keith walked in the direction of the Tree of Dreams, he thought back on the journey that brought him here. Leaving the Heracross be to eat its honey in peace was probably a good move- hungry though the scent had made him, he didn't want to waste too much time. He made a mental note to try and remember to get Hermione to incorporate some honey into his breakfast, though... Not staying to sunbathe with the Sunkern, also a move he wasn't regretting- that surely would've eaten up valuable time. He could sunbathe when he was awake, after all, if he were so inclined, and the Sunkern had no problem with their unwillingness to stick around... The mystery of the Pokémon whose cry sounded from either behind or beneath a fallen tree returned to Keith's mind, but he didn't pay it much mind- he was still satisfied with the notion that his own subconscious wouldn't allow harm to come to an innocent Pokémon, so it was more likely that whatever it was, the tree hadn't harmed it... Helping out that Nidoran, he definitely felt good about doing that, even if Stewie felt differently... He thought back to the distressed-looking Plusle, but reminded himself that he hadn't come across anything that might cause it distress, nor did he think it would've been running towards the source of such distress... though slightly curious as to what that had been all about, a glance at Stewie reminded him that time was running short, mainly because Stewie was miming the action of looking at one's watch in a blatant attempt to remind him that time was running short. He quickened his pace as he recalled helping out Oddish, and coincidentally travelling down the same path as Cottonee... overall, Keith realized, as he focused on the massive tree he was headed towards, he could honestly say he had no regrets about this, considering he had come here decidedly earlier than he had planned to.

The ride on Golett's back was something he wouldn't be forgetting any time soon. Seeing the landscape of his own dream from such a distance reassured him that the power of the human mind was astounding - in essence, it had created an entire world for Levin and Solair to explore. At the same time, it seemed like the automaton was enjoying himself as much as Solair, both engulfed by an aura of future power that revealed their future forms. This was the first time Levin saw Solair for what he could one day be, and it was a glorious sight.

When Golett finally landed, and after they said their goodbyes, Levin was left with the feeling they'd meet again sooner or later. He had the same feeling even before the events that transpired, but now it was a near-certainty. Perhaps now wasn't the time for a permanent union, but that Golett would one day join his ranks was something Levin had no doubts about. He wasn't one to believe in destiny, but it was hard to ignore such a strong conviction.

Solair, back to his regular Fletchinder form, landed on his shoulder; he was visibly enjoying himself, much more than he'd ever seen the Fire-Type - and especially considering he was still recovering from the humiliation suffered at the hands of Vesper and his Mega Beedrill. It was a comforting feeling; he had researched the Dream World so extensively and pushed for the approval of Dream Dust so hard that being here, and seeing his Fletchinder so happy, made him feel vindicated. He'd been right about the Dream World as a whole, he was right to bring the crestfallen Solair along, and he was right to reach out to that Golett.
He was Levin Sanders and he was very, very often right about things. The choice of path presented by the crossroads, therefore, became an easy one.

Right it is.
