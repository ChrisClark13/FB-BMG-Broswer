As Keith knelt down beside the Nidoran, she took a step back. The sudden presence of a human made her wary, but she wasn't frightened enough to flee. It was clear to Keith that the small Poison-type was giving him the benefit of the doubt before deciding whether or not to act on her survival instincts. Not exactly the smartest thing to do when survival is your goal, but this Nidoran was clearly a trusting sort. Still, she looked rather disbelieving as Keith deposited a number of berries on the ground before her. Keith supposed he couldn't blame her, after all, he had to look at it from her point of view- if Keith wasn't the first human Nidoran ever saw in her life, he had to be close to it, considering she lived in his own subconscious. But was this really Keith's subconscious, per se, or was the Dream World some kind of strange alternate dimension that the Dream Dust allowed access to, or- Keith banished these thoughts from his head, lest he give himself a headache pondering the true nature of the Dream World. Or worse- lest he become so lost in thought he wound up running out of time.

Seething in silent fury, Stewie watched the Nidoran as she gathered up as many berries as she was able to (which, to his and Keith's mild surprise, was all of them). His mind was swimming with the very tempting idea of shoving Keith down the path, or blasting the Nidoran with an attack of some sort, but he kept reminding himself- he wanted that shot at his Hidden Ability, even if it meant that the hat man would have to wait until he was awake to be punished for this needless distraction. Yes, that seemed to comfort him- the notion that he could always wait until he actually got Iron Fist to vent his frustration at having to endure this insufferable holdup. Near as he could tell, once a Pokémon actually acquires its Hidden Ability, there's really nothing the Trainer can do to reverse this...

Blissfully unaware of the true nature of Stewie's patience, Keith watched as Nidoran gathered up the berries and dashed away behind a nearby bush. At this, Stewie gave an evil grin, pleased that the Poison Pin Pokémon had gone. But then, just as Stewie was preparing to start shoving his Trainer again, Nidoran returned! This time, she only held two berries, but as she shyly approached Keith, she dropped them at his feet.

Keith smiled. "For us? Thank you!" he said gratefully. He picked up both berries, then turned and offered one to Stewie. The Shadow Ledian initially had every intention of refusing the berry, but as he opened his mouth to start ranting again, his stomach cut him off with a rumbling sound. Between that and the knowing smirk forming on Keith's face, Stewie knew that if he refused, he'd be the one wasting precious time that could be better spent getting him Iron Fist. Without a word, he swiped the offered berry from Keith's hand, sniffed at it suspiciously (it's a habit one picks up when one's girlfriend is an unreasonably paranoid Pawniard), and took a very small bite out of it. Though his face didn't change, he kept on taking bites of the berry, more and more frequently, until he had devoured the whole thing. Keith smirked- Stewie must have enjoyed it, even if he was trying to hide it. Taking the berry Nidoran had given him, Keith ate it in just a few bites (his mouth being considerably larger than the Shadow Ledian's). "Mmm," he said appreciatively as he finished the fruit. "Thanks, Nidoran," he smiled.

Gentle waves lapped against the hull of the Rough Skin, causing her to rock softly in her moorings. It was getting late, and many of those who called the submarine home were getting ready for bed, whether inside, in the forest on the land nearby, or even out in the lagoon for those who didn't like being cooped up in the relatively small amount of water inside the hold. Deep in the bowels of the ship, the lights were being turned off for the night.

In his living quarters, Dave looked around. Only a few Pokémon shared his room after hours. In the corner, Jason was settling down for the night, the Croagunk preferring to sleep while squatting for reasons unknown to his trainer. On the floor near his bed, two Pokémon snuggled together, a Houndour nuzzling up to the slightly larger form of a Luxio as they curled up on the heated metal. Smiling, the trainer stepped around Carrot and Angua, heading for his bed, already dressed in his pyjamas. This was a special night. Opening a bedside drawer, he reached inside and withdrew a small vial, glittering purple mist swirling inside.

He regarded it for a while. The substance had recently been harvested and distributed within Fizzytopia, giving trainers and one of their Pokémon access to the Dream World. He'd visited the realm before, when he was just starting out, but had been interrupted mid-dream, cancelling his adventure before he could capture a Pokémon within. This refined Dream Dust could him to control when he went there, though it was limited to twice a year for every registered trainer, which made sense. Didn't want the region flooded with Pokémon with rare Abilities, after all.

As he opened the vial, he sprinkled its contents onto his pillow, watching the almost smoke-like substance pour out, swirling around for a moment before settling down. Shortly after, his chosen companion for his trip to the Dream World entered. A blob of slime dripped down from the ceiling onto the bed with a splat. However, rather than being the result of shoddy waterproofing, this was actually a Pokémon. Green spots lined around its body, while two beady black eyes peered at him, a crease in the body forming into a smile. The fisherman smiled back. "You sure about this?" he asked, to which the Goomy nodded as vigorously as a limbless blob could.

"Alright then." he said, opening the covers and sliding in. Ever since he'd met Liessa in the Friend Safari, he'd promised that he'd help her become as strong as possible. Most members of the species that had been documented had either the Hydration or Sap Sipper Abilities, but a few, extremely rare specimens had been observed to have extra-thick slime that slowed the opponent down if they made contact. The Ability had been dubbed Gooey, and no other line of Pokémon had been noted to have it. If he was going to help Liessa increase her strength, getting this unique Ability would be a great start. "See you on the other side, lass." he muttered, feeling the Goomy squidge onto his chest for the night, both trainer and Pokémon settling in for a long night's sleep...

--------------

He'd never really been able to focus on dreams. But this time was different. He was floating in some kind of void, Liessa on his shoulder, with him clad in his normal fishing garb instead of his PJs. As they floated through purple mist, he wondered how long it would take to reach... well, wherever it was they were going, but he managed to make out a few shapes within the fog. A wriggling, whiskered form he recognised as a Barboach slithered past. Coming the other way was a clapping shell that he'd owned before, there was no mistaking a Clamperl when he saw it. His had since evolved into Moist, but the mollusc had a different fishy evolved form that he'd love to train... Lastly, a figure that looked like it had five limbs swung past from imaginary branches. A Mankey! Didn't quite fit with the other two, but he wasn't complaining.

Before long, the mist thinned, revealing a bright blue sky with a rainbow running into it from his location. He looked around, finding himself on a strange grassy island, while across the multicoloured archway were several other areas. Looking around, he shared a glance with Liessa, smiling at the Soft Tissue Pokémon. "Right then, time for you to get some thicker skin, assuming we don't meet any Pokémon I want to bring hom with me." he said with a grin, to which the Dragon-type blew a sticky Razz Berry. With his friend on his shoulder, Dave set off across the Rainbow Bridge into the Dream World, this time to come out on his own terms...
