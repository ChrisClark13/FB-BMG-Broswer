Inexplicably, you have the distinct feeling that any one of these Pokemon would be willing to leave the Dream World behind at your request…

A Berry, unlike any other you’ve ever seen, glowing with a myriad colors and vaguely shaped like an Espeon– once again, despite not being told, you recognize the item as a Dream Berry, which Carla looks decidedly interested in trying out…

This is the culmination of your dream. Moments away from waking up, you must now make the final choice!

"Is this really a dream...?"

That was what Katsura found himself asking after he opened his eyes to find himself suddenly gazing out across a bridge of rainbow that branched off in numerous directions through an open sky, connecting to a multitude of floating islands, each seeming to have its own unique biome. He had been to the Cloud Garden before, so the sight of gravity-defying landmasses was hardly unusual to him, and he knew a likely reason for why he was in this odd place.

He had purchased an odd item known as Dream Dust from the local Pokemart on a whim. It was said to have the ability to cause one to enter a particularly deep sleep; within which one could experience miraculous things. He had also heard it was best experienced in the company of a Pokemon, and so he had given his Gible, Tia, the privilege of sharing his cozy bed the night before, the little dragon having found a comfortable spot by his feet to lay.

Now she was standing at his side, glancing about the unfamiliar landscape, and looking quite bewildered by her sudden change in scenery. For a dream, it felt all too real. Katsura had had lucid dreams before, but this was something else entirely. as he felt totally in control of everything he was doing, and all of his senses felt just as sharp as if he were awake. Still, he didn't feel entirely convinced, and proceeded to inquire of Tia to help him confirm whether or not he was in fact, dreaming.

"Hey Tia. Do me a favor and give me a nice big chomp on the leg, if you would please."

The Gible tilted her head to the side at his odd request before following through, though her "chomp" was more like a nibble, for he could scarcely feel a thing as she gently closed her massive jaws around his left leg, seemingly afraid of harming him.

Katsura sighed before scolding her a little. "Oh, come on. Just pretend I'm Flandre. Pretend that I'm being annoying, and give it to me good, okay?"

After hesitating a moment, the Gible gradually tightened her grip until her teeth finally began to puncture Katsura's flesh, eliciting a sharp cry of "OW!", after which she immediately released him and backed off. Even though he had asked for it, she still looked a little guilty about what she had done.

"Well... I guess if I am dreaming, this must be one heck of a deep sleep." he said, as he rubbed his minor shark bite. "In any case, we should take a look around."

With that, the two of them headed off down the rainbow bridge. A thought was lingering on Katsura's mind, though, and that was a cryptic vision he had witnessed before he "awoke" in the Dream World. He had had fleeting glimpses of a trio of Pokemon; a lively Helioptile, a cheery Goomy, and a skittish Joltik . He wondered if it was perhaps a sign of things to come. This was no ordinary dream, after all...
