"You're sure the two of you can look after the shop yourselves? Perhaps I should stay after all..."

Waffle smiled and shook his head as he once again reassured his wife things would be perfectly fine in her absence. Following his father's cue, Pan nodded vigorously as well to indicate he was more than happy and willing to help out by covering the others' shifts, having fully recovered from the funk over his first fight's failure. In fact, he practically assisted his dad in gently but insistently ushering Mom and his sisters out the door (particularly a pouting Mimi whom he hurriedly plopped in the picnic basket beside Patty).

"Well, all right then. If you're certain..." Blueberry elevated an eyebrow slightly at the boys' suspicious behavior, as if they couldn't wait to get rid of her for some unknown reason. Nevertheless, she shrugged and bent to blow her husband a farewell kiss, sparks flying between them as she adoringly pecked his cheek.

"I'll be back later tonight. Take care, darling~"

She dotingly pinched a part of Pan's wax as well, making him promise to call their trainer immediately if anything went wrong, before floating off to join Jess and her colleagues. Waffle and Pan waved as the group of girls hopped on Tessie the Lapras's back as they set off towards their destination: a day's outing in the big city. ...As soon as the speck of cerulean vanished from sight of the shore though, the two remaining ghosts twisted and grinned at each other, Waffle raising a mitt to his lips as he winked and whistled a summon to the sky. A circling shadow overheard suddenly dived down to the beach, nearly knocking over the unprepared pair with the intense wind resulting from the width of its wingspan. Waffle extended out his static like a tennis racquet to steady his son and turned with a tut to the sheepish Togekiss, who lowered his head in apology before cautiously reaching out his large, fluffy limb as a ramp for the riders to board. Waffle carefully boosted the Liltwick up and lifted himself afterwards, as the airplane Pokémon prepared for takeoff by adjusting his flight goggles. Remembering one last thing to take care of before they left, Waffle leaned over the side and zapped the sign in front of the Café to temporarily read "CLOSED", before settling back into place and advising his child to hang on tight as Hermes beat his wings, shooting straight up into the atmosphere again with a scream of glee from Pan. In an attempt to enhance the thrilling sensation and show off a bit, the cheeky plane performed a few extra loop-de-loops, pausing to let his load recover from the dizziness before drifting off in the same direction as the girls had gone, tailing safely from a far enough distance so they wouldn't be spotted...

...After about an hour of sailing, both parties arrived at Cape Effervescence, and the airborne unit cruised around the coast as the seafaring crew pulled into the port, the former only approximating land after the others had disembarked and completely melted into the crowd. From above, Pan idly observed a rowdy game of tag apparently being played between a trainer's Pokémon on the deck of one of the boats in the harbor, nearly causing their cargo to capsize overboard. Disappointed by the anticlimax, he shifted attention to the streets as they descended and glided guardedly over the city, still on the wary lookout for fear of a member of their family or staff glancing up and instantly recognizing familiar figures - despite their destinations being in entirely different districts. On the way to their own goal, the jet's altitude continued to decline as they passed right over a woman walking along Affinity Avenue with her Flapple, managing to catch a snippet of their conversation:

"But... with this place being so backed up... I suppose I should have seen that coming, in hindsight. Valentine's Day, it's so obvious a shop like this would have a lot of business."

Waffle and Pan glimpsed nervously at each other, worried what this might mean for their own objective. Waffle had already put in a preorder a while ago online - plus previously looked over potential adoption candidates in private and found the perfect match for their growing family - so he was only here today to pick up his wife's surprise Valentine's Day present personally to prevent any shipping and handling mishaps (as well as avoid paying the fee in the first place), given the especially "delicate" nature of the package. Thus all there was left to do was sign the papers and finalize the process... So he hoped.

As the trio touched down in front of the door to the sweet shop, they could see a long line already queued up at the counter through the window. Hermes handed the spirits off with a salute before heading off to patrol along the avenue - bow and arrow at the ready "just in case" any couples were in need of a little push - eager to play Cupid's partner for the coming holiday.

When Waffle and Pan entered the establishment, it quickly became apparent just how overbooked the place was, as it took some time to hail down one of the employees. Still, since Waffle had fortunately arranged everything in advance, his request was indeed ready and waiting as the worker smiled (albeit somewhat wearily) after scanning down the list, disappearing into the back room before returning carrying a big basket decorated with berries and bows. He set it down on the floor as Pan peered curiously within whilst his father went over the paperwork, spying a tiny teacup swathed in blue blankets, seemingly fast asleep. It was his first time seeing his soon-to-be second baby sister, and something about her serene, innocent expression told him she'd be a lot easier to handle (compared to Mimi, whom he could swear he recently witnessed stitching together a raggedy doll resembling himself when he coincidentally passed by her room during a midnight potty break, muttering darkly something about "big brother" and "balloon times").

"Right, everything seems to be in order... I'm sure you and your wife will make great parents to this little one, given how well you've raised your other children. The two of you are quite lucky, actually. Turns out this Sinistea is a rare breed, it even has a mark of authenticity if you look at the bottom..."

As the clerk droned on, Pan idly lifted the corner of the sheet covering the cup out of sheer boredom and nosiness, noticing a small symbol stamped on the underside as described. He blinked and shrugged before tucking the tea back, not thinking too much of the birthmark's meaning. Meanwhile, Waffle was firmly shaking the human's hand, seeming to have come to an agreement at last - only the organic guy seemed the one grateful for some reason.

"Would you really? We'd really appreciate it... I'm afraid we can't pay you anything, but how about we waive the cost of the basket itself? Or we could provide a complimentary one on the house, for your trainer perhaps...?"

Waffle nodded in acceptance of the deal, and called his son over to inform him he'd be helping out in the kitchen for a few hours to try and clear through all the backlog. While he wasn't quite as skilled at baking as his wife (his specialty being main dishes), he figured it was the least he could do to offer his own services in return. (Besides, it could also provide an opportunity for free advertisement of their own business.) So if Pan could please watch over his new sibling for the time being and swear not to get into any trouble...

The candle simply nodded in understanding, not terribly keen on trying his hand at cooking himself at any rate. Thus he waddled back over to the basket to "wait" while his dad was led behind the counter to join several other Samaritans who had also volunteered their aid. As Pan approached the container again however, he frowned as he realized the spread seemed somewhat... flat. Pulling off the layers, he paled upon finding it completely empty. Casting about in a panic, he perceived a path of slight purple puddles leading outside, and determinedly stalked the drops after their obvious source. The drips lasted all the way down the road, and at this rate Pan knew he wouldn't able to catch up on his own. Scouting out Hermes as he was about to sneak up on a shy pair sat on a bench in a nearby park, Pan tapped the Togekiss's shoulder before he could cause an embarrassingly explicit display of PDA. As soon as he explained the situation, Hermes nodded and let the Liltwick on again, soaring over the street as they tracked the trail all the way to the...

Trader’s Bazaar

Squinting amongst the busy stalls and throngs of shoppers, Pan finally discerned the drink from afar as she ducked behind a stand selling an assortment of silverware, as if intentionally hiding from them for some strange reason. Puzzled, the pair alighted beside the kiosk and Pan poked his head around the bend to discover the ceramic softly crying, tears of lavender liquid spilling down the sides.

"S'not," she mumbled between sniffs, "true..."

The candlestick cocked his wick in confusion, and she tilted a couple degrees to point to the base below, where the branded seal that had been there before was now smudged beyond recognition. After some consolation and questioning, Pan came to learn that she had originally been sold in this very marketplace as a "genuine" article - only to be abandoned by the buyer when an appraisal determined the item was a forgery. She thought her only chance of being taken in again was to deceive someone into thinking she was the real deal, so she drew another decal on herself by tracing the design, but felt too guilty for being a "fake" to keep up the ruse.

"I'm not special. Not like you..." she sighed wistfully, and it took a moment for Pan to comprehend she was talking about his own unique coloring scheme. It had never occurred to him others might be envious of his coat - after all, he hadn't exactly been born this way either - but that led a flash of inspiration to occur to him. While he didn't believe any embellishment was necessary for endorsement, if it made her feel better (much like Mimi's comfort costume), then there was one idea he could try... Something he had snuck out of his trainer's bag when her back was turned, and stowed away expressly for this trip with the intent of tagging some buildings with naughty graffiti, but perhaps it could fulfill a different purpose instead...

…

When Waffle eventually came out of the kitchen, Pan was waiting patiently for his father beside the basket with an angelic beam on his face. The spectral toaster eyed his son skeptically as he peeked in on the parcel to check its contents, scrutinizing the still peacefully snoozing mug - who now stood out in stark contrast against the azure background by a fresh coat of bright pink paint. He stared for a solid minute, before deciding not to inquire and wrapped the cobalt bolts of cloth back up to protect the priceless porcelain bundle, ready to love and cherish the young chalice lass regardless, as he knew her new mother would do the same.

OoC - Picking up 1x Star Basket.
