Alcremie's Amore
~A Fizzy Valentine's Day Event~


Nestled on a quiet corner of Affinity Avenue in New Fizz City's charming Alderboro district, Alcremie's Amore is a specialty sweet shop that also doubles as an adoption agency - particularly for food-based Pokémon such as Milcery, Applin, etc. - which prides itself on its custom candy baskets for that very special someone, complete with an extra topping of a new buddy for the occasion. <3 However, with the holiday of love fast approaching, orders are backed up and they're extremely behind schedule. The owners are desperately looking for volunteers to help out in any way they can: Whether it be baking in the kitchen, wrapping and decorating baskets, or delivering them to their destination - the busy staff could use all the assistance they can get this year to make sure everyone receives their gift in time. As compensation, trainers are allowed to take home one free themed basket filled with goodies of their choosing~

Please pick ONE of the following:

Strawberry Basket

Berry Basket

Love Basket

Clover Basket

Flower Basket

Star Basket

Ribbon Basket

So what are you waiting for? Please help provide the best present of all this season to the lovely pairs out there and Pokémon waiting to be united with them! ^-^

You are allowed to claim a basket until February 13th, 11:59 PM GMT. The contents will then be revealed on February 14th. RP is optional, however you will not be able to receive Pokédollars or Bond from this event.

"It's believed by the people of Galar that if you give the one you love an Applin, you'd be together forever. Keith knew this, too- that's why he sought you out, caught you, and gave you to me all those yea- no, for you, that would've only been last year, wouldn't it?"

"Flap! Flapple!" came the response. For as Willa Masters was walking down Affinity Avenue, she was accompanied by the very Flapple she had gotten from her then-boyfriend for their very first Valentine's Day together.

"Heh... I had hoped I could return the favor," Willa murmured. "I don't know whether he'd any interest in training an Applin of his own, but if I know my husband, I know he would absolutely welcome one onto his team regardless of his prior intentions, and would certainly appreciate the gesture. But... with this place being so backed up... I suppose I should have seen that coming, in hindsight," she added. "Valentine's Day, it's so obvious a shop like this would have a lot of business.

"Flap Flapple flap?" inquired Flapple, with a curious look at his Trainer.

Although Willa did not understand Flapple language, the Grass/Dragon-type's meaning got across nonetheless. "We're here because they've offered free themed baskets for any volunteers who come and help with their large workload," she explained. "I might not have been able to buy a gift basket for Keith as I had intended, so all there is to it now is to help out here and hope that the one they give us will suffice," she sighed. "We will be assisting them in their kitchen, Flapple," she stated. "I would think you particularly well-suited to giving meaningful input in regards to Applin-themed basket items in particular, and after all the praise my cooking skills have consistently gotten from my husband and daughter over the past decade, even I think it feasible that there might be something to what they're saying," she chuckled.

"Flaaapple?" asked Flapple.

"Oh, Keith doesn't know I'm doing this," Willa said with a sly smirk. "It will be a surprise. He and Maribel are doing some training- as far as they're concerned, I am out running errands. It's... not exactly a lie," she added. "I believe Meowth once put it as, 'just vague enough to work'."

"Flapple, Flapple, Flapple," chuckled Flapple, flying in circles over Willa's head, seemingly chuckling.

Willa chuckled as well. "I know, I know, I'm worrying too much about that," she conceded. "Indeed. Ah, there it is," she added as the shop came into view. "Flapple, let's go," she added.

"Flap!" responded Flapple, following Willa into the bustling shop.

OOC: Collecting 1 Ribbon Basket. Thanks!

"14 Strawberry Baskets? Check. 12 Berry Baskets? Check. 26 Love Baskets? Sounds like February..." Silic paused as he looked back over his cargo for the day. "Hold on, we're missing one. Noted."

His smaller pokemon scurried around the boat as he finished taking stock, taking full advantage of the fact that they weren't need at the moment to play a game of tag. It sounded like Larvitar was getting agitated, though- Milcery had been hovering a bit too high for him to reach, giggling at the stony lizard's hapless attempts to tag her. Unfortunately for the Cream Pokemon's sense of superiority, another playmate had decided that two can play this game with the 3rd dimension. Before she could realize that something was off, Chinchou had already leapt off the stack of Ribbon Baskets toward her with unexpected accuracy!

The Trainer started to call out "Hey, be careful now," but his warning fell on deaf ears- milcery was already spinning through the salty sea breeze in attempt to shake the larger pokemon off of herself... and right into the uncounted baskets they tumbled! Larvitar simply trudged over to the mess, pushed a container out of the way, and proudly tagged his adversary as her now-blue eyes stared dizzily out into the horizon. Silic sighed and bookmarked his progress. "Alright, I think that's enough roughhousing on the boat you three. We'd all be in trouble if one of those had fallen into the harbor..."
