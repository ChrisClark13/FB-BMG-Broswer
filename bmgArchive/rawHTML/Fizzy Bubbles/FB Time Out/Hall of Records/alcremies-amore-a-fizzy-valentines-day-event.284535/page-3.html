It was well into the evening. The place, the dining room in the Poisonous Palace. The day, Valentine's Day. And the people involved, well, as one could reasonably guess at this point, Keith and Willa Masters. The couple had just sat down to a lovely candlelit dinner, including steaks cooked well-done. To some, this was entirely too cooked, but a preference for one's steaks done this way was just one of the things this couple had in common.

"Ah," smiled Keith, as he looked into Willa's eyes. "It's been a while since we've been able to have a nice dinner just this, just you and me."

"Indeed," Willa smiled back. "Maribel's volunteering at the Pokémon Center, and our Pokémon are all doing their own things. It's very nice when we can have moments like these."

"Absolutely," Keith agreed. "I love our daughter so much, but it can't be denied that we need to have our time as a couple. And I'm glad we could do this on our first Valentine's Day since the time distortion."

Willa nodded in agreement. "It concerned me at first," she admitted. "The time distortion, I mean. But in all honesty, we'd spent the first decade of our marriage basically focusing on raising Maribel, so the Pokémon training had taken a bit of a backseat. And now-"

"-now it's like we have a chance to pick up right where we left off, without having to worry about neglecting our daughter," Keith finished. "Have our cake and eat it, too."

"I couldn't have said it better myself," Willa said, her smile widening ever so slightly.

Following this, the couple dug in, finding, to their satisfaction, that Cotton and Shelley had cooked everything to perfection (not that this surprised them any, of course!). And once they had finished, Keith took Willa's hand, and led her to the living room, whereupon he handed her a small wrapped present. She accepted the package gracefully, though then ripped the wrapping off in pretty much one quick motion, showing that she was holding a book of some sort. As she allowed it to fall open, her mouth quickly followed suit; she flipped through pages upon pages of photographs of a young Willa with a slightly balding green-haired man. Several of these pictures depicted the two sitting on rocks overlooking a tall cliff and watching colorful swarms of Vivillon flying by. "...me and father?" Willa murmured, her eyes moist with tears. "Keith... wherever did you get these pictures?" she asked quietly.

"I talked to your mother," Keith replied. "When she visited last month, I had asked her to bring all the old photo albums she had, and while you were helping Maribel actually train Salamence that day after the Galarian Slowbro raid, she and I looked through them and picked out all the best pics of you and your dad," he said. "I know how close you and he were, so..."

At this point, Willa wrapped her husband in a tight hug. "I love it. I love you," she murmured, her voice thick with emotion. The hug lasted several minutes, after which Willa had calmed down somewhat. "Um... I did get you something as well," she continued, striding over to the nearby closet and pulling the Alcremie's Amore gift basket out of it. "But... it didn't exactly go as planned," she admitted.

"How do you mean?" Keith asked.

"Well, you know that shop, Alcremie's Amore?" Willa said. "The sweet shop/adoption agency? Well... I had hoped to give you a custom gift basket that included an Applin, for the same reason you caught one for me on our first Valentine's Day," she admitted. "But... well, you can imagine, this time of year, that shop was absolutely overworked. They needed volunteers to help out, they were behind schedule, and I was only able to get a complementary gift basket from them in exchange for my volunteer work. Unfortunately, their complementary gift baskets did not include any Pokémon, let alone Applin..."

"Well, let's see what we got here," Keith said, opening up the gift basket.

"...oh, not one of those," sighed Willa as Keith pulled a Chocolate Ball out. "How any halfway decent Poké Ball manufacturer could see any sort of practical use in a Poké Ball made out of chocolate..."

"Yeah, I don't know what they were thinking when they decided that was a good idea," Keith chuckled. "And I dunno if you've ever seen this around Christmastime, but there's a Gingerbread Ball as well. Same idea- it's as edible as it is impractical." With that, he reached into the basket again, this time pulling out the Box of Chocolates.

"Well, those could be good," Willa murmured, eyeing the chocolates.

"Yeah," Keith agreed. "Uh, that said, says here these are only meant for Pokémon to consume for the purposes of teaching Chocolate Bomb."

"...of course," sighed Willa. "I- what in the...?" she added, as Keith now pulled a bow and arrow out of the basket.

"Cupid's Bow & Arrow," Keith stated. "You give it to a Pokémon to hold, and in battle, they can try and use it to make the enemy infatuated with them. Heh, Artemis might get some use out of this," he smiled, putting that aside. The rest of the contents consisted of Valentine's themed Rare Candies, chocolate Mysterious Gummis, and a red case containing
500.

"...I apologize," sighed Willa. "I tried to get you as meaningful a present as the Applin you gave me..."

"You said you were volunteering at the shop to be able to get this?" Keith said.

"Yes," Willa nodded. "I didn't know until we were given the baskets that they wouldn't contain Pokémon."

"Well, what'd they have you doing?" Keith asked.

"Mostly baking," Willa replied. "Flapple had come along with me, and was giving his input on what should go in the Applin-themed baskets, whether anyone wanted it or not."

"Would this account for where you were going all last week?" Keith asked.

"Indeed," Willa nodded. "Again, I apologize for-"

Keith did not let Willa finish, instead walking over and hugging her. "You spent a week toiling in an overworked shop, baking for complete strangers, just to try and get an Applin to give me," Keith said.

"But I didn't get the Applin," said Willa.

"But you tried," Keith smiled. "When I gave you Applin, I had known even then that I wanted us to be together forever. To know that, ten years into our marriage, you still share that sentiment, it means more to me than anything, regardless of whether I actually got an Applin out of this or not. It is the single best gift you could have possibly given me."

Willa had no words to say in response to this. Fortunately, her manner of responding ended up being entirely nonverbal in any case, and it still got her point across to Keith loud and clear. And once that kiss had ended, the couple left the living room, and hand in hand, upstairs they went.
