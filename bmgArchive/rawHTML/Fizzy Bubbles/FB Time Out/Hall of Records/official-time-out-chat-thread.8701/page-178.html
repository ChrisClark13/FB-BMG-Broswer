Updates certainly have changed over the years, but that was an unforseen result of the quality of RPing improving as well. The RP section of the registration test was designed to weed out those who weren't even attempting to reach the maximum four line rule that was around even in my early days, as well as to nip self updating in the bud before it could become a major problem. We didn't expect for this to lead to massive replies, but it eventually did. This has been both a blessing and a curse. The upside is that we've gained some excellent roleplayers who love to give their replies the best they can each and every time, the downside is that some feel they have to give lengthy replies or they don't cut it. It's unfortunate really, as I've stressed many times that replies don't need to be long to be good, and the same goes for updates. Obviously some effort is required as no ZA wants to update someone who doesn't even attempt to provide them with a decent reply, but it's all down to the individual in the end. Those who put in more effort will be rewarded with better prizes and a much more rewarding adventure and feeling of self-satisfaction, it should be as simple as that.

One consequence of the new generation of "big repliers" is that they have also gone on to become updators, and with them the quality of updating skyrocketed as well because storylines became much more involved than they once were. This hasn't necessarily been a complete bonus for FB either, because it's extremely difficult for updators to write lengthy updates for each individual on a regular basis.

Unfortunately, those of us who gave simpler, speedier, updates were being criticised for not giving the same quality as those who gave lengthy updates. The pressure was on to create larger adventures which only served to add to the problem. We're there spitting out the updates (quite decent ones at that, just nowhere near as long), and people are more than happy to reply to them because that's what they're here to do. The moment a massive update came along by someone else, however, people were fawning all over them and saying how great it is to see such an update and how they wish they could have a chance to be updated by that person as well because the adventures are soooo much better. It's a complete slap in the face for the speedier updator who is providing many more people with many more adventures to see those kinds of comments as it makes them feel inadequate. If they couldn't step up to the mark they were criticised for it and eventually they resigned, leaving us with fewer updators which is never a good thing as all it's doing is compounding the problem. Speed was sacrificed for length, and as a result FB started to slowly grind to a halt.

Both kinds of updates have their advantages, but it's up to the members to decide what they want to do. Shorter updates are still very decent and there's no reason you can't give a long reply, but it's really unfair to criticise the updator when you're receiving ten times more updates than you will from another ZA who likes to provide a long update. The longer, more 'epic' updates also have their own fanbase, and are definitely a bonus for FB as it gives those who really thrive on RPing the chance to challenge themselves. If you want this kind of adventure though, you'll have to stop sniping at how long it takes your updator to get them posted. If you want epic then be patient and wait for it. If you want frequency then don't make the person updating you feel that they're not good enough. Comparison is really unfair, and so long as you're enjoying replying it's not needed at all. If they're bad updates that's one thing, but if they're good, just a lot shorter and therefore more frequent, there's no need to be critical. There is no reason both can't work side by side and be appreciated by everyone.

Rapid fire zones were introduced to cater to those who wanted more frequent updates, but that didn't really work out as "rapid fire" implies that you will be updated every other day and that's just not always possible when we all have responsibilities outside of here as well as the desire to do other things. We're not robots. All of us, ZA's or not, have lives. We have school, work, friends, family, sports, hobbies, other sites that we visit. An updator shouldn't have to give any of that up just to devote all of their time to updating. They do it because they enjoy it, but the moment they feel pressured they leave and that's not getting us anywhere.

LC, please don't compare your own updates to anybody else's. You were one of the first ZA's to have updated me when I first joined FB. Your updates weren't extremely long, but I really enjoyed your adventures. Just do what you do best and update, enjoy what you're doing, and I'm sure those being updated by you will enjoy your adventures as much as I did. I tried something a little different in the AR this time around, but it's not something I feel I'll stick with long-term and will definitely be reverting to my former style with the next group of sign-ups. Everyone has their own style of updating. Some prefer to make them longer, others prefer to give shorter ones, and some like to mix it up, providing a little of each. Just be yourself and you'll find it far more enjoyable.
