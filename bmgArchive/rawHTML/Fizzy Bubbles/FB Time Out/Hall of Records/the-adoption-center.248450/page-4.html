He had been keeping track of this Pokemon for some time, mostly because it looked familiar, like it was a smaller form of a Pokemon he met before. Now that Hyrem had a closer look, the two baby amphibians with a blue bump on each side that were swimming around in the small pond of the Adoption Center were looking more and more familiar, and as he checked the Pokedex he finally figured out what they resembled:

Tympole, the Tadpole Pokemon, and the pre-evolved form of Palpitoad. When near each other, Tympole will vibrate their cheeks to produce sound waves at too high of a pitch to be heard by humans in order to communicate. Usually this communication consists of warnings of danger.

"So that's what you're related to!" said Hyrem. "Emily never showed me one of them...I bet she'd really like to see me have one though, whenever I see her again!" His mind was made up from that point as he called over one of the employees. "I'll take the male, please! Henrietta might like him too...anyway, off we go, James!"

Balmund said:
As Belinda walked down the adoption center's hallways, she thought to herself just what sort of qualities she was looking for regarding a new party member. In truth, most of them where rather superficial and shallow. The thug wasn't looking for a friend, but rather just a fighter, one who would look and act the part of a tough and hardened gladiator, and as long as it had guts to spare she would be happy enough to take it regardless of whatever character flaws came with that package.

Of course, it wasn't like she would confess her intent to the caretakers. She knew what was their angle. Their type was all about finding loving and caring homes to the abandoned and forgotten, and this was a task that required due process to ensure the right trainer matched the right Pokemon. The fact made her a tad anxious; she wasn't good at faking personality tests and interviews, but perhaps if she remained somewhat honest and withheld the right information she could get away with it.

As she pondered the possibilities Belinda halts suddenly as a creature finally caught her attention, and she grinned in satisfaction as he glared back at her in defiance. She didn't need to know him any further. Only one look and she knew he was the one.

"No," Tate grunted, petulantly, standing in the doorway of the Adoption Center. A group of visitors to the neighboring museum had gathered on the lawn, watching the exchange in morbid curiosity. A white truck with a colorful Fizzytopia Parks and Wildlife logo was parked outside of the annex, a frayed looking man with curly blonde hair was clearly trying to reason with the disgruntled manager.

"But-"

"No."

"But you have a government contract-"

"No."

"But there's no where else to take it-"

"Listen here, you," the manager asserted, aggressively, pointing a finger at the poor man who was only trying to do his job. "We have three already. Do you know anything about Shuppet? They have mondo abandonment issues. Like, all of them. Species wide. Big ol' hivemind of insecurity. You're not giving me another one to deal with. I refuse. Do you even know how hard it is to contain Ghost-types? Literal impossible. Literally impossible! They go wherever they want, do whatever they want, terrorizing my staff. No. I refuse. You can't make me. You can't make me."

The government worker stared, mouth agape, a broken look in his eyes, as 5'5" of belligerence refused to let him do his job and move on with his damn life. The small assortment of carriers at his feet echoed with various noises, their inhabitants growing restless. For most of them, this wasn't an issue beyond the pain of dealing with a cranky Pokémon, but the source of the problem, perhaps realizing she was being talked about, decided she'd had quite enough of her confinement, and a moment later her unnatural body -- little more than a limp cloth supported by ethereal force -- squeezed out through the grating. She flopped on the ground, before expanding to a proper size again, drifting into the open space between the man who'd brought her here and the manager that evidently was refusing entry. A pink tongue that shouldn't have existed slowly extended from a slit in the fabric, bright yellow eyes staring at her gate keeper. Tate glared back, and for a long moment the pair were locked together in this way. Then, Tate hissed through clenched teeth and threw both hands up into the air, before seizing the Shuppet in a sudden hug.

"Celebi save me, you're too fucking cute," Tate growled, aggressively affectionate. The man blinked, bewildered.

"S-so... are you going to take it, then-?" He asked, not daring to hope.

"Yeah," Tate grunted, as if the lack of willpower was somehow his fault. Still gripping the poor Ghost-type against the front of an inappropriately casual t-shirt, Tate made a vaguely hand gesture, and turned towards the door. "Let me go get the paperwork."

Left alone with the rest of the drop offs, while the mercurial manager disappeared inside, the man heaved a sigh. He was getting way too old for this shit.

Terri did not blink as she eyed the grey bug, even as aforementioned grey bug hissed warily at her. The Nincada clearly did not like her at all.

And yet, if this served to deter Terri at all, she had the most peculiar way of showing it. She tilted her head and gave a small smile. "Yes, I think we'll be good friends," she stated.

"Um... miss?" came the voice of one of the Adoption Center workers. "I... couldn't help but overhear you just now, and... well, this Nincada seems to loathe you."

"Oh, she does," Terri nodded in a very matter-of-fact way. "I wasn't talking to her."

Bewildered, the worker looked around, wondering if he'd missed something. But no- Nincada was the only Pokémon in the vicinity. "Um... I don't-" he began.

"I'll take the Nincada, please, mister," said Terri. She held out her hand, in which was clutched the 200 adoption fee. She already had a Poké Ball in her other hand, ready to go.

"...If you insist, miss," relented the worker. As he went off to fetch the paperwork, Terri turned back to the Nincada.

"It's a shame you're stuck with her for the time being," she said. "Don't worry, Vanish- it'll go by quickly," she smiled reassuringly. "And Anger, I promise, you only have to put up with me for a little while. Then I'll find you someone you can tolerate." More irate hissing from the distrustful Nincada. And again, if this was any sort of a deterrent to Terri, she hid it well.
