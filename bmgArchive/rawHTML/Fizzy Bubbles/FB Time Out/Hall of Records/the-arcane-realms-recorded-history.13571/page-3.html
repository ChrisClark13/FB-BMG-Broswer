As it was they were just going to have to chalk it up to missed opportunities and move on as best they could with what they had. Still, it sure would have been convenient.

"Cydaquil!" he shouted to the little fire type pokemon, "You're doing great! Just hang in there a little longer. I want you to try to put some distance between you and Spearow. Just enough so that it can't get you with it's beak. Then I want you to Leer at it again so that it doesn't leave the ground and then follow up with another Tackle."

Stark's hands started to clench into fists as as his focus and determination became stronger with each passing moment. He knew that if Spearow became air-born again that they would lose the little bit of advantage they had managed to gain. -Come on Cyndaquil. I know you can do this,- he thought.

"No," the word softly escaped Stark's lips as this unexpected turn of events unfolded. In a perfect world Spearow would not have been able to get air-born again and they would have been able to keep their advantage. Then again, this wasn't a perfect world. And sometimes, one has to adapt to the unexpected changes and difficulties life throws at you. Otherwise you're bound to never succeed. Not that any of those thoughts were of much comfort for Stark at the moment.

The Spearow was fatigued. That much was obvious, but still it continued to fight rather than turn and flee, giving everything it had into one powerful attack. Stark had to admire the bird pokemon's resolve. Still that left himself, the girl, and Cyndaquil in a precarious position. One that he had hoped to have avoided.

Cyndaquil stood upright watching as Spearow gathered it's energy while waiting for his partner's new strategy. A shiver coursed down the fire pokemon's back. Fear? Excitement? Probably a little of both causing the effect. His eyes opened wide as he lowered himself back down to all fours in order to take full advantage of his speed and agility.

Stark's right hand instinctively reached down to the one LevelBall hanging from his belt. Maybe, just maybe he would get lucky enough to hit the Spearow with it and once again throw it off from it's attack. His hand soon pulled away from the LevelBall. It was too great of a risk. He had gotten lucky once. He wasn't about to take that chance again. Not at the expense of Cyndaquil possibly being hurt and not with the girl's safety at stake. Besides, he really didn't want to waste his only PokeBall that recklessly. Regardless he needed to think of something quick. It wouldn't take Spearow long to gather enough energy for it's attack.

"Cyndaquil!" he shouted, deciding to stay the course with his partner, "This is it. Spearow is going to be coming. I want you to Leer at it when it gets about two-thirds of the way down to the ground. Then doge out of the way in case your Leer fails or it just keeps falling. Finally, I want you to Tackle it once more when it gets low enough that you can get it. Just stay strong pal. I know you can do this."

Cyndaquil's eyes widened as it waited for Spearow to make it's move. Suddenly, the flyer cawed loudly. It's voice echoing while sending a shiver down Stark's spine. Within moments the flying pokemon began it's descent, while the girl behind Stark let out a scream of her own. Instinctively he reached his right hand behind him and offered it to the girl in an effort to comfort her and possibly to comfort himself as well. He prepared himself to turn and shield the girl with his body from Spearow in case it decided to attack them rather than Cyndaquil.

"Take my hand," he said softly to the girl as his eyes stared intensely at the events ahead of him, "I won't let anything happen to you. I promise. Just keep Hoppip safe, okay?"

Amazed by how deceptively fast Bella was, Stark eventually arrived at a house only to hear her teasing voice tell him that nobody was home. "Humpf," Stark grunted as he took a step back on the doorstep to assess the situation. He hadn't realized that he was going to be playing an impromptu game of tag when he accepted her offer to come by the house. However, he did find that even while not exactly in control of the game, he was having fun. A reminder of times past. The constant sticking out of her tongue at him giving him the extra motivation he needed to catch her.

"Alright, you wanna play? I'll play," he commented to himself with a smirk. His eyes began to scan for a way to communicate with Bella. The windows were closed, the curtains drawn, the door shut. There seemed to be no way to get a message through. That's when it hit him. A message. Like a lightbulb turning on, Stark suddenly found the letter box and smiled.

"Alright, now that I've solved that problem, what next?" he pondered quietly to himself hoping his words would lead him to the next answer like following a river to it's source. Unfortunately, all he could think of was an old story about some pigs and a wolf. "How did that go again?" he muttered to himself, "Little pig, little pig, let me in.... or I'll huff and I'll puff and I'll blow you're house down."

Stark smiled at the visual image in his mind of blowing a house down before shaking it off and getting back to the problem at hand. He approached the letter box and opened it, peering inside to see what he could see, then doing the only thing he could think of at the time, he yelled in, "Hey! I know you're in there. Let me in or I'm gonna... I'm gonna... well, ummm.... I'll huff and I'll puff and I'll blow you're house down!"

Cringing at his solution, Stark turned and looked at Cyndaquil shrugging his shoulders in defense of his actions. "Oh! Like you had anything better," he remarked to his little partner.

"What the...?" Stark's question was cut off by a horrible stench. Within seconds he found himself sitting on the ground coughing and gagging in an attempt to overcome the smell. Cyndaquil found himself on the ground as well, almost in synchronization with his partner coughing and gagging. Bella's verbal taunting only adding to the insult.

"At least she was nice enough to blow the stench away," Stark muttered to himself, "Of course we're still going to need a tomato juice bath to get rid of it completely."

Looking up Stark could see Bella looking at him through the letterbox. She was so going to get it when he got to her, he thought. Soon enough a purple and white pokemon made it's way out of the letterbox followed by Bella's challenge.

"Oh I'll beat it alright!" Stark responded defiantly as he stood up, "Even though I have no idea what it is."

Pulling out his pokedex, he scanned the toxic smelling pokemon. "Stunky. The skunk pokemon. It protects itself by spraying a noxious fluid from it's rear. The stench lingers for 24 hours."

"Great," Stark exasperated sarcastically with a roll of his eyes, "24 hours. Just what I needed. Alright Cyndaquil, as much as I'd like to let you take this one on, I think we should give the new guy a chance. Okay?"

Cyndaquil stared at his trainer sheepishly before nodding his head in agreement. Stark, smirked in confidence as he pulled his LevelBall from his belt and tossed it out, "Okay Spearow! Let's do it!"

In a flash of light Spearow popped out of the pokeball flapping it's wings to gain some altitude, then turning and diving toward Cyndaquil landing next to the small fire pokemon. Suddenly the bird started pecking at the little firestarter causing Cyndaquil to scurry behind Stark for safety.

"Spearow! Stop that!" Stark shouted at his new teammate, "You're supposed to be on our side now!"

A loud "Caw" was the only response as the bird spread out it's wings wide and stomped toward Stark and Cyndaquil. It's eyes narrowed in determination. Obviously it still hadn't forgotten that Cyndaquil had beat it in battle and was still holding a grudge against the little starter.

Stark shook his head in disbelief, rubbing his forehead in the process, "Look. Do you want to battle or not? If you do there is a perfectly good Stunky over there. There is no reason to try to fight Cyndaquil," Stark explained, "Besides you guys should be friends! Now are you going to help us fight that Stunky or do I have to put you back in your pokeball?"

Spearow glared at Cyndaquil for a moment, then huffed and turned toward Stunky. The bird's eyes narrowed as the determination to not be embarrassed again flowed through him. With a loud "Caw!" Spearow flapped his wings and hovered just above the ground ready to take on this new threat.

"Alright then," Stark said with a nod, "Whatever you do, try to keep it facing toward you. It's stench comes from it's behind. Use your speed and your flying ability to pick your opportunities by moving in and out quickly. The last thing you want is to end up smelling like us. Alright Bella! Get ready to lose! Spearow fly up in the air out of it's range and Growl at it."
