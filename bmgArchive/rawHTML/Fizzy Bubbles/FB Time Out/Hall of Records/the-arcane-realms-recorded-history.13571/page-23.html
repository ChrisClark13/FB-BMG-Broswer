While last minute preparations took place, the young thief sat himself down near a window seat, placing the bag of things he had acquired a side and waited for their departure.

He looked outside one more time saying goodbye to the city he had adopted for more than a decade.

An hour past before he opened his eyes again, finding more passengers had come aboard. He yawned, wiping the drool that formed during his nap aside, and stretched, looking outside again, this time greeted by an emerald green forest view whooshing past as the train sped along.

Content with how much distance he had placed, he reclined in his seat and let his eyes wander a bit before having his attention brought to a man across the way that seemed to be picking at something underneath his bulky jacket.

The gentlemen was acting strangely; Ethan simply dismissed this as someone who was just nervous about riding a train, but before he could turn away the man looked back at him, and jumped to his feet, his attitude changed to one full of convictions. The stranger opened his jacket and revealed to everyone on board the bombs the size and shape of a baseball strapped around his stomach, yelling "No one move or I blow us all up!"

Ethan's body was no longer his own, going through a sympathetic fight response; he sprang from his seat and lunged at the perpetrator. Using the adrenaline that now was coursing through in his veins, with all his power and weight he brought the would be mass murder to the ground.

He's hands now entangled around the man's throat, angry that his getaway plan was being ruined by such an amateur, he whispered to the man, "Let's see what'll kill you first me or your bomb."
