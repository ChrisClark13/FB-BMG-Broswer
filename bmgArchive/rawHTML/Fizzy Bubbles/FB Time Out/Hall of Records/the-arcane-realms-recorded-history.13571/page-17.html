Lunatone crashes to the ground and with a final flurry of Embers from Volcape it lay unmoving, the light in its eyes diminished as it was KO’ed. Level-Up for Volcape.

He had been travelling for four days on the ship now, and from what the announcements were saying, it would only be a few hours before he would arrive in Fizzytopia, at which point he would go his separate ways with many of the passengers and find the location for his first adventure. The tension was getting to him; he had been waiting for such a chance for a long time. Glancing at the waters once more, he then turned and made his way down some of the ships narrow stairways, heading for his cabin at the bottom.

It wasn’t the nicest place to stay for almost a week, but it did exactly what it needed to, but at the same time it did only what it needed to. There was a small bed against one of the tiny, sickly white walls of the cabin, with a single pillow and a mattress and cover. No bedposts. On the other side of the room, there was a plastic desk, a plastic chair, a small phone, and one of those tiny little hotel style fridge’s which you had to pay for use of. Strolling over to the desk, he slowly sat down, letting his previously windswept black hair fall back into what was generally its usual position during most times of the day, a heap of untidy mess. Grabbing a comb from the interior of his backpack, he slowly combed down all of the sticking up hairs to make it look fairly average. This was simply because Rick despised his hair getting in the way when he was reading, and combing it out the way seemed a simple method to stop such a problem.

Reaching for his backpack once again, Rick placed his Guide to Fizzytopia down on the desk, and stared at it thoughtfully. On the front cover itself, Rick could see a variety of different places, including a picture of a cloud filled sky, a picture of a beautiful tropical beach, and a picture of a tribal village all of which arranged to show the best of the places they were advertising. Rick smiled and began to flick through his guide. He went through section by section, slowly ruling out different areas due to the fact that they were too far away, too hard for beginning trainers, or illogical like the Ocean of Mystery. After about an hour of looking through the individual guides, he narrowed it down to three options. The Graveyard, The Desert of Illusion and the Silverton Ranges. Scratching his head in puzzled ways, he reached for his belt, and slowly gripped the pokeball residing there. “Right then, looks like I’ll need a bit of help choosing. Come on out Belle.” He exclaimed to nobody in particular as he pressed the center button on the pokeball, a flashing red light spraying out of it instantly as the appearance of his starter Pokemon arrived.

Quite an unorthodox starter Pokemon due to its tiny moveset and rarity, Ricks Combee happily flew out of the pokeball and fluttered her wings a bit, flying around the cabin in search of what Rick could only assume is honey. It seemed that since being captured, Combee still can’t get over the fact it’s a honeybee, and the Pokemon searches for it at any opportune moment. Rick reached out and grabbed the bee Pokemon, turning it around to face the guide. “Belle, I’m going to need your help choosing a suitable location to start our travel. I’ve narrowed the choices down to three, and decided that you probably deserved a say in it too.”

“Bee, Com” The three heads of Belle replied as they stared down at the pages bookmarked by Rick. They glanced quickly at each of the locations, and then pointed with both wings at the Desert, flying around and chirping happily. Rick nodded, and although shocked at the Combees quick decision, he put his guide away in his bag, as well as the comb from earlier. Tidying his desk quickly, and putting all his possessions away, he left his room for what he assumed would be the last time, opening the cabin door and making his way back up to the top floor of the boat. Looking out from the front of the boat, Rick saw that they still had two or three hours to go, so sat down on the floor and began to play with his starter Pokemon to make the journey fly by.

Eventually, the boat Rick was sitting on docked in Fizzytopia, and Rick stepped out onto the shores of adventure…or something like that. Looking around the area, he looked for any signposts at all regarding where the Desert existed. Returning Belle to her pokeball, Rick walked towards what appeared to be an information desk and began to speak to the woman that was sitting behind it. After a fairly short conversation, Rick gathered he wasn’t able to get any form of train to the area, and in fact, he would only be able to get there via walking or getting a taxi driver willing to go that far. Sighing, he walked towards the main road, and began to walk down it slowly, heading towards the place suggested by the book.

*

Rick stared slowly towards the horizon, his eyes attempting to see any sort of shapes or buildings in the distance. He saw none. Managing to reach the desert had been an easy task in the end compared to the actual journey across the desert. He’d been walking for a few days through the sands now, attempting to find some form of shelter in the huge empty wasteland. Grabbing his pokeball, he grabbed the ball attached to his belt, and pulled it out in front of him. Tapping the center of the pokeball, his partner emerged into the air in front of him, flapping her wings happily as she enters the area. Rick smiled to his partner slowly.

“Well then, we’re here. What shall we do then?”
