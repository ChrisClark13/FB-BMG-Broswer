Not far from the mysterious Tree Grove is a giant building with bright lights and enticing music, advertising a place for trainers to relax and socialize with other trainers without the stress of having to watch over Pokemon and keep them out of trouble. Perhaps in direct reaction to the ban on trainers, or perhaps out of a potential business opportunity, the proprietors of this establishment have a strict no-Pokemon policy. It is rumored that the owner, a gentle but insistent young man in his late twenties, once owned a club that was Pokemon-friendly before a Swalot not only ate all the food he had, but ate the entire club, as well! Unfortunately, that particular Swalot just loved the taste of his last business, and proceeded to eat the next three establishments he built until he finally created this establishment. It is also rumored that the mere mention of Swalot sends the owner into panic-mode, so it is advised that you avoid discussion of that particular Pokemon.

The Trainer-Only Nightclub is the perfect place to dance the night away, and the DJ is open to nearly all music requests. Of course, if dancing is not your thing, why not sit down and enjoy some food and drinks at the appropriately-named Wallflower Pub that's taken residence directly inside the Nightclub? With a perfect view of the action on the dance floor as well as a lovely window to the beautiful forest outside, you can escape from the madness with a nice beer (or soda if underage) and a bite to eat, or simply fuel up before another round of dancing.

Isn't it time to take a break from those adorable little monsters of yours? Come visit us at the Trainer-Only Nightclub~!

The night club was packed full of trainers unwinding from their daily training. When Auric walked in he immediately felt out of place and was less than thrilled with the idea of dancing. He was already out of his element being here and felt naked without his cloak, training gear or his pokeballs. Looking around through the bright lights and scores of people he spotted the entrance to a pub attached to the nightclub. 'Perfect' he thought moving through the crowd. In truth he didn't want to come here tonight, but his team needed some down time from training and they'd earned it. So if he was going to have to spend some time away from his team, getting a hot meal and time to think would do just fine.

Once he had moved past the restrooms the crowd started to thin out and he found himself in the Wallflower Pub. A server quickly lead him to a booth in front of the window and brought him a menu. "My name is Kelly and I'll be your waitress this evening. Can I get you something to drink?"

"What do you have on tap?" Auric asked?

"Erm... This is my first day. I can't remember so I'll go ask." She stuttered nervously.

Auric shook his head. "It's ok, any light beer will be fine."

Kelly smiled and walked away stopping at a near by table. Auric looked over the menu thinking over his options for food. He really wasn't that fussy about his food. Normally he ate what was convenient to eat on the move. When his waitress came back he ordered the chicken club with fries and took a drink of his cold beer. Looking around Auric could see the pub was actually quite busy. Not as busy as the nightclub, but still pretty full. Turning he looked out the window which looked out over a near by forest. Lightning broke in the distance, a storm on the way. Taking another drink Auric reached into his back pocket and pulled out a little book and a pen. Setting it on the table he started flipping through the pages. The pages were full of scribbled notes and sketches. Most of it was about his journeys as a pokemon trainer. But towards the back of the book was a special section in which the pages were worn a little more than the others. A sketch of a family, and a home. Scribbled notes meant to help trigger his memory and provide leads to what happened.

He constantly went back to that night in his mind. It bothered him that he couldn't remember, that his mind had found a way to shield it from his memory. Everything he could remember, about his family, was now nothing more the pen marks on paper. He had no pictures of them, just his sketches. He used to stare at it every night when he was still a kid just to fall asleep. Eventually his team became his new family and the sketch of his parents and brother became fuel for his search. He was so focused on the pages he hardly noticed when his food came.

"Here we are! Hope you enjoy." Kelly said placing a bottle of ketchup on the table with his food.

"Thank you." Auric said closing the notebook.

Pushing the book to the side of his plate he took another drink of beer and glanced out the window at the storm that was coming in over the forest.
