As Coselle continued to massage Keith's shoulders and after speaking to him related to the evolution choices of his PokÃ©mon, she commented on how Okiku's name was in folklore.

But to be honest..." her eyes wandered away, looking at nothing in particular, "I don't know what I'm attracted to dark types, along with psychic types too." She paused for a moment, lost in thought. "...Maybe it's their strengths and weaknesses. Unlike some other types that are based on classical elements of nature and whatnot, it's not just fire burns grass, grass grows with water, yada yada yada. It's goes deeper than that."

"I'm one of those that believe that Psychic type's weaknesses are based on common fears, as in afraid of the dark, bugs, and ghosts. I would add though that these fears messes up with the mind, therefore weakening it, and since Psychic types' strength is based on the mind... well all falls down like a house of cards. It makes you wonder if those fears are actually set in everyone's mind by default, just since Psychics are more attuned to their brain, those fears are either multiplied or affects their very being. As for their strengths, well I find it obvious with fighting: mind over matter, brain better than brawn, and the fact its the brain that coordinates all movements of a fighter, so without control... all those muscles turns into mush." Okiku giggled a bit sadistically at the visualization she had, but reverts her face to a puzzled expression. " But poison? That one actually stumps me. I heard theories about how Psychics can rearrange the molecules structure of poison, therefore rendering said poison no longer effective, but I don't see how psychic powers can go that far... then again, I heard of stories of people turning into dolls... but then again, it's possible it was just a mind transfer, not an actual transformation." Her eyes were still squinted in confusion, but widened all a sudden. "Hey Keith, you being a poison type specialist, do you got any ideas about that? It's ok if it's not correct, it might give me more insight on this," she smiled at the possibility of learning something interesting on her favorite type, or at least maybe get an 'eureka' moment.

"Anyways... Dark types. Strong against psychic and ghost types, weak against fighting and bug types. But you already know that," Okiku teasingly pulled her tongue out. "But... why? Well, we already have its strength against psychic covered, but what about ghosts? ...I think the answer is within what 'dark' really represents: void of light, nothingness. That interpretation applies to mind-oriented type, since that void of thoughts or knowledge would drive anyone crazy. But... ghosts, stuck in the afterlife here in the material world... to what purpose? A lot of them are staying in this plane to either send some kind of message to someone, usually along the lines that they have not forgotten them... But what if whoever the ghosts were in their past life were completely forgotten by the living, lost in the void ruled by darkness? their message would be for nothing, or cannot tell they have not forgotten others if said others forgot them, stuck to lament whatever problems it had but with nobody to listen... Hence removing their purpose of staying in the plane of existence. ...Anyhow, the whole 'void' thing could explain dark types' weaknesses too... at least for fighting types. Being mostly hard-headed and brave PokÃ©mon, they're not afraid of them... As for the weakness of bugs though, that's another thing I'll need to figure out, and would like to eventually find out," she laughed.

"Anyhow..." Okiku finally noticed she went off rambling again, this time way more than she did with Keith earlier, she started to turn crimson red yet again and nervously took a sip of her drink. "Sorry to go all out like this..."

She quickly glanced at her watch, and noticed it was starting to get close to Christmas day. She would've gotten to wear her fluffy Christmas hat she brought in her backpack, but she didn't feel secure enough to wear it in front of everyone.
