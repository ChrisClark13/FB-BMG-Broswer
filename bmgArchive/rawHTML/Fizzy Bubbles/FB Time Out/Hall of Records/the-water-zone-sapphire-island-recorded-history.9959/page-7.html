His Shiftry burst forward instantly, infuriated by the turquoise crocodile now and eager to prove himself. He held his arms high above his head as a crackling green aura started to surround his body, then roared and unleashed the mystic natural power towards the Totodile who stood transfixed. At first nothing happened, but Sasuke felt the ground beneath him begin to rumble, and the swamp waters started to rise. Augmenting into a huge pillar, the muddy water surged towards the crocodile pokemon, the spiralling column of water engulfing her and spinning her about helplessly. ”Saru that’s enough!” shouted Sasuke, sensing the anger in the attack, and feeling a twinge of mercy for the Totodile. His Shiftry shrugged and suddenly the wave crashed to the floor, dropping the Totodile from a great height onto her back, where she lay in the mud. Sasuke took a Premier Ball from his waist and stepped forward, ready to make the capture, but suddenly the crocodile pokemon stood back up, panting heavily but ready to battle on. She really is strong, Sasuke thought to himself, admiring her courage and strength. Though some might call it stupidity, she wasn’t going to give in if she could help it, and stepped forward to attack. Saru sneered and glared at the Totodile as his eyes started to glow white, the intensity grew and soon the light was blinding her, as she frantically fired off a few Water Gun attacks which missed their target by a long way. Feeling pity on her Saru launched himself forward, and cut the Totodile across the torso as he rushed past. She gasped as a splash of blood burst from the wound, splattering the ground red before she fell forward onto it, knocked out cold.
Ino covered her eyes with her ears and started to tremble, and Sasuke stepped forward slowly. He held up the Premier Ball which was already in his hand towards the fainted pokemon and opened it, and with a flash of red drew her inside. It struggled violently in his hand as he stood there with his arm outheld, but after about thirty seconds began to subside. With literally no energy left to resist, Totodile succumbed, and the pokeball stopped moving. He respectfully clipped it to his belt and smiled, but Ino whimpered from on his shoulder.
Sasuke stepped towards Saru who was stood with his eyes closed, panting for breath after the victory, but just then the skies ominously began to turn grey. At first a few specks of rain began to fall, but then the heavens opened and a torrent of water began to pour onto the swamp, which was already difficult to navigate. Sasuke cursed his luck and withdrew his Shiftry, thanking him for winning the battle as he did so, then cradled Ino in his arms. She lifted her ears as a makeshift umbrella to block the rain, as Sasuke began to run towards cover. He hadn’t got very far though when a huge wave rose up infront of him, blocking his path. He turned to the side but it happened again, and he gulped, having no idea what was keeping him trapped. The entire swamp waters, which were rising rapidly due to the rain, began to churn once more.
Hearing a noise he span round to see two huge scythes lunge out of the swamp and impale themselves into the bank of the swamp. With a roar a shadowy creature pulled itself out of the murky waters. Sasuke struggled to see what exactly it was, he desperately tried to wipe some water from his eyes and make out the figure but it was no use. The creature tore it’s scythes from out of the mud and crossed them above him in the air, then roared to the skies. Ino was shaking terribly and Sasuke took a step backwards, but the creature moved towards him. As it got closer he could make out the features more clearly. A flat, wide, brown head, long bony legs, a hard armoured body and the beady eyes of a long-thought to be extinct pokemon – Kabutops. Sasuke gasped and began to backpeddle more urgently but the creature began to murmur. At first Sasuke could only hear random noises but in his head they began to untangle, forming words he could clearly understand.
’The flow of peace has been disturbed .. new successor will be me .. for capturing her .. your punishment will be death.’​
Sasuke barely had time to comprehend what was happening before the fossil pokemon crouched down then sprang majestically through the air, landing with a thud only feet infront of him. From such a short distance Sasuke could see every minute detail of this ferocious pokemon. It’s scythes looked razor sharp and it’s hard armoured body looked impenetrable. Struggling to think what to do a pokeball at his belt began to tremble. Looking down it was a white Premier Ball, but not that of his newly captured Totodile. The ball sprang open of its own accord and stood in the pouring rain before him was his Mankey. ”What are you doing?” Sasuke asked in shock, ”this is no time to be macho.” His pokemon turned around and looked him in the eye, then nodded determinedly and faced Kabutops, who seemed amused by the display of foolishness and flashed his scythes menacingly.
Suddenly Sasuke gasped as his Mankey began to glow a fierce white. Against the murky swamp the light was almost blinding and Kabutops shielded his eyes as the radiance grew even stronger still. As it faded Sasuke turned back to where his Mankey had stood, to see a rabid Primeape in his place. He smiled and looked at his old friend who turned to him and nodded again, Sasuke nodded back resolutely, ”OK Enma,” he said, ”let’s do this.” His Primeape pounded his two huge fists together and faced Kabutops, who seemed more intrigued by the challenge now, and stepped forward to battle. Enma was snorting heavily and twitching from side to side, eager to fight against this worthy opponent. ”NOW focus your energy and attack with fury swipes,” Sasuke shouted through the rain, ”If he comes at you with those scythes give him a low kick.” His Primeape snarled and beat his chest as Sasuke watched with interest, while the rain continued to pour down around them and the two pokemon went to battle.
