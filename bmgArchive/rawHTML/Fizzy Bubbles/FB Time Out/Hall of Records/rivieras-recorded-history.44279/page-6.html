Rayne watched, mesmerized, by the utter carnage that their little crew of pokemon were creating. Powerful winds rushed past him on the ground, sending his shoulder length hair to fly aimlessly. Soon gigantic fire blasts, followed by a shower of rocks, and a devastating ice cold blizzard filled the night sky ... yet the Duskull would not cease. They fell in swarms, dropping to the ground like lead balloons, but they came washing over the castle walls in twice the number. From a far it most probably looked like an ominous black wave was endlessly flowing from the castle walls. It was only a matter of time before they were engulfed by this mass.

Rayne stood in place, mouth agape, trying to coordinate another set of attacks for his pokemon. At the same time he was desperately looking for an escape route.

<... This is ridiculous Rayne! ...> Drought beamed telepathically. If Rayne couldn't hear the anger in her voice, he most definitely felt it; a sharp pain sprang into existence right above his eyes, a kind he had never experienced before.

<... I know. I think ...> Rayne stopped short of finishing his thought. Above the never ending squeals of the Duskull he picked out the sound of something new. A very steady beat of new cries rang throughout. Unlike the disorderly Duskull, these cries were in perfect sync, beating at precise time intervals, loud and unwavering, steady and menacing.

"Clops. Clops. Clops." Rayne looked up to see a zone of clearing around two Dusclops; the alpha dogs have come out to play it seems. The Duskull shrieking subsided slightly, and the thick throng of inexperienced ghosts seemingly cleared the path for their superiors. The sight of these apparitions descending was hypnotising, something about the wave-like swaying of their hands made it impossible for Rayne to rip his gaze away.

<... What? Rayne. What do you think? ...> Drought asked urgently, but Rayne couldn’t be bothered to answer her at this time. A wide laser beam shot out from their one eye, like a floodlight that illuminated both Rayne and Pepper.

“What?! No, you fools! Look away.” But it was much too late. A feeling of sorrow and despair raced through Rayne, before settling down. The noise of the battle around them disappeared, even Drought’s mental barrages could not be heard; they were trapped by Mean Look. Rayne looked over at Pepper who came to the exact same realisation. Though the two could not communicate, Pepper gave him a thumbs up, to which Rayne nodded in response.

“Raaaah!” There was that blood numbing roar of Andromeda, presumably the pokemon the Dusclops chose as its opponent. Rayne smiled slightly, glad that his ground based pokemon were not chosen; this was a level playing field for his Salamence. Outside the Mean Look bubble Minerva and Drought continued their bombardment of the Duskull. Rayne knew their training would keep them safe for the time being, but he didn’t know for how long they could hold up.

“Andromeda, let’s make these ghosts regret singling you out. Start out with a quick Dragon Dance, followed by an Aqua Tail. Give them a glimpse of your Scary Face to finish up.”

Despite this Andromeda swoops in, furious that she's left to an effective crawl by her opponents, and with the force of a river torrent she slams an Aqua Tail at her specified enemy. Smirking she turns to see an expected look of pain, but the sight of Dusclops' steely gaze perturbs her slightly. The instinct is one well expressed as this particular Dusclops slams its hands to the floor, and with a cry Andromeda crashes to the ground as the both of you grasp your knees, struggling to maintain posture as Dusclops curses the relevant area with its natural manipulative powers over Gravity. A creepy laugh emerges from the other Dusclops as it raises its hands, and Rayne recognises the summoned debris: a repeat of the Rock Slide that Minerva used earlier. As Drapion strugglingly regains its footing Martéo screeches a storm above it, and already pushed to limits by the Taillow that simply won't stay down Drapion scowls, swinging its large claws in attempt to Knock Martéo Off her charted flight plans. Having a diversion mounted by his colleague, Chroma boldly stomps the ground, and as the energy is transferred through the dirt you see several Rock Tombs rise up, each one catching a foot of the Dark scorpion. Without focus on its lower regions Drapion is quickly caught in the stony traps, and when it finally notices its legs are well and truly caught.

"Now, Chroma, assist Martéo!" Even as the draconic winds leave her feathers Chroma is ready to rumble, breathing Ominous Winds to join those from Martéo that swirl with accelerating velocity. Soon enough a tornado a good bit taller than the ogre arachnid begins to thunder towards Drapion, who can only continue struggling in vain as a response. Not quite finished, Martéo flaps an Icy Wind just as the air funnel strikes, combining all three elemental zephyrs in a combination that enshrouds Drapion entirely. A horrible holler is barely heard from Drapion as the triple winds drown out its noise in the midst of damage, and eventually it fades as the attacks continue. By the time the tornado dissipates completely Drapion is left wheezing inaudibly on the ground -- whether from exhaustion or injury, you can tell that it no longer has the capability to fight on. Level-up for Martéo.

"We apologise, Drapion. That you attacked us was no sole fault of your own... I place this in hopes of your swift recovery." Opening his satchel Yuanli moves forward, and you see him place a Sitrus Berry as an offering for the fallen Pokémon. "Now, it would be quite vulnerable if Drapion was left here weakened. Chroma, would you mind providing some cover for Drapion?"

"Sabl." A light nod is observed from Chroma before he raises his little hands, and instantly you see a set of Light Screens surround Drapion, forming a chamber that slowly fades out of view, taking Drapion with it. "Given that Sableye naturally have affinities for crystal, they also possess some rudimentary powers -- this includes the refraction of light at will," explains the sage. "Now, we must continue. We cannot say for certain Drapion's route will be safe for us now that it's collapsed from our duelling."

The walk through the ghost town -- while no more comforting than before, now with the active threat of being attacked -- remains as serene as ever, under the guidance of the venerable Yuanli and Chroma, who continues to scamper about, using his abilities to locate a possible way underground. As calm settles over your senses, the scene of death no longer seems to disturb as much; almost fascinating you, as you wonder what must have happened here. What stories did this abandoned settlement have to tell?

"Will, I think we now have a possible option." A quip from the old man halts your footsteps, and peering ahead of Yuanli you spot a well in a cleared bit of town. "Presumably this was where a fraction of the water supply, if not its whole, must have originated. If what the researchers told me was true..." You see Yuanli go for his bag once again, this time producing what looks like a clear, flaky film. "In general insect Pokémon do not enjoy a long life span. Much as it is unfortunate, some leave behind invaluable legacies -- in particular, Venomoth wings lose their toxicity after the original Pokémon... moves on, shall we say. You know from the PokéDex that the wings of a Venomoth may vary in shade dependent on the poison they carry," says Yuanli. "The dried wing of a Venomoth can be used to determine the presence of latent poisons through these colour variations, in the same way that scientists would perform chromatography -- that is, to identify the substances that make up a single mixture. And now..." As Yuanli holds the wing over the well, you observe that the translucent object starts to darken, turning a sickly greenish-black after half a minute's passing.

"I was told that the cemetery of our objectives is laden with a variety of plants, none particularly favourable to general health. They release spores that can poison quite severely after overexposure, although it would take at least a few days by my reckoning. The Venomoth wing proves it, though -- we're right above the graveyard's outskirts, at least. The sensitivity of Pokémon to their environment brings vast knowledge with each experience... although, this does present us a bit of a problem now," muses the elder. "How might we descend this structure? The well should be able to accomodate us if we went down at the same time, but by what means can we do so? I must admit to not having rope in my inventory, and even that might not be enough. Might we have to call on the help of another Pokémon?"

What do you think? It doesn't look like Yuanli has the resources to go past this obstacle, so it's up to you now...

Having caught the Tangela in a bind with the poisoned status, you call desperately for Bellatrix to tend her efforts to you instead, gaining you a buzz in the affirmative. Calling on her previous experience with gathering honey Bellatrix releases a pulse of Sweet Scent in a bid to becalm the raging Tangela, wafting a cloud of aroma over to the screeching bunch. It takes a while before the mist appears to have any effect, but straining your ears you notice that the Tangela's babbles have started to quieten down in volume, almost to a troubled brooding as the Tangela presumably try to get the Toxic off each other. You hear a brief buzzing before Bellatrix's worried gaze meets yours, and relying on the night vision most insect Pokémon possess she carefully tears the knots apart with her Fury Swipes, barely leaving any scratches at all where her claws meet your skin. Grasping your hand in hers Bellatrix buzzes you up to a standing position before hurriedly pulling you along the path, sidestepping the muddled group of Vine Pokémon, dashing across the stone bridge ahead without them so much as noticing.

By the time Bellatrix stops for you to catch your breath and sufficient time passes by, you manage to take a proper look at your surroundings -- a decision well made, you find, as the view is more breathtaking than you thought. Under the light of the full moon, the shadows cast off the pillars and beams resemble stalwart guards keeping the peace in the abandoned castle, while the dark leaves of shrubs reflect the illumination in a sheen almost soft enough to lie upon. Even in the darkness of night the garden bears a distinct aura of well-maintenance: an observation you find odd considering how the area must have been long abandoned by humans.

"Poli?" The sound of wild Pokémon, more inquisitive than demanding, turns your attention to one of the pillars lining the tiled floor, and a brief scan yields a Poliwag peering out from behind one of the stone structures. The single tadpole is soon joined by another, then another, then several of the roly-poly Water-types, all looking at you in curiosity and -- perhaps a hint of fear? You can't be certain as a gloved hand, then two, start appearing as a couple of Poliwhirl show themselves. At least this group of Pokémon don't seem intent on attacking...

Rick watched with hopeful eyes as Bellatrix buzzed in response to Ricks cry, the bug Pokemon obviously willing to turn her attention to help her trainer rather than simply letting him be throttled by the sheer amount of vines that spread through the area. The tactic was one he knew would work, having used it when trying to calm down Pokemon in his first adventure in the world of Fizzy Bubbles, when he had to face off against a extremely large criminal organization, and yet he somehow won. The organization had retreated, but Rick expected that he hadn’t heard the last of them. Since that day he had always watched his back, always made sure to carry his pokeball, but if he was being completely and utterly bested by a group of overgrown weeds, what chance did he have against the might of an entire criminal organization, which had probably rebuilt itself already. It had been a few months since the day he met Jirachi, and the experience was still vivid in his head; a legendary Pokemon! Bellatrix moved onto her orders, and Rick snapped back into reality, the aroma of a Sweet Scent floating through the area, and apparently it was working at least slightly, the Tangela calming down considerably compared to what they were like prior to this event. A brief buzzing emerges from Bellatrix lips as she slowly began to cut away the knots that bound Rick together, barely leaving any scratches where she ripped away the knots from his body. Gripping onto Ricks hand, a large comforting sigh spreading out of Ricks mouth without him even realizing it, she pulled him up to a standing position, and he couldn’t help but feel great gratitude towards his Starter Pokemon. Hurriedly, Bellatrix pulled Rick across the area, and Rick tried his best to help her do so, the pair sidestepping around the vine creatures and rushing over a cold stone bridge. Nervously looking down at his clothes, Rick noticed a large amount of tears had been cut through his clothes. Brilliant. Next time he was in a town, he’d have to buy himself a completely new set of clothes. It was about time he got a makeover anyway, physically and mentally. Sure, adventuring was fun, but he didn’t really have a goal in life, going anyway the wind blows or something similar to that. As Rick took a proper look at his surroundings, he found that he was nearly knocked of his feet by how beautiful it was. Under the light of the full moon, the shadows covered the pillars and guards, but gave them a far more mysterious look under the light of the moon. Amazing, the dark leaves seemed to be even more beautiful that the pillars and statues, their beauty in the moonlight almost overthrowing Rick. Considering the area has supposedly been uninhabited for years, Rick couldn’t help but feel slightly mythed at quite how well kept it looked, but Rick wasn’t one to question the ways in which natures beauty decided to work.

“Poli?” a sound echoed from nearby, and Rick almost couldn’t believe what he was hearing. If it was truly a Poliwag, Rick would be more than ecstatic, the Pokemon being one of his favorites behind Slakoth. Well, really, all of Poliwags line were considered favorites rather than just the young one, and he couldn’t believe his luck. Maybe he’d get something more from his journey, something he didn’t realize that there was even the possibility of; finding a new team member. Turning towards the pillar which the cry seemed to come from, Rick noticed not one but several of them, all looking up at Rick not in fear but curiosity. He smiled down at the Pokemon, but tried to keep himself fairly unintimidating. He didn’t want another Tangela situation. Apparently, it wasn’t just Poliwag either, and Rick almost let out a gasp as he saw the Poliwhirl as well. He knew this side of him was slightly odd, but he couldn’t hide his enthusiasm as he looked at the Pokemon. Unsure as to what to do next, he gripped one of his team members Pokeballs, Duskull, just in case of a fight, and lowered himself to one knee. Beckoning towards one of the Poliwags, he hoped not to scare them, but just in case he put his Duskulls Pokeball in his shirt pocket, still able to be reached quickly in the chance of a crisis. He only hoped that the group would see he was trying to make a friendly gesture, and not trying to harm them in any way shape or form. Bruises from the Tangela attack still showed on his body, and frankly, he refused to add to that collection.

Any doubts Will might have harbored concerning Yuanli’s motives had been effectively put to rest by the oldster’s gracious actions. Marveling at the man’s compassion toward Pokémon, Will followed him and the lively Sableye through the deserted streets of town.

The last time he had seen a town in such a forlorn state was when he had been treasure hunting in the ruins of Lacuna. While there, he had somehow been magicked into the past and thrust into the middle of the civil conflict that had ultimately led to that society’s collapse. Commissioned by Arceus to put a stop to the violence, Will and his team undertook the task of unlocking the keys that were needed to reunite the fragmented nation and save Lacuna from cataclysmic destruction. In the process, the boy had rewritten history, and discovered that the feud had been fuelled by Darkrai and Cressalia’s longstanding rivalry. Will knew there was simply no way of predicting just what secrets this eerie settlement might hide.

After a while Yuanli, Chroma, and Will stopped before a well in an open area of town. Yuanli tested the toxicity of the water using a form of Pokémon chromatography and found that the levels of poisonous material indicated the trio had indeed chanced upon the entrance to the sought-after Cemetery. He explained that it lay beneath the surface of the town, but admitted he was at a loss as to how they should proceed further. Turning to Will, he suggested enlisting the help of a Pokemon to aide in their descent.

Will nodded in response to Yuanli’s plan, reached into his messenger bag, and quickly produced a shiny steel Poké Ball. Holding the silver capture capsule at arm’s length, he pressed the round trigger switch and a brilliant scarlet light appeared, which morphed itself into the form of a slender mystic before fading, revealing the elegant Pokémon, Medicham.

Released from the cramped confines of his containment orb, the Medicham Medéo made an exaggerated show of reaching over to touch his toes, running in place, and doing jumping jacks. Unimpressed by this ostentatious display, Will sweatdropped and put on a slightly embarrassed grin before shrugging sheepishly in Yuanli’s direction. Turning back to Medéo, who had only just completed his elaborate stretching routine, Will issued his instructions. “Right then, mate. Enough of that. We need your help. There’s a cemetery buried somewhere beneath us and we’re looking for a way in. I think you just might be able to safely Teleport all of us down this old well.”

Southwest of Elendia lies a forest, within which lies the ancient ruins of Nelde -- home of the Binon royal family, who died out in the last few centuries. A slender flutter of his wings had him flipping in midair, now flying backwards in front of you, a sight that could only mean the reappearance of the resilient Ariados. The force of the wind sent every loose particle in the immediate vicinity hurtling in the opposite direction of your sprint. The soil from the forest floor could not keep its grip on the ancient roots, and it too spiralled into the Gligar’s distractive technique. The displeased screech of the spider informed you of the successful attack, but before the feeling of safety could really sink in you notice darts flying past you in a scattered pattern. To your relief you don’t feel any of the poisonous darts hit you; the Ariados was shooting blindly through the opaque veil of particles.

So as to not give it another shot you pick up your pace further, tugging at the poisonous darts in the Combee’s back. Lacrimus was once again flying in front of you now, leading you in the least obstructed path. Although you could not hear the spider pokemon anymore, you had a feeling that the Ariados would not give up that easily. The look in its eyes as it watched its soon-to-be meal escape right under its “nose”, well, that was enough to let you know that this Ariados would probably chase you to the other end of the forest before quitting the chase. Having no other choice you run, run as fast as your legs would let you.

As fast as your legs would let you … why was your left leg suddenly feeling a bit heavier? Not a bit, actually, it felt significantly heavier. Now that you noticed it, it actually felt as if your whole body was heavy. Every second the air around you thickened. Now it felt like you were running through chest deep water, and now molasses. What was going on? Startled you look down at your left leg, the source of this odd sensation. It didn’t take long to notice the suddenly all too visible dart sticking out of your left hamstring. Now it felt as if you’re running through sand, impossibly slow. The realisation hit you harder than freight train … the poison that soaked the dart set into action immediately; both a potent localized anaesthetic and an effective neurotoxin. Before you could really contemplate the consequences of …

Floor …

Darkness …

Now it felt like you were buried in concrete. Somehow you manage to force your eyelids open, and the sight that awaited you took several agonizing moments to reach your brain. You were in a dark room, that much you could tell, but no detail was taking shape. Maybe the poison hadn’t worn off yet. The recognition of a sharp pain in your left hamstring disproved that theory. Your attempt at wincing was not much better than your previous eye-opening ordeal. By the time your eyelid muscles responded to the automatic pain reflex, the pain was pretty much gone. It was as if someone had taken your nerves while you were blacked out, and replaced them with wires that raced around the whole world before they ended at their target. If opening and closing your eyes took seconds, you didn’t want to think how difficult walking would be.

“Ah … so you’re finally up.” A raspy, female voice cut your contemplating off. Your brain had no problem realising this voice grated your very nerves. Why did your brain have no problem integrating her voice? You were suddenly aware that this woman was actually hovering right over your face, blocking the light source from you. Goose bumps ran down your arm, just now reacting to her voice. You could feel your muscles moving in response to the gag reflex, but before the natural motion of things could dish out its product; your muscles stopped moving, and then relaxed.

“I wouldn’t move … cough … if I were you … baha … you may have beat Sheila’s poison … but … hak … the poison running through your veins now … haghaghak … is much more deadly.” She laughed a disgusting, blood curdling laugh; her lungs so full of mucous you’d think she would have drowned a long time ago. Again you feel like vomiting, but again your muscles tense and then relax. “Don’t even try child … now tell me … hak … I would love to know … bahag … before I end your life, how you managed to steal the Queen Bee … who sent you here child … haha … if you are a child at all.” Your jaw muscles and tongue are suddenly much more solid feeling than the rest of your body, the neural connections inhibited no longer. Was this hag controlling your body? Was she allowing you to speak? Whatever it was, you were granted the ability to speak, yet full body movement still eluded you. Better make the best of it. Apparently your life depended on it.

What will be your response?

Stark: Your trek across the cliff path continued to be just as eventful as before; that is, not that eventful at all. The sound of your boots meeting the ground broke the silence of the night twice every second, and while at first the sound bugs you, soon it fades once more into the periphery of your mind. The castle was still a ways away, by the looks of it, another thirty minutes or so at this pace would have you at the bridge. What you begin to notice is that this walk is far too quiet … the sound of your steps breaks the silence again, and you half curse at noticing that again. But, the silence around cannot be ignored; it’s dead quiet. The sea that spreads outwards beneath you is very calm tonight, no waves are hammering up the cliff’s face. There’s no wind, no pokemon, nothing … aside from your own footsteps that is. Curious, you scan all around you for anything living, and finally you spot three shadows cut across the abnormally large moon. Two Murkrow and a Honchkrow you identify quickly. They are much too far to hear however.

Sure the silence was curious, but you don’t feel terrified; you know you’re not alone on this mysterious island. Whether the company you know is here, is friendly or not, is an entirely different concern. Suddenly, another shadow cuts across the night sky. This time it was much closer. The shape flutters overhead with amazing speed, circles around, before diving straight at you with incredible velocity. Instinctively you reach for a pokeball, but before your hand has the chance to unclip a pokeball the shape changes directions once more, disappearing above the cliff with a shriek of terror. Not entirely sure what to make of the exchange, you take a few hesitant steps forward. Realising the flying beast is not coming back for you; you walk on, occasionally stealing glancing above you for another kamikaze dive.

Several more minutes pass uneventfully and now you could definitely feel an incline in the slope of the path, signalling the last stretch of the Wilheim Path. From farther down the path you could tell that once the slope steepened a bit, it curled around a bend where you could no longer see the path, before emerging once more in your line of sight right at the foot of the bridge that would lead you to the castle. Knowing your goal is now less than ten minutes away you pick up your pace slightly, reaching that bend that you had spotted earlier. You take the turn, not thinking much of the blind corner. No one could have been prepared for what waited for you around the corner. It wasn’t the sudden appearance of another human on the path that startled you, but rather who that person was. You were looking straight into your own eyes; the man in front of you was you!

“You came?! This … this can’t be … this is just like before. Impossible.” The man … you … babbled. There was no mistaking it. The man’s voice was yours, a little hoarse and tired, as if you’d aged a bit, but definitely your voice. “I only … why? I only stopped for a second … this is just like before … just like.” His voice was frantic, scared, crazed. You were speechless. The man was wearing exactly what you had on at the moment, although his garb was tattered and covered in blood. The same red coat, every article of clothing the same, and yet this man was not you; this man was off. He looked like he was insane. His eyes darted incredulously from place to place, stopping on your awe-stricken face every few moments. The man’s breathing quickened, he started mumbling to himself, pulling at his hair (he was missing several chunks; apparently he pulled hard) and fidgeting.

“No … no … haha … ha … no … impossible … unbelievable … just like before.” He was definitely insane, and being around him made you uncomfortable. “You … I mean me … me in the past … no not in the past … this is the present … listen … make all the right choices … don’t make my mistakes … you’ll end up where I am …” After giving you his warning, the man pushed you to the side and ran for it; the opposite way you were heading. To your horror you watch as the dark shape from before hurtles downwards from the sky, slamming hard into the man’s body. You reach out to him, but he was past the point of no return. He tried to catch his balance on the edge of the path, but lost that battle. The sound of his body shattering onto a protruding rock preceded the splash by a fraction of a second. He was gone. Suddenly you feel really woozy, and you lean against the cliff face for support. As you slide down to take a seat your head naturally cocks back and for a brief second you think you can see a face looking down at you from above the lip. You blink once, and the face is gone; must have imagined it. First the flying beast, now this? What was wrong with this island?

It is only then that you notice that the rock face across from you had eyes; eyes that were staring directly at you with evident anger. The eyes blink and you flinch, not really expecting it. The eyes open up once again, but now they were pulling out of the rock, gaining three dimensional shape. The dark purple eye sockets followed, then the floating hands, finally the full body of a majorly pissed off Haunter hovered above you.

“Haunter!” It accuses sharply, pointing one of its crooked fingers at you. You woke this Haunter from its slumber and it is not at all happy to see you.

How will you react?

Two choices lie around you for the moment, and now that Yuanli has placed the decision in your hands, what will you do?
