CyberBlastoise: With Gold at your side, you march confidently into the Wiese Forest. The forest itself seems to exude a sense of peace and tranquillity. Rays of emerald-hued light shine through the thin canopy, casting light on the curious exotic flora that grows here. You enjoy a quiet walk through the twisting, muddy paths, noticing the mushrooms of all sizes and shapes growing between the roots of large trees. You ponder eating one, but the knowledge that some of them will kill or paralyse you is not a comforting one; instead, you opt to walk right on by. Gold is captivated by the unfamiliar new environment and soon, after running around getting stuck into everything, he comes back to you with a bouquet of flowers (looking at it, you notice some mushrooms in the mix too...).

"Ray! Luxray!"

A Thunderbolt cuts through the air, coming dangerously close to your head. Shocked (not literally, to your relief), you whirl around. In front of you is a Luxray, crouching low and Sparking with electricity. You barely manage to get out of the way as it leaps at your head... or rather, the Spinarak that was above your head. The electric Pokémon lands nimbly on the ground and stares up at you with shining yellow eyes. You eye the Spinarak lying prostrate on the ground; its fangs glisten with poison. Saying the Luxray saved your life is a bit of a stretch, but had the Spinarak gone unnoticed, the consequences wouldn't be good.

The Luxray trots arrogantly towards back towards another person- your real savior, if he could be called that. Immediately, you notice several things about him. Firstly, he looks to be around eleven. You were saved by someone nine years younger than you are, you think to yourself. The way he stands is odd, like he isn't sure of himself in the way he walks. The most perplexing thing of all, however, is that he has the garb of a Pokémon trainer but all six of the balls on his belt are empty. You don't see any other Pokémon anywhere in the vicinity except the Luxray, which is now pawing the ground and growling softly at its trainer, turning occasionally towards a small gap in the line of trees. The boy doesn't seem aggressive (in fact he's smiling warmly at you again) but the oddities in his manner, his Luxray, and the issue of his Pokéballs has you on edge. He hasn't said a single word to you yet. Instead, he is gesturing urgently to the gap in the trees - a side path, you realise - and walking towards it. He wants you to follow, you realise...

Tetyth Lake - please reply in aqua

Treepandaone: Hera languidly Surfs across Tetyth Lake while you sit on the her back. The Milotic seems joyous and relaxed as she basks in her natural environment, a gentle aura of calm surrounding her. Meanwhile, as you ponder Tetyth City's fables, you take in the sights. It truly is a spectacular sight. The water is a stunning aquamarine and the lake itself stretches vastly in every direction. At the center is the ancient, long-gone civilisation known as Tetyth City, the oddly geometric architecture of the drowned city pristine and melancholy in its abandon.

All is quiet on Tetyth Lake- almost unnaturally so. The Pokédex warned you of savages and beasts lurking in the depths of this lake- where are they? The water is calm and you can't see a single other soul in any direction. Those thoughts are quickly dispelled, however, when a guttural roar echoes through the air. Hera shrieks, looking around wildly. There's nothing there. The air distorts slightly, and you catch snippets of an argument.

<This prey is mine! I ... meal. Don't you dare...>

<...challenge me? I'm the king of ... back off. You...>

You hear raised voices, but you can't see anything. As the dispute escalates, the air distorts even more. With horror, you realise that there are a set of ethereal blue claws mere inches from your throat. Hera, still panicking, takes the initiative and blasts a Water Pulse at the distorted figure. The figure staggers back with a splash, and the illusion dissipates. Two yellow eyes glare balefully up at you. As if this day could get any worse, the Gyarados looming over the three of you doesn't look happy at all.

<You-!> snarls the Golduck, clawing at Hera. The Milotic shrieks and skirts away before the Gyarados' Leer stops her in her tracks. Suddenly, you're uncomfortably aware of your vulnerability out here on the lake. There's no one except Hera between you and the two predators- and she's currently scared stiff.

The Gyarados laughs. There isn't a trace of humour in its voice. <You were always worthless, Sila, but I never knew that even the simple task of securing a meal was beyond you. Kill them.>

The Golduck's eyes shift from Hera to you. It obviously feels mutinous about its leader but from the look in its eyes, it has no qualms about following these particular orders. You need to think fast before this situation gets out of hand...

Matthew's face turned from a usual colour of pink to white as he first heard the argument. Though not a soul was to be seen on the face of the river; he had read in his Pokédex that the wildlife wasn't friendly, at all. Gulping, he begun to sit upright to attention, scanning the water carefully with each twitch, almost frantically trying to find the source as they moved onto scarier topics.

<This prey is mine! I ... meal. Don't you dare...>

His eyes almost burst out of their socket, the conversation turned to meals, and though he wasn't certain it was him he was talking about, he was certain it was him they were talking about. It had to be, it was just his luck that two creatures of the deep fancied him as a light snack.

No! That was defiantly not going to happen. He hoped. The struggle to find the beasts was driving him crazy, his brain knew they were out there somewhere but his eyes did not. They played tricks on him, seeing slight shadows on the clear blue water when there wasn't any, flickers of something in the corner of his eyes. Shaking it off he pierced the sea water with his eyes, scanning harder as the conversation seemed to erupt with hostility.

<...challenge me? I'm the king of ... back off. You...>

The air got harder as his eyes began to shake with fury, his inability to see the creatures driving his to the point of anger. Erupting with rage he soon saw what he was looking for, though he dreaded what he saw. Three claws stood mere inches away from his throat, the sharp tingle of them so near to ripping his throat out.

Hera, being the protecting Pokémon she was, squeaked with surprise, pushing a Water Pulse attack into the figure sending it, and its claws, stumbling backwards; saving Matthew's neck for now. Peering down at the thing which had so nearly taken his life mere minutes into his adventure, Matthew was met with two piercing yellow eyes and a Gyarados looming over him.

Gulping loudly, Matthew tried to escape, Hera squirming its way through the water like a sea-serpent before getting stuck by a Leer, the deadly eyes of the Gyarados stopping the Milotic dead in her tracks. Looking around, Matthew realized in his haste to get to the Cathedral he had been completely oblivious to his surroundings, the lake baron from any life aside from his two predators and himself and Hera. Things were not looking good, Matthew's head whirring away as it tried to think of any possible escape, but none came. The Gyarados seemed to laugh in his face, the larger sea-serpent hinting toward the Golduck as it spoke.

<You were always worthless, Sila, but I never knew that even the simple task of securing a meal was beyond you. Kill them.>

Suddenly Matthew's brain connected, came into fruition as he began to panic. The Golduck's mutitious behavior had reason for celebration, but his plan certainly needed a straight face to get out of, and all the bargaining skills he could damn well muster.

“Shame you can't eat both of us for yourself” he said in a calm and collective manor, addressing the Golduck directly “That Gyarados will probably eat most of me, at best you'll get scraps from the bone. A shame as well because I taste mighty good”

His eyes dipped as he smirked at the Gyarados, “Y'know, my partner and I could have put him to sleep for you to kill...” he said, hinting down to his Milotic and its Hypnosis attack. “But you probably wouldn't want to be king of the sea anyway... You don't look like you want it enough.”

He couldn't help but forge an American twinge in his voice, the southern-american accent coming from his mouth was completely unintentional but the TV he had seen always pointed to the southerners as better bargainers.

In his mind, Plan B stood ready. His lips on standby to utter to words “Dragonpulse to Golduck and Charge Beam the Gyarados” if needed, though his plan was certainly better and stood more chance of keeping him alive, if only for a few minutes.

Originally a city in the centre of a lake, southeast from where Elendia is located, Tetyth was said to have been cursed in the distant past when the local inhabitants angered the gods with their extravagance and arrogance. Due to this the once beautiful city of splendour and prosperity was submerged within the waters, killing all the people in the city's limits and leaving behind the city's rich treasures and archives. In light of this, adventurers and researchers have made attempts to explore the sunken ruins, in order to discover more about the ancient lake city that has now become infested with Pokémon. However, not all investigative expeditions have been successful; many of the city's traps are still very active, and those who set foot on Tetyth's abandoned soil have returned with claims that the water has a mind of its own...
Tetyth Lake
With no port to cast off, Surfing on a Pokémon is the only other way to access Tetyth across the watery depths. Usually the waters seem calm, but it is rumoured that feral aquatic Pokémon have staked their territory in various bits of the lake, ready to attack any intruders...

“Land ahoy!” Wes grinned nonchalantly, sporting a wooden paddle which he co-ordinately oared against the calm lake. The sun’s warm bask washed against the seasoned trainer’s face, stimulating his senses and encouraging him to press on. The buoyant Cloysteris remained discreet and mysterious, her pearly visage hardly probing through the navy bivalve. Wes sat with either leg hanging over the Pokémon’s conical shell, his backpack securely fastened against one of the bivalve’s turrets. It was brimming with supplies he’d gotten from a quick skint in Elandia; a few medical supplies and enough food to last them the night. He firmly lifted his row from the water, the city of Tetyth barely jutted off the horizon of blue. They had a lot of ground to cover, but Wes revelled in the idea of spending this trek on water whilst breaking a rookie battler.

“So, how about we up our pace a bit?” Wes teased. Right on cue, the Cloyster dipped a few inches until its spherical head was completely submerged – The soles of Wes’s sandles skimming against the surf. The bivalve huffed, her shell propping open a few inches due to the increased pressure within her shell. Pulsating with energy she jetted the inhaled water from her rear, Wes gripping tight to each turret with the breakneck acceleration. Pressing on like a speedboat Wes opted to tuck the row back into his backpack. The wind blowing through his shaggy hair, Wes set sail for Tetyth City!
