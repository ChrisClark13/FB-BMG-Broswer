Heaven's Gate​

Crumbling Sanctum

Lonely Cubone
"Potions... they would help alleviate some of the base damage inflicted on his physical form, but the burning is a different story. My village used to specialise in metallurgy so I've some degree of experience where it comes to the effects of fire on metal," explains the girl. "Under normal circumstances Skarmory grows enough metallic feathers to replace its coat when damaged, but from my preliminary observations, it looks like even the hypodermic layers have been badly scorched. Suffice to say, it's not something a simple Burn Heal or Rawst Berry can alleviate." Pausing for a moment, she continues. "I suppose you're right. It won't help if we took shelter here; if we do come across her again we'll be in a far worse state than we were earlier. It's really all or nothing, isn't it?"

Another period of quietude passes, punctuated only as the woman warrior moves over with a Steel Ball in her hand, recapturing her trusted friend in a wave of red energy. For a moment she grasps the PokéBall in her hand, her eyes half-closed in sadness before she turns back at you. "Seeing that we'll be working together for a while, now... introductions would only be reasonable. ...Sharene. That's my name. And yours?" She nods gently as you give her your name. "We should go, as soon as we're all rested up and ready. Before she does decide to come looking for us."

***​

While the threat of Alycos' descendant appearing is an everpresent one, hours later after moving out from the dilapidated temple you find a far more pressing issue is navigation. With only an expanse of stones and metal to guide you by and little else in landmarks, you begin wondering if you're not travelling in circles -- albeit, up-and-down, back-and-forth circles upon rugged rock and chain.

"I'm afraid this is best we can do. Without Skarmory to give us a bird's eye view we won't be able to see the Remote Sanctuary from here," grunts Sharene, as she hefts you up on a larger rock platform. "...That's enough climbing for now. We need some degree of strength for evasive action if the situation calls for it, and we might need to set up camp for the... what?" Her hand separating from yours, Sharene takes a good look at your surroundings for the first time in a while, and starting to feel a sense of unease you turn about as well. Where Heaven's Gate had hues of red, you find the sky now tainted a strangely ominous shade of violet.

"This... it can't be!" Sharene whispers, clearly trembling and afraid this time. "I'd only heard of it in legends, but that sky, this darkness...! There's no mistaking it, we're -- aaaargh!"

As though an invisible force cleaved the flooring below you, you feel the now somewhat-familiar feeling of falling down once again; like a digestive biscuit the rock crumbles under your weight, leaving you and Sharene to fall to the next stony platform underneath. While not bonecrushing the bruising experience is hardly a pleasant one, and as the two of you pick yourselves up a sinister presence looms overhead. Like an unidentified flying object the statue-like creature hovers down at you, beholding you in apparent disdain with beady eyes as three smaller figurines follow suit, surrounding the sides of the large-nosed beast.

"Probopass," identifies Sharene. "And it looks angry enough to kill..."

What will you do now?


Nelde Ruins​

Wiese Forest

Excessive Spare Time

With Volante taken out of the picture you have little alternative but to release Torchlight for battle, and with a few instructions laid out the Cyndaquil eagerly jumps in. Out from his mouth a cloud of Smokescreen billows forth, cloaking the male Nuzleaf who gives a disgruntled cry at having his view obscured. "Now, Cormag! Follow with Icy Wind!" Seizing the chance, Duessel calls for another encore of his Murkrow's freezing weather, and cawing loudly Cormag sends another miniature snowstorm into the smoke cloud. For a while, nothing seems to happen, until several curved, focused pockets of air explode out of the fog. Instinctively you raise your arms, but even with the move's inaccuracy a shot of Razor Wind still succeeds in catching you by the gut -- not sharp enough to cut cloth or skin but strong enough to knock you over.

"Are you alright, Miss?" You have little chance to reply before Duessel hoists you by the wrist, bringing you back to a stand. With the swishing zephyrs separating the Smokescreen and Nuzleaf in full view, the three males charge in as one with their respective limbs raised. A Peck, Scratch and Tackle, carried over by the simultaneous force of three Pokémon, cleanly throws the leaf-topped Pokémon off his feet, sending him tumbling into the undergrowth's dirt. For a while he tries pushing himself upwards, but his wooden arms eventually collapse after a few moments' passing. Level up for Grinder.

"It's too late. We weren't fast enough!" At Duessel's grimace, you peer upwards only to immediately wish you hadn't. In the trees above you scores of Nuzleaf stand defiantly on the branches, glaring at you like Swellow on Wurmple. "Stay close," hisses Duessel, a command quickly heeded as you press your back against his in defensive position; the tension oozing about you like a plague of poisonous gas.

Half expecting pain to begin raining all over your body, you are momentarily surprised as music begins reaching your ears: a hauntingly whistled aria that almost seems beautiful if not for the situation. A buzzing sixth sense informs you that something is very wrong, and sure enough as you turn you see to your horror Duessel collapsing on his knees with little more than a grunt. Frantically, you look at Torchlight and Grinder who only follow suit as your gaze sweeps by, and before too long as the music's volume increases you feel your senses failing, your strength fading. As your vision blurs, the last thing you feel is a muffled sensation of the cold earth striking your face...

***​

"Missy? Missy, wake up."

Roused by the voice of Duessel, you blearily open your eyes. How long had passed since you fainted this time? It was irrelevant, in any case, reminding yourself that time held no meaning in this forest. Sitting up you identify your current surroundings with a jolt: the house that Duessel and you had lodged in.

"Nuzleaf are known to make music by blowing through flutes they make from leaves. When they do so, listeners -- people and Pokémon suffer a variety of effects. In any case the encounter brought us back to square one... as it always has." As Duessel bows his head -- in regret or frustration, you're not particularly sure -- you take a look outside; the trees the same state as when you had left.

"What did we do wrong?" he mutters, scratching his head. "I don't think we were wrong in thinking along the lines of a clock, but if that's the case... hm. Maybe the season of the forest we were in had something to do with the direction? Could it help to reread the instructions?"

What do you think?


Mireno Cemetery​

Ghost Town

Hayward
Going for a diplomatic approach, you try communicating to the Sableye in calm, hushed tones, while planning on a swift escape if the situation called for it. Unexpectedly the Sableye ceases its hopping, instead opting to stare at you with its crystalline eyes, devoid of pupils to read its expression by. The silent exchange persists for a minute or so, only interrupted by a blink every now and then, and if not for the area's dangerous reputation you might have otherwise found the scenario amusing.

"Sable." Nodding a little, the Pokémon of Darkness raises its claws to you, gesturing its fingers towards itself in a sort of "come hither" message -- or, at least, you think that's what it's trying to say. Pointing towards the walls from which it came you're ascertained that the Sableye does, in fact, want you to follow it, but what might the reason be? Dwelling on the decision you eventually reason that whatever the case is, not much could really hide in the open of a ruined building and Sableye, at least, doesn't seem to mean any form of harm.

Whatever you expected, you are surprised to find the figure of a reclining man, wearing tattered robes you're quite sure would have belonged in a Eastern-inspired museum as opposed to on an adventurer's frame. It takes a few calls from Sableye for the man to turn around; his weary, yet smiling face framed by shoulder-length greying hair and thin, wispy moustache and beard.

"And who might this be, Chroma?" he asks gently, as the Sableye titters back at him; it hadn't occurred to you that the little imp was actually a Trainer's Pokémon. "Yes, you're right. He's not quite like most of those who pass by here. I can see it in his eyes; he has a good head upon those shoulders. He has seen much and accomplished likewise for one so youthful. Yes, I think he will be at least sympathetic to our cause." Coughing to clear his throat, the man proceeds to address you.

"Forgive my unbecoming appearance and poise, young man. My name is Yuanli; wandering mediator by trade. I specialise in the harmony of people and Pokémon, nature and spirits, no matter their history or character," he introduces. "For years I've trekked the lands far and wide, with the sole aim of resolving conflicts where they might arise, alongside my faithful partner here." At this Chroma flashes you a cheeky grin, his mouth lined with sharp teeth.

"However, I suppose even experience is no curative for the effects of aging. My most recent assignment required me to appease some spirits whom, I believe, reside in a massive resting ground below this very hamlet. I had come across a research group who claimed that an artifact they procured was cursed, and thus begged me to return it to its true location of origin," continues Yuanli. "After weeks of searching I chanced upon this abandoned settlement, but unfortunately I was set upon by a band of thieves, who pilfered the bag that contained the artifact in question. I was injured in the process; while not grievously, my age left me quite unable to continue then. ...The incident was a fortnight ago. Chroma assisted me, faithfully as he always does, by searching for scraps of food and scaring away any potential intruders. He judged you and deemed that you would not threaten us, and so brought you in.

"We have only just met, young man; yet, my work has taught me one truth amongst others. Few things happen without rhyme or reason. That we met here is likely the sum of a greater equation." The aged man smiles at you again; positive as his demeanour is, you can tell that he is considerably weakened. "If it would not trouble you too much to satisfy the whims of an old man, I would appreciate it if you assisted me in my quest. In my state I doubt I could retrieve the artifact -- or, if you're not minding, a little support out of this town would be nice, to prepare for another venture."

What will you do?

In Mireno’s Ghost Town, the trainer Will Hale listened intently to Yuanli’s tale, and marveled at the elderly stranger’s resolve and resiliency. Will gaped as he learned that Yuanli had survived in this forsaken hamlet for the past two weeks on nothing but the bits of leftover food his faithful Sableye Chroma had brought him. As the wizened old man finished explaining his predicament and fixed Will with a questioning look, the boy dragged a hand through his short brown hair and thought hard about what he should do. His first instinct had been to help the man out, but his recent journey through the Volcanic Mountain Range’s Sacred Forest had taught him to be more cautious.

While trekking through that arboreal region, Will had unwittingly been involved in a tribal feud when a woodland warlord had tricked him into “helping” escort a biological weapon straight into the heart of enemy territory. The boy’s intentions had been virtuous, but still he had inadvertently caused a great deal of damage and destruction. Almost irreparable. Will learned a valuable lesson on that journey; unfortunately, not everyone who asked for help was honest. Since then, he had resolved to no longer blindly trust someone just because they requested his assistance. He knew it might seem harsh, but he didn't want to be duped again.

After another moment of contemplation, Will looked from Yuanli to Chroma and back. He certainly had no reason to doubt the wizened old man’s story. Despite Yuanli’s circumstances and having lost a good deal of strength, he had retained a cheery attitude and optimistic outlook, and Will greatly admired the man's determination not to give up. Looking at him lying on the ground in such a poor condition, it was hard for Will to imagine he could be planning something sinister. Will nodded, consenting to help Yuanli retrieve the stolen artifact. “Alright, I’ll help you out,” he replied. Bowing respectfully, he introduced himself as Will Hale, a trainer from Pacifidlog Town, Hoenn, before continuing, “But what about you? Are you going to be okay? I have some Berries in my bag you can have. They might help you recover a bit of your strength.”

Unfortunately when you found me, Will, I was unable to perform a prior investigation, but thanks to you we're now working together, and I can perform the necessary surveys proper." "Sableye!" Like a child in a candy store Chroma leaps ahead, pattering across the ground in a crouched position, scratching at the dirt every now and then. "Sableye, being partially Ghost-type, have natural affinities for spirits and related interactions. They stand out as individuals also because of their natural skills; as cave-dwellers, they have developed heightened senses for navigation and identifying the presence of others in situations where vision is highly impeded. We recognise these abilities as the techniques Foresight and Detect," explains Yuanli. "In addition, their preference for crystals as food grants them not only clarity in sight, but to use the power of crystals as their own; focusing energies and dissipating them at will. As such, I owe a great deal to Chroma in my work." The venerable sage takes a few moments to proudly stroke his beard. "Pardon the ramblings of an old man, Will. Age tends to render a direct relationship shared between experience and long-windedness."

"Sabl?" Some distance away Chroma circles a bit of ground like a Growlithe about to recline. A set of red lights streaming from the imp's eyes of gemstone bathe the dirt, as Chroma attempts to sense what lies beneath, but out of nowhere Yuanli's grip on his staff seems to tighten as he steps back.

"A looming presence of hatred and fear!" he declares. "Will, Chroma! Get back!"

"DRAPI!" "Sableye!"

Barely scurrying back in time, the ground where Chroma was only moments ago bursts outward, littering the surroundings with dirt clods and dust clouds. Segmented limbs rise out of the choking wake, two jagged arms connected to a spiked head. Swaying as it scuttles, the Drapion makes its entrance known with another loud roar.

"Hmmm. Not quite what I expected," muses Yuanli, then dropping a hand to pat Chroma on the head in assurance as the Sableye looks guiltily on. "It seems that our new acquaintance has suffered a noticeable degree of torment and pain," he observes. "This forced it to re-emerge in front of us, although I cannot say for sure what the cause was. Regardless, it appears to be beyond pacifist means of calming down." With the signal of a sharp jab from Yuanli's stake to the ground, Chroma stands up boldly, walking towards Drapion as the latter continues screeching and flailing. "Are you ready, Will?"

Too chilled by the freezing zephyr to react in spite of resistance, Kingler helplessly lets the parasitic pod bean it on its crest, which soon wraps its siphoning tendrils about its carapace.

"...!" Tensing his muscles in apparent anticipation, Eriden gestures forward as both enemy Waters wave their appendages in annoyance; soon enough issuing Bubblebeams from their mouths in response. Not one to be outdone, clearly, the merman blows a Powder Snow ahead this time, freezing the incoming orbs of water in mid-air. Weighed down by the latent change from liquid to solid the bubbles crash to the floor, harmlessly breaking into smithereens. "Attacking by wild instinct... an effort doomed to failure, for certain," declares the experienced warrior, and you affirm to yourself that he's definitely not an individual to be messed with, as Arctacea launches into a phase of Growth. Blips of energy, wrung from Kingler's essence, fly through the air towards their intended recipient, and noting this Octillery appears to slither in front of its battlemate.

"Hm?" Not having expected the defensive tactic, Eriden watches as Kingler begins a Harden from behind Octillery, the latter raising its tentacles up and down in a ritualistic pattern. "Focus Energy, I see. Combining its immovable traits and its sniping ability to help protect its comrade... But even such plans are not free from falling astray." While Arctacea blasts the octopus with his Icy Wind, you turn to Eriden who has taken to clapping his hands in a mystic rhythm at which you find yourself tapping your feet to.

"Encore," explains the warrior, "Now we know what the enemy will be up to next, as well as created an opening in the battle. With Octillery's Suction Cups blocking the way we need to take every chance we get to eliminate this obstacle as swiftly as possible."

While not natural for a pokemon such as Arctacea to learn, a halloween celebration had brought forth a little tutelage from Volcape, and as the Snover glanced towards his foes, the trainer rubbed his brow in concentration.

"This should buy us time. Arcy, take advantage of your grass typing against Octillery, as it's body is softer than the shell of Kingler by flicking a razor leaf barrage into a hail of seed bombs. Ghost Town

Hayward
Screeching in affirmative at the alteration in plans, Martéo speedily executes the new commands she is given. A breeze billows behind your ears as your Taillow summons up a Tailwind, pushing herself and Chroma to greater speeds with the attuning of wind patterns to suit your team's movements, while Chroma appears to raise his hands to the sides of his head, clacking his fingers while growling. "Role Play allows a Pokémon to replicate the special ability of their target. Whether this Drapion was destined for protection or exploitation of anatomical weaknesses, it will quite indubitably aid our cause if we borrowed it. Even from those who mean harm to us, there is still much to learn." Again, stroking his beard, Yuanli appears to mull the situation before continuing. "Chroma, I believe the time calls for a Dark Pulse."

"Leye Sableye!" It doesn't take long at all for the Dark-type to tap into his natural reserves, blasting rings of negative energy from his mouth, but even with its speed rendered inferior to yours Drapion is no less capable of battle and in response, storms a squall of the same dark projectiles to meet Chroma's. As the temporary stalemate occurs, Martéo circles the exchange overhead, but even as she flies you know that she's Focusing her Energy to strike Drapion wherever she can to do the most damage. With the seconds pushing on you find Chroma still holding strong but undoubtedly beginning to fail, you signal to Martéo for the next phase to begin. Swinging her wings Martéo fires waves of Air Cutter at Drapion's head, and as the focused winds hit home Drapion isn't particularly pained, but the attempt is enough to get its concentration to weaken, letting the remainder of Chroma's attack get in.

"Now, Chroma! Low Kick!" The command could not come swifter as the senior's Sableye swings into action, diving for a kick that wouldn't look out of place in a baseball game. With the attack targeted at its lower regions, Drapion howls as the technique designed against weightier Pokémon causes it to topple over onto its side; a shower of Poison Stings littering from its jaw and sprinkling harmlessly over the dusty road.

"It's beginning to get careless in its aim and execution of techniques. We should be capable of disabling it soon enough if we remain consistent in our advancement," Yuanli advises...

Yuanli was right. Will wholeheartedly agreed. They must continue to press their advantage. In its current, distressed state, there was absolutely no way of predicting what Drapion might do next, and they had to try to immobilize it before it could regain any of its wits. Chroma was fighting well, but Will wondered how much longer the little purple imp could keep it up. It certainly must have consumed a considerable amount of energy using Role Play. Despite Will’s first impression, Yuanli had turned out to be quite the strategist and Will felt slightly foolish that he had judged the man solely based on his outward appearance. Will realized that if kept his own eyes open, he just might learn something about battling and timing from the mysterious oldster and his faithful Sableye.

Turning to his tiny avian companion gliding overhead, Will felt a fierce sense of pride suddenly surge inside himself. Martéo was certainly doing her species proud, he couldn’t help but admit, living up to its Pokédex entry of courageously taking on larger, stronger opponents.

“Brilliant, mate!” the boy praised, grinning. “Don’t get cocky! Stay one step ahead of Drapion with Quick Attack! Then try to slow it down with a Twister-Icy Wind combo. Wait until the Ogre Scorpion is in the center of the cyclone though before you let fly with the chilly gust! We’ll to try to stop our opponent by flash-freezing the air swirling around it!”
