Mireno Cemetery​
Cemetery Entrance

Hayward
Conceding with Yuanli's views, you ponder the riddling inscriptions as you pore through your inventory -- with the little information that is provided, you're quite certain that the item in question is one of edible varieties, and the line "of creatures adamant" helps you to eliminate some of the other choices you have. Eventually deciding on a berry that you conveniently happen to have multiples of, you bravely extend a hand with the Pinap in your fingers, depositing the fruit at the bottom of the stone slab. Stepping back, you wait for something to happen, but nothing can prepare you for what takes place after a few moments -- right before your eyes, the skin of the Pinap Berry darkens in patches, until the entire of the spice-laden food is a disgusting, rotten shade of black. Deflating like a punctured beach ball, the berry releases a cloud of its stinging scent that ends in a putrid pong, before crumbling into the ground; somehow you have an image of the berry screaming as its freshness was wrenched away. Moments later, a low, ominous rumbling fills the passage as the stone sinks back underground, allowing you entry.

"They don't mince words, whoever put this in place. I suppose we should be thankful that a costlier toll was not demanded of us," sighs Yuanli, shaking his head. "I fear that the tasks to fulfill will only become more insurmountable, but we must press on."

Everyone concedes in silence as you enter through the ivy-invested gateway, a sharp chill diving down your spine with your passing under its cover, and you can only feel stronger apprehension as the group moves into an open area of the catacombs. You find that while no torches stand in this area, light is provided by a variety of bizarre, spherical fungi sprouting about the landscape like freaky little light bulbs -- any other vegetation is sparse, with but several types of creepers and pitiful stems, curled and malformed by the lack of light and proper nourishment, almost like hands of the buried alive desperately trying to break free but to no avail.

"Very little information exists on the cemetery of Mireno. Even the research group who requested my services could only offer what they themselves had heard," Yuanli recalls to you. "Some stories say that Mireno was initially a subterranean location of worship, others say it was a facility of study -- of whom, or what, I'm afraid no one can quite remember. But what is generally agreed on is that six priests attempted to, by means of Mireno, forge a gateway to what they called "the promised land". No one knows what they meant, but since then Mireno turned into the wasteland everyone recognises. In fact, very few locals dare to speak of it directly, I hear, believing the name itself to be cursed. They say that the ruined village above is testament to Mireno's power..." The sage pauses. "What can be confirmed is that since the time of those priests, traps laid throughout the years are effective even today. If this would follow like the challenge earlier, Will, we must proceed in utmost caution, more than before... Chroma, if you don't mind, a Flash would help us out."

"SABL!" You almost see the gears grind behind the imp's crystalline eyes, as a spectrum of light bursts out of Chroma's face, widening such that you can see the surroundings more clearly: a pair of dark caverns, heading to your extreme left and right, and deep in the wall ahead you're shocked to see another cavern -- the shocking characteristic being a series of sharp, steel formations the shape of fangs choking the entrance, with a single circular symbol embedded in the centre that makes you think of an eye.

"Another seal... My guess is that this new obstacle is far more stringent than the one we faced earlier," remarks Yuanli. "Whatever lies behind it likely derived its origin of some fiendish power; unfortunately our final objective -- that is, to return the item to Mireno's centre -- lies beyond said barrier, and I doubt teleportation could bypass this. I can feel the intensity within these bones of mine." Indeed, looking to Chroma and Medéo you find both of them frowning as one; whatever they're sensing and you're not, they don't like it one bit.

"Much as I would prefer to spend less time in this miasma, it seems we have no choice." Turning to you, Yuanli nods as he continues, "Without a better understanding of this place and its key, we cannot hope to succeed... Will, considering how your keen insights have brought us this far, I believe it will not be inappropriate if you decided the navigation for us. We need to explore the depths of the catacombs available to us posthaste in order to advance with the breaking of this seal."

Two choices lie around you for the moment, and now that Yuanli has placed the decision in your hands, what will you do?

Faced with a decision about which passage to take, a myriad of questions filled Will’s mind and swirled around like a twister. The boy was certain that something sinister had happened in this subterranean location. But what? He recalled Yuanli’s tale of the six Priests, their attempt to open a portal to the “Promised Land,” and the resultant uninhabitation of Mireno. Will couldn’t even begin to guess what the priests had done, and had to admit that such mystical matters were quite beyond him.

Feeling slightly overwhelmed, Will massaged his forehead and glanced at Medeo. What had they gotten into? Ever since he had set foot below ground, a sense of foreboding had steadily grown inside of him. He now had the feeling that this old graveyard might not be all it had originally seemed. Secrets lay buried here. Mysteries dwelt here, occupying the seemingly vacant tunnels. As he recalled Yuanli’s warning, Will realized that one thing was certain, whichever fork he chose, they would have to proceed cautiously, as there was just no way they could predict when they might be walking straight into a trap.

While Yuanli, Chroma, and Medeo awaited his decision, Will inspected the entrances to both passageways. As far as he could discern, there was nothing noticeably different about either one. Both appeared the same to him, but the boy knew that looks could be deceiving. The quiet, secluded tunnels seemed like the perfect place for wild Pokemon seeking shelter. He shuddered at the thought of encountering the massive rock-snake Onix in its home territory. Will tentatively sniffed the air surrounding the right entryway first, then the left, thinking he might just take the time tested advice of 'When in doubt, follow your nose,’ however, neither tunnel smelled foul to him and he couldn’t detect the scent of any Pokemon. Shrugging again, he silently made his way back to the right tunnel, and motioned for the others to follow him.

As he stared at the gaping opening to the rocky, right-hand corridor, Will cautioned Medeo, “Be careful, mate. There’s no telling what might be living down here or hiding in the shadows. Stay close now. I’m sure there will be side tunnels that branch off in all kinds of directions. It wouldn’t do us any good to get separated.” Medeo agreed and Will commented dryly, “If we get lost in here we could end up wandering around in the dark forever.”

The black-clad Trainer and the elegant Medicham chose the right tunnel, not knowing what they might find at its end…

Stark: Your trek across the cliff path continued to be just as eventful as before; that is, not that eventful at all. The sound of your boots meeting the ground broke the silence of the night twice every second, and while at first the sound bugs you, soon it fades once more into the periphery of your mind. The castle was still a ways away, by the looks of it, another thirty minutes or so at this pace would have you at the bridge. What you begin to notice is that this walk is far too quiet … the sound of your steps breaks the silence again, and you half curse at noticing that again. But, the silence around cannot be ignored; it’s dead quiet. The sea that spreads outwards beneath you is very calm tonight, no waves are hammering up the cliff’s face. There’s no wind, no pokemon, nothing … aside from your own footsteps that is. Curious, you scan all around you for anything living, and finally you spot three shadows cut across the abnormally large moon. Two Murkrow and a Honchkrow you identify quickly. They are much too far to hear however.

Sure the silence was curious, but you don’t feel terrified; you know you’re not alone on this mysterious island. Whether the company you know is here, is friendly or not, is an entirely different concern. Suddenly, another shadow cuts across the night sky. This time it was much closer. The shape flutters overhead with amazing speed, circles around, before diving straight at you with incredible velocity. Instinctively you reach for a pokeball, but before your hand has the chance to unclip a pokeball the shape changes directions once more, disappearing above the cliff with a shriek of terror. Not entirely sure what to make of the exchange, you take a few hesitant steps forward. Realising the flying beast is not coming back for you; you walk on, occasionally stealing glancing above you for another kamikaze dive.

Several more minutes pass uneventfully and now you could definitely feel an incline in the slope of the path, signalling the last stretch of the Wilheim Path. From farther down the path you could tell that once the slope steepened a bit, it curled around a bend where you could no longer see the path, before emerging once more in your line of sight right at the foot of the bridge that would lead you to the castle. Knowing your goal is now less than ten minutes away you pick up your pace slightly, reaching that bend that you had spotted earlier. You take the turn, not thinking much of the blind corner. No one could have been prepared for what waited for you around the corner. It wasn’t the sudden appearance of another human on the path that startled you, but rather who that person was. I only stopped for a second … this is just like before … just like.” His voice was frantic, scared, crazed. You were speechless. He tried to catch his balance on the edge of the path, but lost that battle. The sound of his body shattering onto a protruding rock preceded the splash by a fraction of a second. He was gone. Suddenly you feel really woozy, and you lean against the cliff face for support. As you slide down to take a seat your head naturally cocks back and for a brief second you think you can see a face looking down at you from above the lip. Peering to your right, you see Suméo catch Sneasel in the Force Palm of his hand, sending the feline skidding backwards by the magnitude of the strike, boosted from the anger it had cursed your Pokémon with earlier.

Renewed confidence marking his chubby complexion, Suméo pushes himself forward for the next move, but contrary to what you hoped for Sneasel swings its leg under the Makuhita's bulk, playing his weight against him in a Low Kick. "Ssssso... you would choossssse to perish here... We will honour your decisssssion!"

"Will, look out!" Your time in Mireno so far has led you to place good faith in your new companion's interjections, and Yuanli's alert call is not one you ignore. Leaping back with Medéo you barely avoid a sizzling spray of substance spraying from the snake's jaws; while you're no idea what it is the piercingly pungent smell clearly informs you that distance would be the best policy. Medéo on the other hand is not as lucky as his arm catches a stray spurt, and he cries at the poison eating at his limb.

"Gunk Shot. It's the strongest Poison-type attack known to Pokémon of that type, and judging by how it's affecting the floor... we can't take this lightly," mutters Yuanli, before he takes a glance to the left. "Chroma, the time for support is at hand; a Reflect if you don't mind."

"Sabl..." Drawing back from the Houndour, spatters of an Ember just fizzling out from his chest, the ever-trusty spirit taps into more of his Psychic capabilities. Much like how he had safeguarded the Drapion earlier, a glimmering panel appears in front of your team which Suméo hurries behind, leaving the trio of scowling Pokémon watching at the protective screen. "Excellent work, Chroma. Now, another Power Gem!"

"Sabl sabl sabl!" Another array of glittering jewels leaves Chroma's eyes, barrelling straight into the frustrated Flamethrower of Houndour; while it doesn't stop the assault completely you notice with a smirk it does have the effect of turning Chroma's ballistic missiles into fiery ballistic missiles, received with another yelp by the Dark Pokémon. Determined to complete his task, Suméo rushes at Sneasel with his arms waving around again, but this time Sneasel rushes ahead to meet him. Briefly disappearing for a split second, Sneasel smacks Suméo by his cheek with a glancing Faint Attack; you note the move to be mostly resisted but enough to send Suméo staggering to the side, a development you note with a frown as serious as Sneasel's grin. "Thissss isssss the bessssst of thossssse who might tempt death... Your remains will decorate the wallssssss of thissssss cave, I promisssssse that..."

"It doesn't even seem to be taking us seriously," Yuanli notices with worry, as you spot the faint magenta glow of Stockpile surrounding Arbok. "Perhaps we need to rethink our focus for the... What's that?" In the direction pointed by Yuanli's rod, you see what has newly caught the veteran's eye: even as he lies on the limestone ground a glow has likewise began to surround Suméo...

What will you do?
