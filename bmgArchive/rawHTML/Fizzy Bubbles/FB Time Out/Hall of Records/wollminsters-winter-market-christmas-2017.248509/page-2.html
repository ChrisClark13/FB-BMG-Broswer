Wollminster’s Winter Market

Far deep in the corner, in the land of FB, lay a small and quaint town, with one enormous pine tree.
The land was plain grim, stuck in perpetual snow, where the clouds were oft grey, and the cold wind did blow.
The villagers worked hard, as they toiled through their day, though they never complained, not once, no way.
The people were merry, I know it’s hard to believe, all for that one week, leading up to Christmas eve.

It is in this period, during these fine celebrations that they open up stalls, markets and other workstations.
The vacant town square, a centre in mute snore, comes alive with energy, with the civilian uproar.
Wax candles are lit and choir music is played, as the villagers gather to gossip and trade.
They rejoice in their company with a warm cup of tea, and some other hot drinks, that might make one feel tipsy.
Statues and theatres, both quiet and loud, for you to join in and show to the crowd.
Ice skating and snowballs, or, if you want to relax; Tapu Cocoa by fire, surrounded by knick-knacks.
Singing and chanting, if you don’t want to dance. Come and join in while you still have a chance.

But the biggest of all, is the large Christmas tree, adorned in its entirety, down to a tee.
Behind that of which lay the small town’s small temple, which was usually dull, but today rather special.
At the holidays climax, when all’s said and done, they hold hands and sing, all man, everyone.

And so we invite you to visit us, sweet dear
To Wollminster's fest, which only comes round once every year.​



Snacky Stalls
The local cuisine is too much to take in, as all the chefs in the area converge and begin.
With grace and dexterity, and a tad bit of rush, the cooks cater snacks after you’ve drudged through the slush.
They cook their speciality, all just for you. So take a sip and a bite as you take in the view.
They each compete, or so their songs do tell, as music is played with high decibels.
Plastic discs, coloured, and neatly lined up, to aid your digestion and accompany your cup.
Their dish is quite hearty, it boosts many strengths, as is your Pokémon’s by the songs’ wavelengths.


Sweet Stockings
If you’ve had enough of the cold, and need a place warm, then come on inside, away from the storm.
Nestle by the fire, all proud a crackling, and enjoy Tapu Cocoa while you hear the crowds laughing.
Children join in as they hang up their stocking, eagerly waiting for the Delibird’s knocking.
Trainers may join, along their Pokémon friend, by the fireplace a sock that they may append.
A bonding experience between buddies of gold, with sweeties delivered to both young and old.
Fruit to the good and candy to the better, all will feel right in spite of the weather.

Mélina was tired. Very much so. She had quite a difficult year, full of turmoils in her life, bad and good, but all draining. Job instability, quitting said job, take on Pokémon journey, open a boutique, travelling, base-building… She was completely out of energy. Also… despite being surrounded with Pokémon, from old friends to new, she couldn’t help feeling a little lonely, being out on her own, away from home, for so long. She didn’t know where to go, besides spending yet another boring day playing on her cell phone behind her boutique’s counter, and another boring night sleeping in the little mattress she left in the storage room…

Glitch wanted nothing of this for her. More than anyone, it knew what she has and was going through. It may be just a Pokémon, but it wasn’t any Pokémon: it was her starter, and an Arceus-damn Porygon. And it was going to give her a mini-vacation for the holidays.


One morning, about a week before Christmas, Mélina received at the boutique a pretty thick letter addressed to her. She scratched her head as she wondered what it could be. As she opened the envelope, an eyebrow raised as much as the questions in her thoughts.

“A trip to... Wollminster?” she read and wondered aloud.

It included a pamphlet detailing some Christmas celebrations offered there… and a letter congratulating her for winning an all-included train trip for the duration of the holidays.

“I never signed up for such a contest… Smells like a scam to me.” she walked right by the recycling bin and just about to throw everything in it when she got interrupted by her phone’s alarm, startling her.

“Noooooooooooooo don’t throw it away!” the phone’s text-to-speech said.

Mélina paused for a moment, before putting her hands on her hips with a scorn on her face. “Glitch, what did you do, this time?”

“I swear that I didn’t spend a single dollar!” the Porygon continued to communicate through its trainer’s device. “Not even with someone else’s credit card info! And it is totally not my fault that the contest’s randomisation system really, really sucked. Especially with their security.”

She groaned then facepalmed. “What are you getting me into this time...”

“Aw come on, I’m just giving you a little nudge so you can take a freakin’ vacation. You need a break, and you’re gonna take it. Doctor’s orders. Besides, I just know for a fact that you want to do something for the holidays. Or at least do something other than rot in here.”

“...Yeah, I do,” the woman sighed, admitting defeat. “Did it really need to involve controlling the results of a contest for this? Next time you could’ve just suggested me to go.”

“If I didn’t, you wouldn’t want to go, because the travelling there is either expensive or slow and cold.”

“...Touché.”

“So are you going to this free thing I worked on to get you or not!?”

“Yes yes, I’ll be going. Just promise me that we’re not gonna get in trouble with what you did.”

“I swear it won’t!”

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Mélina may not admit it to Glitch, but its idea perhaps one of the best that he ever did for a while. (Even though it did have a few implications she didn’t agree on.) That train trip was luxurious; something she could only dreamed of. The bed was comfortable, the food was delicious, the view on that panoramic room was beautiful, and she was treated like a queen by the staff. There was even a Pokémon sitter of sorts so they could have a bit of the treatment too. She enjoyed her time so much that she didn’t want to leave… but she had to as the train arrived to its destination: the far-off town of Wollminster.

When she arrived, she couldn’t help but feel a little nostalgic from the weather and the decorations: it felt a lot like like home. A bit too much, perhaps, because her laziness crept up on her again. But she really didn’t want to miss out on the festivities. So she pulled up the scheduling pamphlet to try and find some events that would be simple and comfortable to do.

“Entertainment Exhibitions... ” she mumbled to herself aloud. “Starlette would love that but nah, don’t feel like getting on stage… Merchant’s Market? I’m curious, but ugh, I hate shopping, especially at this time of the year.”

She did a double-take as she read the third event. “Food? By the best cooks of the area? Oh my Mew.” While already salivating just from reading the description, Mélina grabbed a highlighter and circled at least a couple of times “Snacky Stalls” on the paper.

“Trim-a-Tree… Decorating trees is fun, and Catalina could make a good Christmas star tree...” she giggled, “but meh. Feels like too much trouble.”

“Sweet Stockings, on the other hand… I love the sound of that. More food with a side of Tapu Cocoa, all in the comfort of a home warmed up by a fireplace. I’m aaaall in.” Just like the cooking competition, she marked it as a must-see.

Looking over the last event though, she did a bit of a disgusted face. Going door-to-door in the cold to sing to strangers does not sound like a good time. Anyhow, she have a feeling she won’t have enough time to go to any more events.

So with that, Mélina headed to the Snacky Stalls, hoping her stomach was empty enough to sample every single dish it had - and have plenty of room for dessert afterwards.

The festival was in full swing when Schala arrived. Several of her Pokemon were in tow, including Apate and Airmid. Uttu lounged on her new favorite perch and their newest addition to the team, Ager, a Mudbray. While Schala had wanted to explore a bit, Airmid had overheard that you could decorate an Abomasnow at the Trim-a-Tree area. Schala hurried after the Shroomish before she lost sight of her. How does she move so quickly! Ager followed behind at a more sedate pace.

Apate floated above everyone, complete with what Schala had dubbed a ‘Santa Sack’ and a haphazardly attached santa hat. (Schala had gone through all of her gifts beforehand, just in case. The present containing fake Spinarak hadn’t made the cut, unfortunately.) The Pumpkaboo watched the mass of people enjoying the festivities. Her eyes met those of a white Vulpix peeking over the shoulder of her trainer as they leaned over to look at the wares of a booth. The Vulpix’s wide eyes drew her in, and she descended quickly to attach a bow festooned with holly to the Vulpix’s ear, before speeding after her disappearing trainer and teammates.

She caught up just as Airmid arrived at the tree decorating area. The Shroomish made a beeline for the largest Abomasnow she could find. Schala watched a bit helplessly, and looked longingly at the large amount of other, less angry looking trees. Somehow, she figured the excitable Shroomish didn’t quite get the point of ‘tree decorating’.

In fact, said Shroomish was currently in a staring contest with her chosen tree. She held a garland, made of white and red Amaryllis to be more Holiday themed, up imperiously. The Abomasnow stared back impassively, and made no move to take it, or even bend down to make it easier for her to place it on him.

Schala approached, and reached out to break the stalemate by lifting Airmid into reach. But Airmid grunted and shuffled forward, thrusting the garland clutched in her tiny arms towards the Abomasnow with a decisive “Shroom”.

The Abomasnow remained unmoving. Apate floated past with a giggle, but also made no move to help.

“Airmid--” Schala began nervously. The Shroomish was starting to puff up a bit, and Schala had seen first hand how badly she took anyone refusing her gifts.

The trainer was cut off as Airmid started to glow. Her form grew taller, and then when the light faded she stood tall as a Breloom. The Abomasnow looked surprised, but Airmid didn’t waste any time. With her new height, and her stretching arms, she quickly placed the garland onto the the Abomasnow’s head. She leaned back, triumphant.

“Did you really just evolve so you could be taller?” Schala asked, aghast. Apate hovered closer to get a better look at Airmid’s new form. Unrepentant, the newly evolved Pokemon, quickly looked for a new target.

“Woah, you can’t just go ‘decorate’ all of the trees and people.” Schala was quick to stop her before she got them all in trouble.

With one potential disaster averted, Schala explained the point of tree decorating. Apate immediately sped off to look at the various ornaments and other provided tools at their disposal, while Airmid examined her stash of presents. She couldn’t decorate their victim tree with only garlands, so she’d have to construct some things from scratch.

Uttu, meanwhile, had decided to give her own brand of help. Using Magnet Rise to lift herself off of Ager's head, the little Joltik used her String Shot and Spider Web to cover the Abomasnow with drapes of silk. While they weren’t colorful, the pattern of the web was rather nice (in Schala’s opinion, at least), and it offered plenty of extra places to hang decorations from. Apate wasted no time taking advantage of them, hanging a few lighter ornaments from them--little sparkling baubles and gingerbread shapes. Schala took a few from her to place on, though she was still a bit wary of the Abomasnow, it seemed dedicated to playing the role of a Christmas tree, even if it didn’t look particularly happy about it.

As Schala and Apate worked to hang ornaments and then a few Christmas lights, Airmid put the finishing touches on the ‘star’ for the top of their tree. Constructed of bright red Poinsettia’s arranged with a wreath of Holly as a base to act as a crown, the tree topper was set carefully on top of the Abomasnow’s head, ringed by the earlier garland.

The group stepped back a bit to take in the sight, as Ager offered Uttu (who had just returned to his head) the plug for the lights. With a bit of electricity, everything was complete.

“Great job, everyone!” Schala said, enchanted. It was a bit of a tense start, but the decorations had turned out wonderful.

“I did say we would need to get you a real scarf.” A Liepard commented to her trainer as they walked the Merchant's Market. Eskil carried the newest addition, Beira, in his arms. The Alolan Vulpix had quickly discovered the effectiveness of doe-eyes. Or in this case, Baby-Doll Eyes, and had been using them to manipulate everyone around her as much as possible. Even Arwan wasn’t immune, and he couldn’t even see.

The trio walked the stalls, looking for scarves, but also anything else that looked interesting. Eskil had some presents already, (including a Razor Claw for Khione that was sitting in his pack until he found a good time to give it to her), and so he was primarily looking for gifts for Beira and Nyx.

He stopped at a stall with some unique looking pieces of jewelry, though he doubted either of them were particularly interested. He and Nyx leaned over the stall for a better look, while Beira looked over his shoulder, disinterested. The pieces were nice, but Nyx indicated she wasn’t interested, and so the pair moved on.

As he shifted Beira in his grip, he noticed a new addition on her head. He was certain that the bow decorated with holly attached to her head had not been there earlier.

“Where did you get that?” He asked. She only gave him an innocent look. He stared suspiciously for a bit, but she wouldn’t have been able to steal the bow and attach it to her head without help. Someone probably gave it to her. Probably.

Electing to keep a closer eye on her, just in case, he headed to the next stall. This one contained a multitude of leather items--belts, wallets, collars and more. Beira’s eyes lit up (at least more than usual), at the sight, and she leaned forward to place a paw on one particular collar. It was dyed a dark blue, and the silver snowflake charm hanging from it glinted in the surrounding lights.

“That one, huh?” Eskil commented, as he freed one hand to pick it up. It did look well made, and Beira had turned the full force of her eyes on him. Not that she’d needed to. Mysterious bow notwithstanding, he had already planned on buying her something.

“Are you sure? This is your only present.” A nod in response. Eskil turned to the bemused shopkeeper and inquired about the price. After hashing out the details, and ensuring that the collar fit her well, the group left the stall with their new purchase, Beira preening with her new gift. Nyx quickly spotted their next target.

“This way.” She brushed past Eskil, heading towards a stall with what looked like brightly colored fabric hanging for display. Once Eskil got closer, he realized that it was actually countless scarves. He reached up to touch one, thick and dyed deep purple, almost the same shade as Nyx’s fur.

“All of my scarves are made with 100% Mareep wool!” Chirped the woman behind the counter. Eskil didn’t really know the difference, but they did feel like they would be very warm.

“Purple isn’t really your color,” Nyx commented bemusedly, as if she was aware of why he had focused on that particular color. She leaned up against the counter on her front legs, examining all of the options. “That one, or that one. Try them on.” She gestured to two scarves.

Eskil placed Beira on the ground with an instruction to stay put (and ignored her pout), and reached for them. One was a vibrant green. The other was a royal blue. He tried both on and modeled them for Nyx.

“Hmm. I like them both, but the blue matches your eyes better.” She commented. Decision made, he paid the woman, wrapped his new scarf around his neck, and retrieved Beira.

Their main objective had been achieved, but he was missing a gift for Nyx, and so he continued to explore. Keeping an eye on her to see if anything caught her eye, their next stop was another jewelry stall. Nyx was eying an assortment of bangles, simple and made of different metals with various designs. She made no comment, but Eskil made a note of them. He’d stop by later if she didn’t decide on something else. The trio continued on taking in the sights and the wares.

"Brrr. Sure would like to try some of that warm Tapu Cocoa I've been hearing about right now."

Jess glanced to her comrades for confirmation, but unfortunately they didn't seem very receptive to the suggestion. Royal the Carbink merely stared blankly back at her (while Jess wondered whether he ate or drank at all, or even felt the rise and fall of temperature), and Zoom the Sandshrew seemed more preoccupied with acting petrified of all the people surrounding her, clearly not used to being in a crowd. As for the other, well...

Jess glimpsed back down the busy street, where she sensed a brief shadow of movement before it darted behind a pole - although the thin column wasn't near wide enough to cover the bushel of white tails whisking about. She sighed, having hoped that this outing would prove a chance to get acquainted with some of the newest "members" of her team, although at least one of them was deliberately being rather uncooperative.

Fortunately there was one who was more than accommodating and supportive, whom she had brought along for that very reason. Link padded alongside Zoom, encouragingly nudging the young rodent on, while their follower watched from behind her hiding spot, ducking back into darkness whenever he turned his head. Meanwhile Royal kept up quietly beside his new trainer, neither making any intonation nor expression.

Since none of them seemed particularly interested in snacks at the moment, Jess decided to try a different tactic by tempting them with trinkets. Turning into the market mobile, she strolled down the lane full of dazzling distractions, as each peddler they passed vigorously hawked their wares to unwary customers. Despite the din and commotion of commerce, Zoom seemed spell-bound by the sparkling selection, and even Royal appeared intrigued by the gemstones which bore striking resemblance to himself. Out of the corner of her eye, Jess caught the kit's ears perk curiously as well, and shared a knowing smile with Link.

"Well, how about it, guys? Up for some Christmas shopping? If you see anything you like, let me know. You each can pick out one thing and I'll buy it for you."

As they strolled along the stalls, Zoom eventually stopped at a stand selling an assortment of snowglobes. Her gaze was fixated on a small sphere containing a miniature model of a modest igloo, humble and homely. Jess recognized the recent hatchling must be feeling homesick, and missing her mother - whatever happened to mysteriously remove parental presence from her life. Jess's heart softened as she asked the dealer for permission to handle his wares, before gently lifting the orb and bending down to bring it to Zoom's level.

"Here, watch this."

She shook the orb rigorously, tilting from side to side to swirl a storm of glittering flakes inside the globe, as her spectator marveled in awe at the magic raining down within the watery winter world. When the white slowly settled at last, Jess proffered the pocket planet to her amazed partner.

"Want to give it a try?"

Zoom delicately accepted the marble, flipping it upside-down and peering delightedly at the glistening grains of pearls precipitating gradually to the bottom, own beads of black shimmering with joy.

"Would you like to keep it?"

Zoom nodded eagerly, and Jess beamed as she stood to pay for her purchase. They continued on, Zoom gingerly cradling her prize close, careful not to let it drop due to its fragility. As they traveled, Royal abruptly came to a halt before another artisan's artifact booth, with several pieces of exotic jewely on display. Prominent among them was a burnished golden crown with a star-shaped emblem in its center, albeit rather unremarkable compared to the fancy, flashy festoons adorned with a myriad of shining ornaments. Rather than rubies or diamonds though, the Carbink seemed captivated by the dull diadem. When Jess inquired about the work, the trader confessed to little knowledge about its origins either, but judging by appraisal it was possibly of Greek craftsmanship. An enigmatic circlet, circa ancient times, rumored to once belong to a race of powerful warriors. So the legends say anyway.

While Jess had some doubts about the authenticity of the article, Royal nevertheless appeared charmed by the chaplet, which was a rarity unto itself. Thus she consented to fork over enough cash for the coronet, which she placed reverently atop the rock's rabbit-like ears. They merely twitched slightly in response, revealing no other reaction. Something told her the wearer was satisfied though, and he admittedly looked quite regal in it, befitting his name. Jess then revolved to Link (still keeping an eye on the one lingering behind them as well):

"What about you? Anything you want?"

The Poochyena paused for a bit, considering. Eventually he trotted over to another tent stocked with festive clothing such as warm, woolly stockings and sweaters, tugging at a green pointed hat - just the right size for an elf. Jess smiled as she helped him try it on, selecting a crimson cap with silver trim and a jingle bell on its end for herself.

"Now you really look like Santa's little helper."

She declared with a chuckle, admiring themselves in the mirror provided. (While a part of her was tempted to add a scarlet scarf to complement the ensemble, her personal reservations prevented her from physically sporting such items close to her neckline.) As her headgear rang merrily with laughter, she detected a stir as the straggler finally stepped out from behind the screen to investigate the source of the sound, enchanted by the chime. Jess grinned at the Alolan Vulpix, glad she had finally decided to join them.

"Hey, Makoto. Would you like something as well? Go on, feel free to choose whatever catches your eye."

Makoto wavered, but then boldly marched up to a rack of accessories, pulling off a plain red ribbon with a pair of bronze bells attached.

"You're sure?" Jess blinked in surprise. "You just want that? You don't want something better?"

The fox dipped her chin, ringing the string in the process and producing a clear, pleasant note. Thus Jess amiably agreed and knelt down to assist with wrapping the collar around her new friend's snowy scruff in a neat bow (almost simulating a present herself), petting her coat proudly.

"Well, now that we've all got our gifts, what do you guys say to some food now?"

This time the chorus resounded with concurrence, and so they trekked back towards the direction of delicious aromas wafting through the air. When they arrived, most of their mouths were already watering from the smells, having worked up an appetite from all the walking (well, aside from Royal anyway).

"So many different options to choose from..." Jess gaped at the extensive menu, inflated with deluxe dishes. "Anything you guys want to try first?"

Link lifted his snout, sniffing his way to a vendor who specialized in steamed meat buns. Makoto trailed after him, drooling visibly from the delectable scent. Jess took that as a sign of approval and so procured a pack of baked pastries for the two to share. As soon as Makoto bit into the soft, fluffy shell and her tongue met with the succulent filling inside, her blue eyes brightened and she wolfed down as many as she could alongside her wolven companion, who upon reaching the last roll obligingly offered it to her, resulting in a pink tinge against pallid fur as she blushed furiously, which she attempted to conceal with her fan-like appendages.

Suppressing a snicker with her own sleeve, Jess swiveled to her other two comrades. She spotted Zoom eyeing another sign with what appeared to be brown Magikarp painted on it, which turned out to be an advertisement for said fish-shaped red bean cakes.

"Oh, I think those are called taiyaki. They're filled with a sweet paste, kinda like moon cakes. I've always wanted to try one myself, want to join me?"

Zoom indicated affirmation, and so she obtained a couple of the karps, handing one to the shrew as she cheerfully dug in, smearing cherry across her cheeks. Jess giggled and retrieved a napkin to wipe the mess, before rotating to Royal.

"What about you? Do you, um, wish to eat anything?"

The Carbink cocked his head, processing the question. Finally, he floated over to - of all things - an ice cream machine.

"Eh? Really? Are you sure?"

A silent, yet positive feedback. And so despite feeling somewhat nonplussed, Jess scooped some vanilla into a cup (determining a cone would probably prove too difficult for the limbless stone to handle) and set it down for him to consume. To her astonishment, he scarfed down the sundae without problem, and dare she say - seemed to thoroughly enjoy it.

Smiling to see everyone satiated with their meals, Jess wandered over to treat herself to a mug of famed Tapu Cocoa at last, which she sipped contentedly as she nibbled on her own dessert, sitting next to her pals as they listened to the music and mirth around them in bliss; observing as Makoto entertained by jangling in time to the melody's rhythm and Link howled in tune to the lyrics. Zoom clapped along in glee, and while Royal still made no noise, he seemed to bob a bit with the beat. Happy and harmonic, ready to welcome the holiday and toll in the new year - together.
