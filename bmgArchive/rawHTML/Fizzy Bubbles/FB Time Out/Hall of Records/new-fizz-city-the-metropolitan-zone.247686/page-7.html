“All I want is to wonder in peace.”

“So much to wonder.”

“So much to question.”

The silence between each sentence spoke more than the sentences themselves. It was completely oblivious at the chaos it created outside their home. Somehow, Mélina sensed that the strange waves were intended. It just wanted to be alone, after all. However, the rest was just unforeseen side effects of the heightened psychic power. The minds of the poor Mareanie and Toxapex stood no chance against it. Their brains simply sponged in all the defensive emotions and took it as their own.

Mélina also felt the psychic creature’s strong yearning for knowledge. Maybe it was the reason that pushed her of taking pictures of the walls earlier? She was unsure, but she had to admit a big part of her is her curiosity. Whenever information is historical or scientific, old or new, the Kalosian loved to learn about it. Perhaps it was one of the reasons why she quit her job to become a boutique owner and a Pokémon trainer. She yearned for change, and for discoveries to call her own, and going out and see this crazy world with her own eyes is one of the best ways to do it.

“Do you want to question… together?”

The woman flashed a sympathetic smile and nodded. The poor thing, despite yearning to stay here to be alone with its quiet research, it isn’t able to study much by doing so, despite its psychic abilities. (It’s not like it has access to Internet. The wifi or satellite connection must be horrible here.) In the best coincidence, both met here, and wish of the same thing… Without a doubt, travelling together would be the best outcome for both of them.

The Premier Ball which the trainer was presenting suddenly lifted off of her hands, as the question mark-shaped Pokémon swirled around it in fascination.

“How does one fit inside?”

“Question of the ages, buddy,” Mélina giggled. “Despite looking at many of them for so long, I never really figured it out. Kinda stopped thinking about it to be honest. All I know is whenever it touches a Pokémon, by being thrown at or simply touched on the button, it-”

Before she could complete her explanation, the Unown followed her words and pressed the Pokéball’s button. The ball swung open, swooped in the Pokémon into a swirl of red light, then snapped shut.

The Psychium Z crystal, no longer glowing, clang on the cave’s floor.

Mélina sighed. She was relieved, but also tired. She was just ready to call it a day and go home, now that the source of today’s problems has been removed.

Just as she went to pick up the Z-crystal, the whole room started to shake, almost causing her to lose her balance. The drips of water turned into small jets of streams as cracks formed into the walls, crumbling the hieroglyphs the girl took pictures of earlier.

“Of fucking course that happens.”

In a gasp, Mélina rushed to find an exit, a safe place to stay, anything that would help her survive the collapsing cavern… but it was too late. The pressure of the ocean’s depths were no match against the ancient grotto, no longer protected by its guardian. The water rushed in so quickly, the woman did not have the time to understand whenever she got hit by debris or ran out of air…

All she knew, was she blacked out.

She suddenly started coughing out water. She groaned in pain, feeling sore and soaked all over.

But she was alive.

As her energy came back to her, she slowly opened her eyes. The blinding sun of the sea was shadowed by many figures. She first recognized those closest to her, all her Pokémon companions that helped her earlier: Catalina the Minior, Indy the Woobat, her Dratini, Soleil the Espeon, Lucette the Feebas, and Olivia the Corsola. She then noticed a Pelipper flying in circles, squawking joyfully… when suddenly the face of an ecstatic Hank’s face popped over her.

“Oh thank Arceus. I thought you...” he desperately held in tears, and in an attempt to distracting away from it, slowly helped the trainer to sit up.

Mélina shook her head, trying to wrap her head on what was going on. After returning to her senses, she realized she was back on the Hank Tank, safe and sound. On top of that, the owner of the boat seemed to be safe and sound himself, up on his feet and grinning from ear to ear.

"The sea got really agitated a while ago,” he explained, “thought the Tank was gonna flip over... but then it calmed back down, and Lucette and Olivia were carrying you together on their backs! What on earth happened down there?!"

Getting a little teary-eyed herself, the woman appreciatively looked at her Feebas and Corsola. Without them, she surely would’ve ended as part of the ocean’s floor. Thinking back of the little she remembered though, nothing explained how they were able to find her, yet alone dragged her out of there before she drowned…

“Not knowing is scary, isn’t it?” The Unown voice echoed through her mind again. It sure spoke the truth.

Shaking her head to get back to reality, she sighed in relief as she glanced to the old sailor. “Oh gosh… Where to start...”

She did her best to describe the adventure she had. Starting from when Hank was knocked unconscious, passing through the Toxapex shell, the negotiation, the awful headache-inducing mushroom samba, and finally the ancient cave, with the Unown inside that was accidentally confusing the Mareanie and Toxapex… In a bit of a proud moment, she showed off the pictures she took. “Pics or didn’t happen”, as they say. Despite being a bit unbelievable, no fisherman stories were from her today.

"You not only saved my life but also the lives of every Corsola in the area. Broke those Mareanie free from their trance too... That took a lot of guts, miss. Not bad for a Kalosian!” he roared in laughter while Mélina sheepishly grinned.

”I... I want you to keep this,” Hank’s mood changed into a solemn one, as he handed to her a necklace which bore a Heart Scale. “It was a memento my brother left me before he went off on his own, but... Well I think you earned it more than me."

“I-I… uhm...” she hesitated. “I don’t feel like I’m worthy of holding on such a precious keepsake of your brother… but... thank you,” she carefully puts the pendant around her neck, then patted it against her chest. “I’ll take good care of it, I promise.”
