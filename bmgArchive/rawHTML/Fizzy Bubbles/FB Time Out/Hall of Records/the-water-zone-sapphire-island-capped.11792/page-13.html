Sasuke listened with interest to the flame-haired woman as she told the story of the egg. She and the Professor had found it in a cavern on the Ruby Mountain just a few weeks ago.

Sasuke then looked to the blue-haired man, who was also donning a white lab-coat, and assumed him to be this Professor Leingod.

They had scanned, probed, x-rayed and done everything possible to find out what species of pokemon could possibly inhibit such a large and beautiful egg, but it was to no avail as some unseen force seemed to prevent them from looking inside it. Giving up, they had decided to leave it in it’s natural habitat to hatch of it’s own accord. However, as Sasuke could testify to at first hand, that plan had not gone very well, and sitting vulnerably out in the open on the precarious mountain-side had put the egg in danger - even with Machamp to protect it.

’’My name is Nel, by the way. It’s nice to meet you Sasuke,’’ the lady introduced herself rather abruptly, and smiled broadly as she nodded and took his hand to shake. While Nel and Sasuke had been talking the Professor had been walking around the egg, taking samples and placing his head against the surface, but now he approached them and began talking. ’’It has been damaged, we must transport it to the research facility! Nel! Prepare your pokemon to help!’’ Sasuke muffled a laugh at Professor Leingod’s enthusiastic tone, who ran off up the mountainside.

Nel quickly took three pokeballs from her pockets, and released three pokemon to take out the task of transporting the large egg. A Poliwrath, a Granbull, and a Swampert were all suddenly stood on the mountainside. At Nel’s command they obediently ran towards the egg, and began steadying it to transport it away. Sasuke watched as the Swampert hoisted one end of the egg up onto his back, and Poliwrath and Granbull took the other, holding it above their heads.

Nel turned to Sasuke suddenly, ’’Sasuke, the Professor is a busy man, but I’m sure he’d appreciate it if you came to help out. I know I’d like you to..’’ she said as she turned and ran to catch up with her pokemon and professor Leingod, who had already disappeared into the mist.

Sasuke stood still for a moment, then quickly took the empty Leaf and Premier balls from his waist. He withdraw Sakura and Enma, thanking them for their help as he did so, and clipped the pokeballs back to his belt before hurrying after Nel.
