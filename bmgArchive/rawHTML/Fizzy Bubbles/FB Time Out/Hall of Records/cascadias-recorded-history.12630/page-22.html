CASCADIA​

Venom Island
Quite possibly the least attractive area of the entire island chain if you don't have a specific purpose, Venom Island is the epitome of all things putrid. The ground itself is saturated with poisonous substances. Also, all of the garbage from Salin City is transported here, but it is hardly noticed, as the acidic ground eats away at anything at a very quick pace. However, that means that not many things can live in this desolate area, with only a dead tree forest, a poisonous swamp, and a large skull tunnel interupting the landscape. The rest of the island is just plain dirt, but a good amount of poison types inhabit the area. The water surrounding the island is so poisonous that Pokemon cannot swim in it-therefore, unless it is a poison type, it cannot Surf to this area.
Skull Tunnel - Taking up small amount of the island, the top of Skull Tunnel is a giant white rock formation, shaped like a skull. However, Pokemon have dug tunnels far underground from the Skull. Poison and ghost Pokemon call this area home, and the tunnels are pitch black, so unless you'd like to wander around aimlessly, bring something with Flash.

A lone Crobat flies high over Venom Island, scoping out the area with his focused, powerful eyes. On his back is a doll-like young woman with pale lavender hair, whose own eyes are also focused on the ground beneath her. The pair soar above the island for a while, apparently looking for anything interesting, before the young lady suddenly points downward and shouts something to her Crobat, which causes the creature to immediately turn his attention to what his trainer is seeing and swoop down, angled in that direction. The pair descends at blinding speeds toward their target: a man who appears to be in his early twenties, accompanied by his Shuppet. The Crobat’s flight is so deadly silent that the man and his Pokemon do not realize they have been spotted until the Crobat lands inches away from the young man, and the lady riding the Crobat catches his attention by dismounting the Crobat with a soft, yet audible thud as she pats the giant bat on the head affectionately.

“Hi, Keith! Hi, Myrtle! I figured that it would be nice for Sangre and me to drop in and say hello, since we saw you about to enter Skull Tunnel…” Marion looks up at the ominous skull-shaped formation with a curious little half-smile. “I’ve always wanted to explore this place myself, but Minerva is rather resistant to learning Flash.” Marion grins, places her hands on her hips and perfectly imitates the stutter of her starter Pokemon in a light-hearted imitation of the Banette’s demeanor; “Do you take me for a cave dwel-ler, you sil-ly girl? Go buy your-self a flash-light and pre-tend to be a dwarf with-out me.” Marion giggles a bit, and after a moment of thought, loosens a Pokeball from her belt. “Actually, Sakura has learned Flash recently, and she adores underground caverns. Would you mind if we tagged along with you? It would be a wonderful experience for her.”

“Cro Crooo,” Sangre chimes in with a bloodthirsty look in his eyes.

“You think the hunting will be good here, Sangre?”

The Crobat nods with a wicked grin.

“All right, I suppose it would make sense for you to come along, too! Besides, you are also a cave dweller at heart, after all.”

Crobat nods to this statement as well, but with a more solemn, wistful expression. Marion smiles, and aims the Pokeball at her arm; she calls out a small Nincada which materializes onto her arm, and skitters up to her shoulder happily. She twitches her antennae with excitement at the thought of entering the dark, spooky cavern. Sangre, too, seems to be flapping his wings with more vigor than before; as he peers into the cave, however, he lets out a cry to alert Marion of an interesting presence. Marion immediately takes a look to where Sangre is pointing; in the darkness, a faint light can be seen, as if someone else had already used Flash. Marion carefully approaches to see who it might be, and immediately recognizes the voices of the trainers up ahead.

“Hey, Connor! Gaspard! Foster! Is that you? It’s me, Marion! Keith is here, too! What are the odds of that, huh?” Marion laughs cheerfully. It seems as if there’s going to be quite the team for this Skull Tunnel expedition…

CASCADIA​
Far off of the coasts of the mainland Fizzy Bubble zones, remote in the Silver Ocean, an island chain sits in basically, the middle of nowhere. These islands contain the some of the most diverse typing of Pokemon in the entirety of Fizzy Bubbles. All of these islands are actually volcanoes, but the majority of them have gone dormant. As many Pokemon spend all their lives here, many fossils of Pokemon and plants are buried in the depths of the volcanic ground, maybe some of these may be worth a look? You start out on the island that you first choose, via a ferry. In order to change areas afterward, Surf or Fly is a necessity.


Venom Island
Quite possibly the least attractive area of the entire island chain if you don't have a specific purpose, Venom Island is the epitome of all things putrid. The ground itself is saturated with poisonous substances. Also, all of the garbage from Salin City is transported here, but it is hardly noticed, as the acidic ground eats away at anything at a very quick pace. However, that means that not many things can live in this desolate area, with only a dead tree forest, a poisonous swamp, and a large skull tunnel interupting the landscape. The rest of the island is just plain dirt, but a good amount of poison types inhabit the area. The water surrounding the island is so poisonous that Pokemon cannot swim in it-therefore, unless it is a poison type, it cannot Surf to this area.
Bane Forest - Bane Forest, apparently, used to be a prosperous forest, much like Lily Island's Silver Forest. However, as the poisonous ground was formed, the forest died from the inside out, the trees becoming poisoned, and dying. Some toxins in the poison stopped the trees from decaying, leaving them gray and hard as rock. A few rare Pokemon live in the area, but there is also legend that something valuable is hidden inside one of the trees...

Now then, pick an area, and let your adventures begin!

"So, Blaze, I like where you have taken me. It reeks of death. And yet, it is so alive. I can feel it. The anquish."

"If you say so Hades. I just wanted to come here for the rumors about something in the trees. I bet it is gold. Gold is often found in trees as it soaks it up out of the ground."

"Blaze, Blaze, Blaze. What is it going to take to make you understand? Riches and golry mean nothing when you are dead. Nothing means anything when you are dead."

"It isn't going to convince me. I know death is all there is at the end of life. That is why I want to make a name for myself. So, I am not forgotten."

"Whatever."

Blaze had traveled by ferry to Venom Island with his Shuppet, Hades. Why he had come here he had no idea, but, he knew that something called him here that no other island did. Generally, when things call you, bad things happen. And with Hades around, it was bound to be worse. Blaze would never say it out in the open, but, Hades scared him a bit. He was always about death and gloom and doom. He never saw anything positive in life. And Blaze didn't doubt for a minute if Hades would try to make him understand. Forcefully.

After getting off the boat, they had walked out into the forest. The dead trees were everywhere, and Blaze was amazed. He knew about the effects that acid rain could have on trees, but the soil was so acidic that it ate away all the trees. The ones that didn't get eaten away were pretified, turned to stone for all eternity. Blaze knew that he would see some Poison and Ghost Pokemon, and he only wondered what he would find in the forest, far away from help...

Keith was surprised but pleased to find not only Marion, but also Connor, willing to explore Skull Tunnel alongside him. More than happy to have the company, Keith made it clear that he had no objections whatsoever to either one of them tagging along. However, as they were going through the cave, a flock of Zubat flew past, knocking into everyone and temporarily forcing Myrtle and Foster to stop Flash.

"Gah!" Myrtle exclaimed as she started Flash back up again. "What in the hell-"

"Hey," Keith interrupted, having just conducted a roll call. "Where's Sakura?"

After a moment of frantic searching, Connor noticed a light down the tunnel that was fading quickly- obviously Sakura. Without even needing to say a thing, they raced down the tunnel, until they came to a point where the tunnel split in four directions.

"OK," Keith said, taking charge of the situation. "I have an idea. Marion, tell Sangre to search each of the tunnels with Supersonic. If for some reason that doesn't provide any results, we'll pick a tunnel and start there. I don't think we ought to split up, by the way. If something took Sakura, it might be back, and if we stick together, we can at least look out for each other, make it a lot harder for them to take someone else."

"And what if the tun-nel splits off in-to e-ven more tun-nels, Meat Sack, hmm?" asked Myrtle.

Keith thought about this for a moment, then took out a Poke Ball and opened it up, unleashing a four-legged being with a budding flower on its back.

"OK, Pomona," Keith addressed his Ivysaur. "When we head down one of these tunnels, I want you to use your String Shot to leave a trail so we can find our way back, and remember where we've already been. OK?"

"Ivy! Saur!" Pomona replied, nodding.

Sangre immediately draws Marion's attention the weak sound deeper in the cave, a clink-clank that strongly resembled metal hitting metal. Marion listens to the sound thoughtfully, and is about to mention it to the rest of the group, when a flock of Zubat interrupts her musing and knocks the wind out of her and her companions, so much so that the two Shuppet momentarily lose control of their flash. When the pair of Shuppet are finally able to resume the lighting of the cave, Keith remarks that something is amiss and begins a roll call to double-check that everyone is present. Marion suddenly realizes that her shoulder feels a little bit lighter, and her first impulse is to search the ground for Sakura in case she was knocked off of her shoulder by the impact of the Zubat. With no sign of Sakura on the ground around them, the trio come to the conclusion that Sakura has gone missing, much to Marion's horror.

"Did the Zubat take her? Oh, Giratina..." Marion wrings her hands with worry, her eyes darting about the cave fretfully. She and the other trainers simultaneously notice a flash of light trailing down the tunnel (which is more than likely Sakura's) fading out of sight. It was difficult to tell whether she had been kidnapped, or if she was voluntarily following the trail of something that only she had sensed. The trio wordlessly set into motion following the light, until the cave splits off into four tunnels, and no trail of the little bug-type can be found. No visible trail, anyhow...

Keith suggests that Sangre use his echolocation abilities in order to locate Sakura. It was a tactic that Marion had used before in the dark basement of the lighthouse, and one that Sangre used frequently when hunting. Marion orders Sangre to use Supersonic on each of the four tunnels and see if he could potentially locate Sakura down one of them. She certainly agreed with Keith that splitting up was not the best idea, and nods to show her approval of the idea. After all, those Zubat were clearly frightened of something in the cave, and though the answer could be as simple as a few ghost-types having fun and playing tricks, that metallic sound that Marion heard earlier sticks in her mind. What could it have been...?

"Sangre, please let me know which tunnel you think is best for us to take. Little Sakura is depending on us..." The Crobat notes the deep concern in Marion's voice and nods gravely, clearly understanding the gravity of the situation.

Crater Island
Like the other islands of the archipelago, Crater Island is an inactive volcano. After what seems to be an excessively violent eruption, the empty crater filled with water, forming a lake. Many water types of many types live in the fresh water lake, and there are legends that some things lie in the depths of the lake...Possibly underwater caverns?
Iridi Beach - Formed by some strange mineral, Iridi Beach's sand is a very bright, sparkly white. Rather than black, like most volcanic sand, this is more of the sand you might find at a normal beach. A few sunbathing Pokemon who venture from the water may be found here, but mostly, this is just a tourist attraction.​
Click to expand...

It was September time, and in most places – including her apartment home in Fizzy Bubbles central – it was beginning to feel a bit fresh. The fashion and weather gods dictated more subtle colours of warmer clothes, but in Cascadia, the outfits were pressured only by the location’s specific conditions. Alice had spent her summer vacation in various troubling situations: stuck in a labyrinth, running around Phantom Isle with a bounty on her head or fetching berries on an epic quest, and it was time to take a break.

Her blonde hair, highlighted in various light shades by the unseasonal sunlight, had been pulled back into a weaved ponytail by a scrunchie, and her petite frame was adorned by a strapless, coral dress which fell below her knee caps. Honestly, Alice disliked the feel of sand between her toes and caking on her feet, but this was a tourist spot of great renown. The sand was not gold, or even replaced by painful stones, but literally white. Smiling behind prescription, designer sunglasses, her blue gaze hunted out a sunbed over which she threw over a red, patterned towel. It was situated underneath a parasol, and the teenager stripped down to a one piece swimsuit. It was mostly black, with the hip sections cut out and the chest designed in red plaid. It was sexy, and she smiled, satisfied at her purchase choice.

Swinub probably wouldn’t appreciate the heat or terrain too much, but the newest addition to her team would need the life experience. A small hand reached into the glittery black handbag now hanging from the umbrella, happily releasing an electric mouse. It was positively tiny, the salty coastal breeze bristling her static yellow fur and wafting against perked, jagged ears. The pokemon was a Pichu, only a few weeks old. After having a brief look around in fascination, she noticed her owner with a big smile, leaping up onto the sunbed, where she was applying her sunblock.

“Hello, baby.” Alice beamed warmly down at her baby, wiping the remains of the cream onto her feet, petting the cuddly rodent, who nuzzled her. Pichu giggled, and, moving onto the blonde’s stomach, began sniffing and taking in the view. “This is a beach. It’s a very famous beach in Cascadia, called Irdi Beach. I think we’ve been through a lot recently, so we’re going to play here and relax. We can sunbathe, read, make sand castles and swim in the sea! But we have to be careful not to stay in the sun too long or go too far out to sea, okay?” She asked. Her Pichu mumbled a short acknowledgement, still baffled by the beauty and oddness of the new surroundings. It wasn’t so busy during school time, so it felt less cramped, which the former student could certainly appreciate.

“We’ll go and swim in a bit, but I want to nap first.” Alice added, flopping her arms over the back of the bed, sighing tiredly. “I’m lazy by nature, you know.”
“Pi Pichu.”

Yes, actually. She did already know.

CASCADIA​
Lily Island
With a totally different landscape from the first, Lily Island is the second largest of the archipelago. Completely covered with vegetation, this island was also formed by a volcano. However, erosion has taken this island down to almost flat, and giving many homes to all types of grass and bug Pokemon among others. Inside the dense jungle, which takes up about a half of the island, there is a very small lake. There are two different forests on the island, and you can tell when you've gone from one forest to another. Besides being divided by the Diamond River, some strange force causes them to have completely different climates as well...
The Silver Forest - The forest which takes up a bit more than half of the island, Silver Island is named for the silver veins the leaves are adorned with. A deciduous forest, you'll mainly see flying and bug types here, due to the lack of ground vegetation. There is a rest stop somewhere in the forest, helping trainers on their way.
Click to expand...


Quinn pushed into the perimeter of the forest, just inside the treeline. He paused and scanned his surroundings, trying to get used to the subtle movements of sunlight through the canopy, to familiarize himself with the sounds. He stepped slowly, always mindful of his footing, constantly on edge about his surroundings. He didn't have a feel for this place yet, and had heard that this island could be extremely dangerous- not so much where he was now, but should he venture too far...

His hand fell to one of the pokeballs clipped to his belt, the one housing Banzai. He left him up for now, as calming as the thought of having one of his pokemon out and ready was. With Banzai, one always lived with the risk of him being distracted and charging off into a battle without informing his trainer.

On the other side was Nook- not as strong as Banzai, quite, but a good deal more likely to stick beside Quinn. Usually, at least. One could never tell with a Zigzagoon who fancied himself a scientist of some sort. With Pooka the Diglett still being seen after, however, it was just the three of them for now.

In any event, Quinn was just thankful to be doing something, risky or not, and- with a final deep breath to calm himself- began to step deeper into the forest.

Keith suddenly perked up as Jon mentions a clan of Trubbish within the cave, but resisted the urge to ask for directions to the Trubbish clan, intent on focusing on finding Sakura. Then he realized the full significance of something Jen brought up. A Seviper nest... Wasn't Sakura scared to death of Seviper?

"Guys, we should probably take this tunnel," Keith stated. "Make sure Sakura isn't anywhere near that Seviper nest." He felt for a Poke Ball on his belt, not to detach it, but just to reassure himself that it was still there. Which it was. It contained Gemini, his Weezing, and the strongest Pokemon he had on hand right now. He knew that if any Seviper gave them trouble, Gemini would be able to help out. Though he made no mention of Trubbish, Keith secretly hoped that they would also stumble upon the Trubbish clan. Trubbish as a species have always fascinated him. Then he addressed Jon and Jen. "Oh, and the exit for the cave is just back that way," he said, pointing the way they had come from. "Just follow that tunnel and you'll be out within minutes."
