OOC: Congratulations on being the ZO of the newest zone in BMG!

It was just another day on Lily Island, in the Silver Forest. For a week, Soruta and her 'tour guide', Saito, although not much of a tour guide at all, had been crawling through the forest for anything but the usual. This day would probably turn out to be like any other day, with the same 24 hour routine. Wake up in the morning, release all of the Pokemon to have breakfast out of whatever that was edible and not a Pokemon at the same time, return everyone but either Saito or Meiru, travel around some more... Then there came Lunchtime... I'm sure you get where this is going right now, huh?

Rewind to one week ago, upon arrival on the Lily Island. When Soruta had first arrived on this island by riding on Faltzer over a ridiculously long distance over a sea of water (and thus leaving Faltzer exausted and out of any battles for the first three days on the island, not that there were any battles with the native Pokemon during that time), the entire island was pristine, and the air was fresh. The prospect of abnormal silver leaves growing all over the forest was an exciting prospect, but as time went on without incident, she (and all of her Pokemon) began growing tired and fustrated with the daily scenery.

Mabye it's because Soruta keeps making left-hand turns, and never wants to turn right into the heart of the forest. "Is there something here? Or there? Which way should we go to find something besides the ordinary?" she would often ask throughout the day. If Saito happened to be out at that time, he would just shrug his shoulders. If Meiru was out instead of Saito, she'd tilt her head in confusion, too young to ponder about what her trainer is thinking about. All of the other Pokemon would give the same response if she happened to be asking this question during a meal, which usually involved thinking that she was going to be the death of them all if this kept going on.

Fast forward back to the present time. Soruta was traveling through the forest once more, with Saito just moping behind her, waiting for the total boredom to end. And yet Soruta had to say something before the day started that Saito could remember... To an extent.

"But if we go someplace else in the forest, whatever might be in the forest might show up at where we all were searching this past week while we all are gone searching someplace else, just like a Whack-a-Mole game. If you just hold a mallet over one hole and don't find anything, and switch over to another hole just to find that the mole just went to the previous target hole... That's not much of a good thing, is it?"

But even then, most of the other Pokemon couldn't understand what she meant by that. The only Pokemon that did play Whack-a-Mole was Saito during his time in the Cloud Garden, fighting against several Diglett and a ravenous Dugtrio. But Whack-a-Mole is supposed to be fun, not life-threatening like it was back then.

All Saito could do was sigh and continue following his trainer, for there would be no point in going somplace else without Soruta around. Her reasoning must be in another dimension now, as all of the other Pokemon might have put it by now. And yet, from behind both Soruta and Saito, you could see them dissapearing into their normal routines, hoping for something out of the ordinary. Going towards that same clump of trees with the same silver colored leaves, they are.

Going... Going... Gone...
