Horrified, it all dawns on you. This was a set-up all along! As the Aggron makes his way inside the cave – was it the one causing the Earthquakes before? -, it becomes increasingly evident that the only way out now is fighting.

What do you do?

There is no way to be certain, and while some of this may well be superstition on the part of Cam, he is a Pokemon Ranger with certified credibility, and these events are definitely unnatural. On one hand, you can follow his advice and seek a safer adventure on a nearby island, which is only a ferry ride away. On the other hand, if Cam is right, Ion Island may be on the brink of disaster and who knows what might happen even to the rest of the Archipelago if another battle takes place…

Not entirely unexpected, Melissa thought, especially when she considered some of the things her own Pokemon could do. Cam continued, "Some details have been lost in time, but there are undeniable records of two immensely powerful creatures having waged war against one another centuries ago, and their clash was so powerful that impossible phenomena occurred. Among them was the electrification of non-conducting materials like sand. Those charges wore off overtime, while some others persisted to give the island its name. But with the currents running through the isle with increasing intensity once more, us Rangers have been ordered to keep tourists near the shoreline. If we put the island on lockdown without concrete evidence, we could be raising a false alarm. But as it stands, we need to keep visitors safe just in case while avoiding general panic…" Melissa stared in concern as Cam glanced at his feet, appearing to struggle to find words to accurately describe whatever he wanted to tell her. He looked nervous, as though he were trying to find the words to soften the blow. She was just about to tell him to just tell her when he apparently decided to just say what was on his mind.

"If you ask me Melissa, if you want my true feelings on the matter… You’d better leave Ion Island right now, because Zapdos and Raikou are about to clash again." A chill ran down her spine at the bombshell and her mind blanked out in shock, restarting after a slight pause. Had he just said...Zapdos? And...Raikou? How was he so sure? Had these conditions been recorded in those ancient records or was that just a guess by the Rangers? She exchanged a glance with Wildfire, who'd glanced between the two of them at the mention of the Legendaries. He looked a little nervous, which she figured was understandable. The two had magnetized an entire island for years after their confrontation and now there was the possibility of them starting it up again?

Part of her wanted to leave immediately, stating that it was a fight between Legendaries and that there was undoubtedly little she could to do prevent it from happening. Another part of her was having no part of that. It wanted her to stick around, see if she could do anything at all, and, if not, then just to catch a glimpse of the two Legendaries. After all, it was extremely rare to stumble across one of the powerful creatures; what were the chances she'd have stumbled across two? Conflicted, she leaned down to pick up her starter, looking at him as she quietly said, "A part of me wants to leave and not give this another thought; another wants me to stick around to see if I can help. The rest of me doesn't know which way I should go. On the one hand, it's a pair of fighting Legendaries. What could we, a newly-independent adventuring pair, do to stop them? On the other hand, it's a pair of Legendaries! How often will we come across them in our adventures in the first place?" She stared out over the lake, too lost in thought to appreciate the sight as she had earlier. "What do you think?"

Wildfire observed Melissa as her thoughts ran in circles again. Personally, he didn't want to be there at all if Zapdos and Raikou really were going to fight. He doubted any of them were ready to go up against whatever challenges they'd have to go through to convince the two to stop or even the two themselves, if it came down to it. But he could agree with his friend that a part of him didn't really want to go if there was something they could do to prevent the fight from happening. After all, the two were powerful and they wouldn't care about holding back if they were focused on beating each other. He sighed to himself; he certainly hadn't expected to get into so much excitement once they left their family, but at least they'd have stories to tell. Provided they survived, that is.

Melissa was brought back to herself when she felt Wildfire climb back up to his customary position on her left shoulder. With a wry smile, she said, "Guess we're sticking around?" She felt him nod and nodded slowly in response. "Guess we're sticking around," she told Cam firmly.
