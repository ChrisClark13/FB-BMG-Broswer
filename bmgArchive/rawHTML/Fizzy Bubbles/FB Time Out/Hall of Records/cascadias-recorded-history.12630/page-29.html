“S-sorry,” she sniffed into Stacey’s chest.

Receiving congratulations for such a silly (but admittedly effective) strategy from someone as skilled and sophisticated as Stacey made Jessica blush and rub her neck. She wondered if the woman remembered where the idea had inspired from, and flushed in further embarrassment upon recalling the specifics of the situation. A fond and humorous memory served to help alleviate the current circumstances though, and she giggled as Stacey wagged a knowing finger with a wink.

There was something else Stacey had said that stuck though: What did she mean by “trying to save a dead girl”? Jess glanced at the second lady in white, who was watching them without comprehension. After a moment of awkward silence, a shadow fell over the trio and Jess looked up to find Senior Agent Gouka standing over them. She shrank in anticipation of anger – or worse, arrest. To her surprise though, he offered to aid their standing. After seeing Stacey slowly accept, so did she (with their free arms, they guided the third to her feet as well). The man then turned to Jess and cleared his throat.

“I apologize for my earlier approach. Had I realized you were one of them, I would have instead requested your collaboration instead of turning you away like a regular civilian.”

Jessica cocked her head in confusion, comforted at least that she wasn’t being reprimanded. As he continued to commend her abilities, she shuffled again sheepishly, half-hiding behind Stacey as if to somehow give her the credit. When he asked if there was anything she wished him to search for inside the building, she opened her mouth to inquire about the missing sister, though something in her gut told her it would prove fruitless…

Just then, as if to save her from one form of humiliation, five police officers busted belatedly through the door, striving their best to appear professional despite previous blunders regarding repeat security breaches. They grabbed the group of “civs”, prepared to take them to the “p-precinct” for questioning. While Stacey snapped and struggled, her Kabutops sharpening his scythes as he glared at the nearest who dared try to restrain his trainer, Jessica sagged and gave herself up easily. Sergeant Agent Gouka spoke up on their behalf though, allowing them to be forcibly dismissed, but at the same time defending their innocence and also advising against the use of handcuffs. Before leaving, he strongly shook Jessica’s hand and called her a user. (She noticed Stacey wrinkle her nose at the term, though apparently out of disgust rather than recognition.) The expression he wore seemed entirely foreign to his facial lines, but an earnest effort nonetheless.

His subordinates swiftly ushered them away before she could question the man’s meaning. Though perhaps they were following orders more out of fear for their throats, as the rock fossil remained leering at them as they escorted the ladies out. Less menacing in exterior but just as loyal, Haruki flanked the opposite side as they walked, while Atzu brought up the rear.

Once outside, an elderly gentleman approached to gratefully greet the woman in white. Jessica tensed when he took the frail thing’s hand and held her close, suspicious of such a shady character prowling the perimeter, perhaps lurking in wait to take advantage of victims during a period of instability. Especially after what they just went through, she wasn’t willing to trust a stranger presenting kindness off the bat. Call her paranoid, but considering they were a party of three young women probably looking worse for wear, one of whom was obviously weak and easily singled out as a target… They were surrounded by police though (useless as they may be), and Stacey seemed somewhat familiar with him. Plus he referred to “Felicia” by name and assumed responsibility for her actions, so Jessica relaxed, presuming there must be some close relation between the two. Judging by his age, she would’ve guessed grandfather, though she supposed parenthood wasn’t outside the realm of possibility.

She could perceive Stacey’s internal struggle with the decision whether to stay for an explanation, or put the past and all similar painful experiences behind as planned by this supposed vacation. Jessica would agree with whatever her companion chose, should it ease her mind, though her own curiosity desired to know more. So she was secretly glad when Stacey agreed to a conversation, and simply nodded silently in concurrence. Upon suggestion of a towel, her lips lifted as well. She felt a slight pressure in her palm, and squeezed back.

Stacey then released Dwemer to fill him in on the current state of affairs, and returned Jessica’s own Poké Balls to her. Jess quickly called out Kristen to reassure her as well. The little Snorunt leapt into her trainer’s arms and snuggled close, happy she was safe. Jessica cuddled and rocked in return, then turned to her Corsola and Azumarill, withdrawing them for a well-deserved rest. Realizing she forgot to thank Stacey for looking after her team, she shifted uncomfortably, wondering if she had missed her chance to show common courtesy, let alone communicate her thoughts (as was typical). She hastily stammered her appreciation, hoping it wasn’t too late.

We figure he should technically be the equivalent to a Colonel by now, and a lot of the men hate being bossed around by a Navy guy, so we call him Colonel between ourselves to make his orders easier to bear… What’s a regiment without its pride?" There are also some supplies in there, you should stock up. The rest of the crew will be by the gate, I’ll introduce you when you get there!"

Auric nodded to Sid. "Alright. Thank you for your help Private." With Sid now departed, Auric made his way to the tent and stepped inside. What he saw made him do a double take. Who ever these guys were they were well equipped. The tent was lined with shelves of supplies. They may be struggling out here but they weren't in dire straits for surplus just yet. Still he didn't want to pull too many resources from them. Well let's get this done. he thought to himself moving to the PC. It was a step up from the model PC used in Pokemon centers. Looking through his team he saw each of their profile pictures flashing on the screen. He paused on Pandora's picture for a minute. "I promise I'll be back for you Pandora. Please be ok..." he spoke quietly to himself before minimizing her profile.

He had to consider the conditions outside and who would be viable out there. 'Hyperion couldn't fly in this cold, Athene would freeze, Pontus and Eris are too inexperienced. Zephy and Perseus don't have the greatest traction as it is, Chiron's too heavy for the ice to support his weight, Midas would freeze up, Phrixus would be scared of all these people and I couldn't trust Thanatos around other people' He thought to himself, minimizing window after window. It didn't take him long until the screen only had a few profiles left standing.

Charizard- Ares
Houndoom- Iris
Nidoking- Odysseus

It was a decent line up. With Pandora down his team was lacking another ice type, so he'd have to rely on Pokemon who were their own heat source or simply too rugged to be bothered by the cold arctic air. Plus with Iris along for the ride Ares wouldn't have so much weight on his shoulders if they were to get into trouble. Powering down the PC Auric turned his attention to the supplies. On the first shelf he came to he picked up 2x Potions, and 2X Ice heals. A few feet away on a bottom shelf he grabbed 1X Escape rope just in case they ran into another situation where they were backed into a corner. Not wanting to over take he left the tent and headed back towards the gate.

It wasn't a huge crowd but it was much larger then Auric was used to working with. Private Gapp walked over and handed Auric an Orange overcoat. It wasn't really Auric's style, but his current jacket was still frozen. So Auric smiled and took the coat. Slipping it on he noticed a purple 'N' on the sleeve. Nebula he thought to himself as Kessler headed over to the group. When Kessler gave an order his people were quick to follow. Everyone pairing off onto snowmobiles. Private Gapp motioning for Auric to his snowmobile. Here we go was all he could think as the fleet of snow mobiles sped off into the night.

The adventure was on. Commander Kessler leading his team deep into the unknown tundra. At least this time his coat wasn't frozen. Still, there was an opportunity to be had being stuck on this snow mobile with the nicest member of this group he had talked to. Time to see if he could gather some information. Maybe they were good, or maybe not, but Auric didn't have enough information yet to make a call either way and there might not be another time.

"Sorry if this is overstepping a little, but I have to ask. What is Nebula and what the hell are you guys doing out here? I mean I know you're looking for certain strains of Pokemon DNA, and live specimens have been hard to come by, but what is all this for?" Auric asked hoping the sound of the engine would drown out their conversation to the others.
