Stu then turned to the guidebook he had of Cascadia and began to wonder what area of Ruin Island to head...they all seemed to ancient and mystic but two stuck in his head. The Amor Temple and the Ordial Shrine....the temple was ancient and overrun with Psychic Types, yet the Ordial Shrine had a dreamy feel to it which could lead to some fun.

One of the more interesting areas of the archipelago, Ruin Island is also the most mysterious. Giant stone statues and buildings, eroded by time, stand in the overgrowing vegetation of the area, providing a home to many Pokemon, especially those who prefer dark, enclosed spaces. The sea was calm, waves lapping up against the beach almost lovingly. A Ferry chugged along slowly, stopping shy of the golden sands, its bold and bright surface glistening with sprayed with droplets of salt water. Steps gently lowered to the ground below and excited trainers rushed off, heading in all directions. One trainer was left standing at the exit to the Ferry. The young woman stared off in one direction, and as she looked up you could see a glazed look over the normally vibrant sapphire eyes. She had received some strange looks from the other trainers aboard the ferry but with the way she was dressed and the way she held herself it wasn’t surprising. The black hood of her short breast-length jacket was pulled over her face with a purple symbol of the ghosts in its centre, the few strands of brown and black hair that escaped were tousled gently by the wind, the same wind that caressed her bare skin. Her grey shorts hugged her legs and her low cut silver top seemed to provide no warmth in the youthful evening.

Without as much as a word she moved forward, descending the steps with a graceful agility, one you wouldn’t assume of someone with such a look in their eyes. She reached the bottom of the steps, her feet sinking slightly in the warm sand. The Ferry pulled off and she was left on the beach, the gentle waves splashing against her black ¾ length boots, the odd drop landing on her mid thigh-high stockings. A blink, a step, followed by another and she was standing on more stable ground. Her hand reached down to caress a pokeball on her black and silver belt that hung low on her hips. A loud pop echoed as the ball snapped open, the pokemon inside flying out with amazing speed into the ruins. A tilt of the head, and yet more steps heading off the beach and through the buildings and ruins that littered the island. The released pokemon flittered in and out, never in one place for long; a purple blur here and there.

Soon after a short walk in silence, the only sounds being the beat of leathery wings and the woman’s black half skirt flapping behind her as she walked they arrived at a large stone structure, worn by age. The glazed look in her eyes appeared to diminish for a moment as she looked upon the rock with something akin to recognition. The purple blur reappeared flying above its trainers shoulder. The Zubat looked enthralled and circled the rock squealing in delight at the blood splatter still present on its surface.

“Oh this place reeks of death. The blood, oh it sings to me of torment and torture. The Gods, the Gods they demand the screams; they demand life rich and tasty. Mommy has brought me to such a wonderful playground.” Drusilla chanted and clucked; an amusing noise for a bat and flittered around the rock. Stacey bent down and ran her fingers along the blood stains, enjoying the feel of the stone beneath her fingertips, tracing the pattern with care.
