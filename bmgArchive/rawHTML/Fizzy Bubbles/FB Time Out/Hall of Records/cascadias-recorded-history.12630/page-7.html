Ruin Island

Oniero Ruins; Silver Wind –
Eager to start your adventures in Cascadia, you and your drowsy sloth decide to head off to the Oniero Ruins, one of the more mysterious ruins on this generally enigmatic island. After about twenty minutes of walking down the dusty ancient path to your destination, you arrive at the ring of strange rocks. The moment you step within a few feet of them, you feel a wave of relaxation rush over your body. Even the already slow breathing of Delilah seems to slow to a breath-a-minute pace as you step increasingly closer to the stones. In the sunlight, the rocks seem to shimmer, as if wet. As you approach them, everything seems to slow greatly, and your mind drifts off into a dreamlike condition, floating along like a boat upon calm seas. The moment you step inside the circle of stones, you feel light, and every part of your body numbs. All you can see now is the circular stone slab in the center of the glimmering white rocks, which seems untouched by the ages. Vines wrap around it in a spiral pattern, which is perfectly uniform as if it had been created by man.

Slowly, you walk up to it. All of the sudden, you have the urge to touch it. Deep in the back of your mind, your conscious shouts “No! Don’t!” but the cry is lost in the dream, and becomes but an echo of a thought. You, very gradually, reach out towards the rock, fingers spread, hoping to just to place at least one finger upon it. But, before you can put finger to stone, a voice calls out from behind. “I wouldn’t do that if I were you!” Immediately, you snap out of your trance. You double take to see that a woman stands just outside of the ring of rocks. To you, she looks to be in her mid thirties, with a somewhat muscular, yet still retaining a delicate frame and air. Her mousy brown hair is tied back in a long pigtail which reaches her shoulders. A few loose strands drape sit around her deep green eyes, small rounded nose, and average-sized lips. She looks like she fit the picture perfectly, with her many pocketed forest green and tan vest, her sandy brown cargo pants, and her deep tan boots. Quickly, she picked up a pebble and tossed it at the stone. The moment it came within an inch of it, the pebble was suddenly reflected. When it settled again in the grass, it smoked, having been charred by the energy surrounding the black slab in the center.

You turn towards the woman, grateful and relieved. You thank her, and ask her name. “My name’s Amber. I’ve been studying these rocks for a little over a year now.” She responded. “Here, come on out of the ring. It’ll be a little easier to think.” Taking her advice, you leave the formation of rocks, and the moment you step outside of it, your mind clears instantly. “Better, isn’t it?” You nod, now able to think much more clearly. “The natives of this island say that these rocks contain a potent energy which flows from existence itself. I was just here to do my daily observation when I saw you getting too close to the center stone. I must admit, the feeling is quite relaxing, but I guess I’ve gotten used to it after a year or so. So, what, might I ask, were you doing out here?”

You explain to here that you were just out adventuring and thought that these ruins sounded like they would be interesting. “Well, that’s how I stumbled upon these beauties,” she says nostalgically, placing her hand on the white stone to her right. “I love ruins. I’ve been studying the remains of former civilizations for about 5 years now, and it absolutely fascinates me.” She paused for a moment, starring at the shining stones before her. “So, I assume you want to learn more about these ruins? Well, would you like to come back to my cabin? I have a mammoth amount of notes on these as well as a few notes on the legends behind them.”

What will you do?


Crater Island

Pearl Lake; Jon Jen – After escaping from the hustle and bustle of the island’s pier, you head towards the crater lake which is the namesake of this island. A few minutes walk brings you to the relaxing shore of this large body of water, which shimmers in the bright sunlight. Right below the surface, Remoraid and Goldeen swim among the colonies of Clamperl which rest upon the lake’s bottom, most closed, with the occasional few revealing its mother-of-pearl head to the world. Huntail and Gorbyss dance in circles around the lake, gracefully gliding through the water like birds through the air. Everything here is so relaxing. You fall upon the sandy lakeshore, listening to the cries of the aquatic Pokémon around you.

However, suddenly, you detect something around you. It seems that the inhabitants of the lake too can sense this strange change in atmosphere. The Remoraid and Goldeen unexpectedly stop swimming, and flee for their aquatic shelters. The Huntail and Gorbyss break ranks and start to become aggressive towards each other, taunting and snipping at one another. The Clamperl become extremely confused, letting out cries, and opening and closing their shells rapidly. Looking desperately for the cause of the commotion, you espy a slight disturbance in the middle of the lake. In the center, the water pulses, lightly at first, but swiftly intensifies into a rapid throb in the heart of the lake. Unexpectedly, the water of the lake becomes pitch black, and a strange shadow rises out of the pulsation. It seems to turn towards you, but all you can hear, see or otherwise before you go out cold is a shrill cry.

Suddenly, you find yourself standing in a completely dark place. You don’t know where you are, who is there, or what is happening, but you do know one thing for sure, you can’t see a thing. To test your vision, you place a hand in front of your face, but not even that can be seen in the pitch blackness. Then, you hear the same cry as you did before you were knocked out, but this time it seems to be threefold. Slowly, you walk towards the source of the shouts for help, and as you get closer to them, they start to become audible, and eventually become distinguishable words.

“Please! Please save us!” they cry, “Please free us from the shadows! We have kidnapped by the evil darkness, and they will not let us go! You must find our gemstones by the three lakes of Cascadia and ward of the shade to rescue us! Please help! Please help!”

You awaken. Rubbing your eyes to regain your vision, you stand, slowly, and find yourself upon the same lakefront you were what seemed moments ago. Stars now hang lightly in the sky, some covered by the wisp of a cloud, and the moon shines a bright silver light upon the now normal water of the lake. Not a soul moves; even the Huntail have gone in for the night. You seem to recall the cries for help from your dream, but you’re not exactly sure what to do. Looking for some help from your surroundings, you survey the area, and something catches your eye. A tiny stone seems to be inset into a larger rock on the other side of the lake. You probably could walk around the lake without much problem. What will you do?

 Ruin Island

Oniero Ruins; Silver Wind – Finding nothing else better to do, you decide to go with Amber to her cabin amongst the ruins. As you walk up to it, you find it to be pretty run-of the mill. A simple wood-frame house with a cobblestone chimney, piping smoke from its humble flue, and space for maybe two people maximum sits amongst ancient stones and ruins. “Come on, in, make yourself at home.” She beckons you inside, opening the green wood door in desperate need of a new coat of paint. Both of you step into a fairly small room, about the size of a bedroom, which either looks like a tornado ripped through it, a bomb exploded in it, or an exploding tornado tore through it. Regardless of which simile you choose to describe it with, it’s extremely messy. Papers are sprawled across tables, littered across the floor, tacked on walls, stuffed in drawers, even on the fan which squeaked overhead as it rotated on slightly loose screws.

“Sorry for the mess, but they say that a mess shows you’re smart.” Amber chuckled at her own comment, but you give her an odd look, and she stops laughing. “Now… where did I put those…” she mumbles, as she starts to dig through a pile of notes and folders on her desk on the opposite wall. “Ah, here it is.” She pulls out a file of papers, all shockingly neatly arranged and bound quite professionally. “Here you are. I think you’ll find this to be interesting.” You take the file, and open the tan cover, seeing a nicely typed collection of legends. Seeing that the report was quite thick, you take a glance at it, flip to the table of contents, and go straight for the juicy part of the paper.

You begin to read.

“It is said that from the ring of rocks which has been dubbed the ‘Oniero Ruins’ by those studying them that the large black stone in the center emits a very potent power, the origin of which is unknown. Because of this power, when one enters the ring of stones, they suddenly fall into a state which starts to cause their brain to go into a sleep-like state, as if the person were sleepwalking. Additionally, the rock in the center also has a force field of sorts surrounding it, and radiates a high-heat energy, which could easily melt lead if it was left upon it. Surprisingly, however, a species of ivy which is not found anywhere else in the world still manages to grow upon the rock in a distinct spiral shape which has never been seen elsewhere.

Additionally, many other strange phenomenons have been reported to happen from time to time. One local report claims that a large flame was spotted upon the center stone, which remained for twenty minutes, until it suddenly extinguished, leaving no sign of any sort of combustion or burning. One couple reported two separate incidents, one of the ring of rocks being flooded with water which wouldn’t leave the boundary of the rocks themselves, and one of the vines in the middle growing to take over the whole ring of rocks, but not going even a centimeter outside of it. Various other reports have been filed, such as finding strange feathers around the ruin, the ruin icing over on a particularly hot day, and the ground suddenly splitting around the rocks, and then mending itself again.

There are various legends surrounding the rocks and how they got their. Some say that these rocks were constructed by the ancients as monument to nature. Some say that these rocks suddenly appeared one day out of no where. Many say that they were created by a Pokémon at the beginning of time, but no names of this Pokémon were ever mentioned. One thing that seems to be ubiquitous among all the stories though is that the stones are somehow related with creation, or even in some cases, the creator itself.”

Suddenly, as you finish the sentence, the whole house begins to shake. The teapot which Amber was busy filling slips from her hands and makes a loud clang as it hits the sink. “What was that?” she asks, looking out the window. The shaking increases and papers start to fall from their perches, wrecking the already messy room. Curiosity getting the best of her, she runs outside, trying to avoid as many of her fallen notes as possible. You track her out the door, then stand and follow her out, also curious to this sudden tremor. The moment you step outside, though, she’s already run off to the ruins to inspect the situation.

Will you go with her to investigate the ruins?
