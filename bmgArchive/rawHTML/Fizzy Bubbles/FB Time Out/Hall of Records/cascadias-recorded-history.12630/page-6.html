A wistful look had appeared on the man's face. "When I was a Gym Leader, nothing was more exciting than a battle, every time a challenger came I'd do all I could to keep my badges... and that's what it came down to to me. Protecting my badge, my honor. It gave me a will to win, and I did - every battle, all the time. You want to know why you couldn't beat me?" The man had chuckled. "Experience. Get some. Face some other Gym Leaders, but if you ever want a rematch make a name for yourself. I'll seek you out."

And since that day, James had trained twice as hard, fought with triple the will, and had begun to start winning. It was something he was unaccustomed to, but an incredible feeling came with it.

And so, James wished to keep winning. And if taking down this Leader would get him one step closer to battling and defeating the trainer who had earned his respect, so be it.

"Okay," James called, "Let's do this." In a flash, a pokeball has in his right hand, gripped tightly. He was tempted to send out Kodou, his golduck, but that took away the point of the battle... where was the rush in winning if the opponent had a handicap. He wanted a true battle, and so he would do his damnest to fight fairly. His magmar was a no, would absorb all fire attacks... his gengar, no, too high level... that left only his steel types. James grinned. So be it. "Necross, I choose you!"

In a torrent of red energy, the pokeball flew from James' hand, flying into the middle of the arena, a huge snake began to emerge. Its body spiked, and its jaw enormous, it fit the description of only one pokemon: steelix.

Type advantage, snorted James, what good's that when Necross is that big...

The solemn faced steelix grunted for orders, his head crouched down, facing the houndoom straight on. James grimaced. No need to get distracted, he told himself.

"Alright, start off by Slamming into the thing, then Binding it as fast as you can. Once you have it cornered and trapped, do a very close range Dragon Breath - focus it all on the houndoom... no need to be nervous."

The last part was more to himself than anyone else. No need to let anyone know that, though...
