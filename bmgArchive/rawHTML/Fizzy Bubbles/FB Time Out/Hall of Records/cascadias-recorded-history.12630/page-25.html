Brine Island​
Salin City - The only real city in the entire island chain, Salin City is huge. Taking up the entirety of Brine Island, Salin City has everything-from shopping places, to schools, hospitals, a Pokemon Center, an airport, and a seaport, among other things. There is a small park in the middle, with a fishing pond and a few trees scattered about. Stands line the streets wishing to sell you things, and it'd be pretty hard to not find something to do. But if you would happen to find yourself in such a dilemma, there is a Pokemon Gym in the city which holds eight leaders, one each to symbolize the Cascadian Archipelago. However, only one may be challenged per visit to the gym.

Profile​

Exiting the Pokémon Centre, Eric paused for a second to regain his bearings. Cars and pedestrians rushed up and down the many twists and turns that made up the intricate network of Salin City, shouts filling the air. It was all quite disorienting to this small town native of the Johto region, and he had found himself lost many times on the way to the Centre. Eric's arrival to Cascadia was otherwise uneventful, but he hoped to remedy this by exploring much of what the city had to offer. Pulling out a Poké Ball, he gazed at it with a small smile before throwing it up into the air. "Come on out, Cypress!" A flash of light illuminated Eric's surroundings, replaced a moment later with a gangly Bellsprout bobbing back and forth. A mischievous smile lit up the grass type's face upon seeing Eric; the plant swelled up to its full height before swaggering over to stand next to his trainer. Ever since his capture, Cypress had been intent on showcasing his superiority everywhere he went, never stopping to consider how ridiculous he might look. He was also quite the prankster, delighting in tricks and jokes that take advantage of others.

"Well, Cypress, here we are. Salin City!" Eric exclaimed, biting back laughter. "There's so much to do here, it's hard to decide where to start, don't you think?" Cypress shrugged his leafy arms, leading the way down the packed street. Vendors lined the road, boasting their wares to passerby. Eric paused at a few to look at the nice trinkets they had to offer, but Cypress continued to shoulder his way through the crowd. After about five minutes, the duo turned down a narrow side street. An intersection crowned the end of it, past which a park could be seen. Cypress seemed intent on traveling through this wooded area, crossing the street quickly. "Bell-Bellsprout," he called, turning back to Eric and gesturing for him to keep up. Doubling his pace with a sigh, Eric followed Cypress under the trees...
