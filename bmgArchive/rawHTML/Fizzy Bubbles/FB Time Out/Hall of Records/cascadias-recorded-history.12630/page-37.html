After climbing precisely 500 steps, you finally reach the entrance, legs hurting and heart thumping. The front gate, massive and imposing, made of once-finely sculpted rock, is covered in vines and foliage, indicating nobody has opened it likely in hundreds of years. Confused, you decide to step inside nonetheless. After all, it’s why you came here in the first place. But it makes no sense for the gate to open itself that easily when it showed clear signs of having remained untouched for centuries! You decide it’s best to let it go for now and focus on your new surroundings. The entrance hall is a massive room kept alit by three chandeliers hanging from the ceiling, each housing several slumbering Litwick. Their flames reveal the entirety of the area, from the elaborate stone walls to the two doors on the far end of the room. The gravures on the walls have faded with time, giving you no hints about the history of this place, but something does catch your eye and only serves to add to your lingering questions…

A dead body!

In the center of the room is the corpse of what you can only assume was another adventurer. And on closer inspection, he barely shows signs of decomposition, as if… he’d passed away only recently! But in that case, why did it seem as if the Temple hadn’t been entered in ages?!
You force yourself to look at the man a bit more carefully, in case it could provide any clues as to what happened. He looked to be roughly your age, light on possessions for someone risking his life in an abandoned temple; his pants are darker from the knee down, with traces of dried mud, as if he’d crossed a lake of some sort; his shirt bears numerous cuts and tears, along with minor traces of blood; and the cause of death soon becomes apparent when you inspect his neck and arm – several black needles have pierced his skin, likely containing fatal poison. A classic trap…

You notice he carried a backpack, but unfortunately it seems empty; he must have spent all of his provisions during the adventure, so there’s nothing left to salvage. Looking ahead, you inspect the two doors on the far wall a bit more carefully, as they seem to be the only way forward if you still want to explore the temple after this terrible discovery. The gates are identical and much like the images on the other walls, whatever used to be portrayed on these doors has long been eroded by time. Seemingly nothing sets them apart, until you notice that a small puddle of water beneath the gate on the left, and thin vines emerging from beneath the one on the right.

With so little to go on, which door do you choose to pass through?

Apollo is notoriously uncomfortable in this environment, bothered by the excessive humidity, but follows nonetheless as does Don.

The atmosphere is oppressive. Not even your own steps make a sound, muffled by the vineyard underneath, to the point where silence feels deafening - the overused cliché applies in this particular setting as sound of any nature would be much more welcoming than the ominous void you traverse.

A thought lingers in the back of your mind: what could have happened to that adventurer at the entrance? Your guess is that he died after crossing the other corridor, which lead you to choose the one you're currently in, but why should this one be any better? What kind of secrets are worth protecting to the point of setting up measures that can kill a man? That is what so many Trainers often forget; the life of an adventurer is much more than the clean-cut Pokemon Battles shown on TV. A true traveler is always in as much danger as his Pokemon, if not more, making most journeys a lot more bittersweet than they imagined when they left home.
But you are a seasoned Trainer by now. You've grown accustomed to the quirky nature of the Fizzy Bubbles, the treacherous lands and fickle roads. No newcomer would dare explore the lost temples of the Cascadian Archipelago, but trust your long-time partners to keep you safe throughout the ordeal.

Rather than invite you to move further in, thick vines suddenly wrap around your ankles and all the way up to your knees, rendering you unable to walk! You quickly glance over at Apollo to find himself in the exact same predicament, while Don has become trapped by vines on the ceiling which bind his wings!

It's formed out of the hundreds of vines that cover the tunnel, as if it were one with the structure itself. "Hariiiiyama!" bellowed Helga. She too saw the opportunity and was not about to pass it up! She slammed her hand into Magmortar's shoulder, this time creating a shockwave so powerful that it actually knocked Magmortar over, actually caused it to roll precariously close to the end of the rock platform! Of course, the lava wouldn't do a damn thing to Magmortar, but still, the fact that Helga's attack had done that to such a huge beast, Keith couldn't help but be proud of his Pokémon. But Magmortar was far from done- it stood back up, and now aimed both of its cannons at Helga! Keith opened his mouth-

YOUR BLIND DESIRE TO REACH ME AT ALL COSTS... IT'S TIME YOU WITNESS THE PRICE OF YOUR STUBBORNNESS.

Before Keith could wonder just what the Great One meant by that, the answer came to him all too quickly, in the form of a loud rumbling sound right behind him. Knowing that this could be nothing good, he turned around and saw a horrible sight- the tunnel they had come through, it was caving in! Which in and of itself wouldn't have been so bad, except Keith realized with a horrible jolt that Kyle and Doduo were STILL INSIDE!

"No..." Keith gasped, but he knew already there was nothing he could do. The reporter and his Doduo were almost certainly dead by now. But more than that... Keith realized that this was his fault. Had he not just caved in himself and agreed to leave, they wouldn't have- wait, what was he thinking?! Keith turned and looked at the Heatmor- they still needed him. He had made them a promise, and there was no way in hell he was gonna break that promise. He owed it to those Heatmor to see this through, for even if he did concede defeat and leave, the Heatmor would definitely die, and how would he live with that knowledge? No. No, he couldn't give up on them. He had to stick to his convictions and do what he knew was right.

Keith exchanged glances with Meowth. "It weren't yer fault," he said to Keith, though his voice still shook. "Ya told him ta come wit youse, if he'd only listened he wouldn't'a been in dere."

Meowth's right, Gemini's voice echoed in Keith's head. You've been doing everything you could do for those Heatmor. None of the deaths that have happened are your fault. You did what Mustard and I knew you would do in the end, and we can give you no higher praise than that.

Keith knew that his Weezing was right. He knew that Meowth was right. He wouldn't be Keith if he didn't do everything he did up to this point. And then, another point of view occurred to Keith- this happened just after Helga had landed some particularly powerful hits against Magmortar. The Great One knew that his champion was losing, didn't he? He was trying to intimidate Keith out of following through just because he had a shot of winning, didn't he? And if there was even the slightest chance that Keith could save the remaining Heatmor from the fate they would otherwise be doomed to, he had to take it. Even if he was wrong, even if he was doomed to lose, then damn it, he would go out fighting!

NOW, DEFY ME AGAIN... SEE WHAT HAPPENS THEN!

Keith opened his eyes and looked at his Pokémon. Meowth, Gemini, Mustard, and Helga all gave small grins as they saw the look in their Trainer's eyes, the fiery determination he was known to possess. They knew in that instant that Keith wasn't about to call it quits. "Helga, let's do this," Keith said quietly. "For Kyle. For Doduo. For those Heatmor back there. For all those mutilated Koffing and Weezing back in that tunnel-"

WHAT?!

Two voices rang simultaneously in Keith's head. He turned, and sure enough, his Weezing's two heads registered identical appalled looks. It was then that Keith realized- Gemini and Mustard had not yet been filled in on the tunnel of Koffing and Weezing corpses. "I'm afraid so," he muttered to them. "Hundreds of them, maybe thousands, lining the walls of a tunnel, still giving off Poison Gas and Smog, being used in this sick, twisted version of Home Alone as a trap." The Weezing looked sickened- both heads did, and Keith couldn't blame either one of them. "And I swore to avenge them all," he continued. Gemini and Mustard nodded their approval simultaneously, still looking sickened.

"...For Kyle. For Doduo. For the Heatmor. For the Koffing and Weezing. Hell, even for that one Magmar that was getting ready to surrender," Keith continued, addressing Helga once again. "For each and every needless fatality at the hands of the sick, twisted Great One... Helga, use your Force Palm once more, then use your Smelling Salts attack... and follow it up with Brine." He had hoped to save the order for Smelling Salts until Force Palm had actually induced paralysis, but he knew that he had to get this battle finished before the Great One could do any more damage, end any more lives. He didn't know for sure that this would work, but he had to hope it would.

Mt. Aduro

Missingno. Master: The Mountain God’s threats aren’t empty after all. Sure, He’d shown little remorse in disposing of his own minions, and tried to do the same to you on multiple occasions so far, being foiled at every turn. But to actually see Him carry out his word, remorselessly killing the good-hearted Kyle Styles and his clumsy Doduo, who’d shared most of the trials with you… it brings His cruelty to a new level. The Great One yet again orders you to stand down and surrender, lest you want to suffer the same fate as the reporter, and is intent on taking the small group of rebel of Heatmor with you.

As if making good on his Master’s promise, the Magmortar unleashes a violent burst of fire that takes the shape of the oriental Daimonji, a wicked Fire Blast that explodes on contact with Helga; the shockwave itself is so severe, it tosses you and the entire group of Heatmor to the ground, while the Hariyama, having taken the full brunt of the blow, is sent flying across the arena! You watch helplessly as Helga’s body flies through the air, quickly realizing she’ll fall into the lava pit that circles the central chamber if she keeps going!
But just as hope beings to fade (how could you possibly grab such a huge Pokemon?), you catch a glimpse of something beginning to move through the corner of your eye – faster than you thought possible, the Heatmor leader gets back to his feet and dashes toward Helga! The anteater leaps and tackles the Hariyama from behind, stopping her momentum and causing the two Pokemon to fall back down before the edge of the platform!

Helga struggles back to her feet, mutters her thanks to Heatmor in her native tongue and switches her attention back to the giant Magmortar, her Guts fueling an unprecedented rage. She readies herself and lunges forward with her palm glowing, while Magmortar again chuckles in amusement and leaves itself wide open, curious to find out the true extent of its persistent little foe. This time, letting out a bone-chilling battle cry, the Hariyama rams her open hand into the magma Pokemon’s torso and the ensuing energy blast effortlessly topples the giant, who collapses backwards! Furthermore, it seems this time the aftereffect of Force Palm has taken effect – Magmortar’s limbs lock up during its attempt to get back up! Unwilling to let up, Helga gets on top of the fallen titan and repeatedly slaps it across the face, over and over again as if trying to wake an unconscious person up, but dealing heavy damage instead!

When she’s had enough, the heavyweight Pokemon steps away from her battered opponent, but the fire in her eyes remains. Stunned and hurting all over, Magmortar gets to one knee, gasping for air, wondering how could such a weakling be manhandling it this way… but before it can even think of mounting an offense of its own, Helga has already decided to wrap things up! She brings her hands forward, creating a blue orb between them, and releases a torrent of saltwater at the Fire-Type!

The water stream crashes into Magmortar and this time, thanks to the high pressure of the jet, it’s the enemy who goes flying back! Doused in seawater, the Magmortar collides with the rock wall at the edge of the huge circular arena, and when the Brine subsides, the creature’s massive frame detaches from the wall and tumbles into the magma surrounding the main chamber! For a while, nothing seems to happen, and you would imagine Magmortar to be relatively at ease inside the active volcano but… something surfaces, and it’s not the foe as you knew it just now! Helga bends over the edge and pulls out the incapacitated enemy – revealing that Magmortar’s body has been encased in a layer of pitch-black stone! Meowth is quick to explain that the water blast that drenched Magmortar must have caused the lava to solidify around the creature’s body, and because Helga was quick enough to pull it out, it never got to melt back over… Magmortar is unable to battle anymore, having been turned into a harmless statue! Helga gains 1 Level!

Your celebration, if any, is short-lived. While Helga helps the Heatmor leader back to his feet, thanking him again for essentially saving her life, another terrible quake makes the volcano’s main chamber shake wildly, the wide platform you all stand on trembling violently before a huge portion of the volcano throat’s wall falls off with a loud rumbling noise. You all instinctively look up to find…


I HAVE HAD ENOUGH OF YOU ALL. I WILL PUT AN END TO THIS NUISANCE MYSELF!

He didn’t need to speak for you to have known – you’re finally standing face to face with the Great One, the Mountain God and heart of Mt. Aduro! This is the one behind it all – not just Aduro’s continued existence, but the abhorrent “natural selection” imposed on the island, that has doomed so many Pokemon considered “unfit” to perish!

The gigantic creature, several times that of Magmortar, lets go of the wall and crashes down on the platform, causing half of it to crumble under His weight, allowing his legs to sink into the lava with no repercussions! The area you’re all standind on has just become twice as small, and the final foe has finally revealed Himself! But… can you hope to topple such a mythical being?

PUNY HUMAN. YOU DO NOT BELONG HERE, YET SEE FIT TO MEDDLE IN MY AFFAIRS – ADURO’S AFFAIRS! THE HEATMOR ARE NOT WORTHY OF DWELLING IN MY PARADISE. THEY ARE FATED TO DIE! BEHOLD!

With a deafening roar, the Great One causes a stream of lava to rise from the depths of the pit, as if controlling it mentally; the magma tendril wraps itself around one of the remaining Heatmor, and much to the leader’s despair… drags it into the boiling lava near the God’s feet, instantly incinerating the poor thing!

REMEMBER MY WORDS – “DEFY ME AGAIN, SEE WHAT HAPPENS NEXT!” THIS IS THE RESULT OF YOUR CONTINUED DEFIANCE! YOU WILL WATCH THEM ALL DIE, NOW THAT YOU’VE LURED THEM TO ME... AND YOU WILL BE THE LAST ONE TO MELT AWAY!

The final battle begins now! What will you do?!
