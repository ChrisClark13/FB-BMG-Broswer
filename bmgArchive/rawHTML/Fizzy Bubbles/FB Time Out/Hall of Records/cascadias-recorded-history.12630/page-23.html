CASCADIA​
Far off of the coasts of the mainland Fizzy Bubble zones, remote in the Silver Ocean, an island chain sits in basically, the middle of nowhere. These islands contain the some of the most diverse typing of Pokemon in the entirety of Fizzy Bubbles. All of these islands are actually volcanoes, but the majority of them have gone dormant. As many Pokemon spend all their lives here, many fossils of Pokemon and plants are buried in the depths of the volcanic ground, maybe some of these may be worth a look? You start out on the island that you first choose, via a ferry. In order to change areas afterward, Surf or Fly is a necessity.

Glacier Island
Named by the first of the explorers to the island chain, Glacier Island isn't even an island. It is basically a large iceberg sitting in the middle of the sea, which has provided a home to many ice types. Many have attempted to find out why the ice doesn't melt, but each of them has failed in turn. This is just one of the mysteries of this island that go unsolved. Possibly some clues lie in the depths of the iceberg?
Icicle Caverns - Dug by scientists and explorers, a large cavern dips deep into the ice chunk branches off into two a bit in. The temperature in here is much colder than the outside, reaching -50 degrees (F). This alone makes it hard for most Pokemon to endure, but the lack of light also makes this a very difficult area to explore, but the benefits are said to be worth it, as adventurers have returned bearing treasures.

"This place is so cold!" Cele heard the message clearly in his mind as Boing the Spoink telepathically spoke to him. There were lost in a frozen wasteland, not knowing where they where going. They had heard of an island archipelago named Cascadia, which was ripe for exploration. Cele was thrilled at the thought, and got to the shipyard quickly. Unfortunately, he was late, a product of sleeping in and getting in some last minute training. By the time he got there, he had missed all the ferries, except one, which was just leaving. Not knowing where he was going, he quickly asked Boing to Teleport him and himself on to the ferry. Boing did as instructed, and luckily, got the Teleport right, landing on the deck of the ferry. "Thanks, Boing." Cele told him as he caught his breath. Luckily, the fare for trainers was free, so he didn't have to bring out his money, which he supposed wouldn't be enough. "I wonder where this ferry is going anyway..." Cele wondered. "Let's wait and see."

After about half an hour, ice floes had begun to appear in the water, and it began to snow lightly. Cele had thought ahead and brought a coat, just in case, but he didn't see the immediate need to put it on. "Probably just a slight weather change, it'll be back to normal in no time." Cele told himself. In truth, he was getting a bit worried. Eventually, he saw a huge iceberg coming up in front of them. Remembering the Titanic, he began to panic. "Oh my god, everybody abandon ship!" he yelled, until he saw the boat stop next to it. He realized how silly he must have looked, and Boing hopped away from him slightly. "Oh, sorry everyone." he said sheepishly. "We have now arrived at Glacier Island. Enjoy." he heard the captain say. "Glacier Island?" Cele asked. He grabbed a brochure from the ship, one that he had never bothered to read, and looked up Glacier Island. "This actually looks pretty cool, don't you think Boing?" He handed the brochure to Boing. "Looks a bit cold. I say we wait for the ferry back." "Nonsense! This a great place to explore!" Cele said, while Boing was forced to agree. They stepped off the ferry and onto Glacier Island.

Upon leaving the ferry, Cele began to freeze and was forced to put on his coat to prevent hypothermia. Boing was a bit better off, his Thick Fat ability providing some insulation from the cold. They were in a frozen tundra, a barren wasteland, with no signs of life at all. They walked for what seemed like miles, but thankfully, there was no blizzard or anything of the sort. The happiness was short lived, as soon enough, a blizzard started up, and Cele grabbed his coat tighter. "I think I see a cave or something." Boing told Cele, who followed the glow of his pearl inside. When they finally got inside, Cele could see how cold Boing really was. He was shivering while he bounced, while a frightened look in his eyes. Cele took off his coat and put it around Boing, who bounced happily. "Wait. We're in a cave! This is awesome for exploration! Let's go Boing!" They set off to explore, but after going through the cave, they got tired quickly and had to rest. Boing later returned the coat, knowing that Cele needed it more than him. After a break, they set off explore the rest of the cave...
