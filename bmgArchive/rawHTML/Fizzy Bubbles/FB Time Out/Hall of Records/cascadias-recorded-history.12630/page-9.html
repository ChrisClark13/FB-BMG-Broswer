Ordial Shrine; Arc_Angel – (I hope you don’t mind me doing a little speaking on Stacey’s behalf. I just need to do this to keep this explanation from lasting 3 updates instead of 1.)

“Oh, I guess it would be appropriate to explain such a thing to a foreigner. That shrine was built by my ancestors generations ago to worship the Great Spirit Girtinia. Ever since its construction, a creature, human or Pokémon, was sacrificed to it when the sun was high in the sky. This ritual has become daily life for us, and symbolizes the circle of life and death that is constantly flowing through this great land. Usually, the sacrifices were voluntary, as it is considered a great honor to ascend into the Nether Realm under Girtinia’s guide. Inversely, we also see Cressila as a Great Spirit of Life. The legend states Cressila lives in the moon and watches over all of us. When a baby born, a piece of the moon falls from the sky and brings the baby to life. A temple was constructed in her honor, called Amor Temple. It is said that if either Girtinia or Cressila is corrupted, the other can come and purify it.

Stacey sits a moment and absorbs the new information.

“Why were you in such a hurry to get away from the shrine?” Stacey asks.

“Well, lately, being on that shrine isn’t the safest thing. While it was always generally not a good idea to ascend it if you weren’t going to execute or be executed, some governmental corruption has made it even worse. Not too long ago, King Amadoro came into power, and his policies haven’t been the cleanest. Some people have disappeared from their homes suddenly and shown up on the chopping block later, and, while the disappearances are seemingly random, there is one connection, their loyalty to Amadoro’s father, King Benolico. Although nothing is confirmed, it’s generally assumed that Amadoro overthrew his benevolent father in a twisted power play. When he sacrificed his own father to Girtinia, it was blatantly obvious that the handing down of the crown was not a spotless ordeal.

“Recently, there have been some… questionable sacrifices to Girtinia though. Many people who were thought to be missing have shown up on the chopping stone to die. The cover story is often that the new king imposed a new system which involves a variety of rituals that his father supposedly composed but never initiated, which accounts for their absences. He’s been using this ritual as an extermination tool which he can use at his whim to destroy threats while striking fear into anyone who may rise up and diverting anyone who is ignorant to his true motives.”

“Amadoro has been bad for us. Now, soldiers line the streets and anyone who is suspected of prior loyalty is in danger of death. Many people close to all of us were pulled into this horrible whirlwind of death, corruption and deception. In fact…” Ranjona paused momentarily, holding back a sniffle.

“My… my father and brother were both sacrificed by Amadoro.”

Stacey, moved by the story, offers a consoling hand.

“No, it’s… it’s ok. I’m where I am today because of it, and I know it’s for the better. You see, many people have suspected that he is not only corrupting his government, but Girtinia as well. We’ve all formed resistance we secretly refer to as ‘The Full Moon’ to try and overthrow Amadoro’s corrupt government. Our final goal is to summon Cressila, free Girtinia, and defeat Amadoro.” Ranjona paused momentarily, likely to give Stacey a moment to soak all of this information in.

“We have a safely guarded hideout not too far from here. We could use all the help we can muster. Would you help our endeavor?” Ranjona asked, a hopeful look filling her expression.

While Tetsu's natural sense of direction was generally good, being lost in such a dangerous-looking area prompted him to swallow his pride and use the only navigational tool he had, a small (and specially colored) green and blue Pokemon Navigator, or Pokenav for short. Pressing the blue button in the center, the top screen flipped up and turned over, showing a small digital map of the area. In large red letters, the area was identified "The Red Forest," but the vagueness of the map of the area didn't help him re-orient himself. Regretting the bad decision he made and having no real way to retrace his steps in this wild environment, Tetsu just kept going however he thought was right.

After a moment of walking through the cool and dark thicket, Tetsu suddenly heard a sharp crack and a sudden harsh pain upon his back. Stumbling forward from the impact, Tetsu quickly reoriented himself and looked over his shoulder to try and see where the whip came from. Laughter rang through the branches as Tetsu espied a raggedy woman high up in the trees. Wearing scraggy clothes made from leaves and old garments, the fit 30-something woman wielded a fearsome whip.

"Hey, what was that about?!" Tetsu yelled. "Boy! You're a Pokemon Trainer, aren't ya?" she replied harshly. Tetsu nodded his head, face contorted in an annoyed rage. “Hah! I knew it! All you pokemon trainers… You make me SICK!” the woman cackled and leaped from the tree. Landing, she had her whip at the ready.

"Woah, what did I ever do to you?" Tetsu responds, holding his hands up in front of his face in an attempt to protect them. "For your information, my Pokemon are companions and would never leave my side and vice versa!"

Yey another update ^-^ and for the sake of moving the story along I don't mind.

Stacey trained her sapphire orbs on Ranjona's own, hoping to recieve an answer for all her questions. She had been drug away from that shrine, the reason that she had come to this small island and she wanted to know why. She could still feel the shrine calling to her now, like her organs were being softly tugged in one direction and it was unnerving, something that this strong-willed ghost trainer was not used to.

Ranjona oblidged and proceeding to tell the tale of the shrine, built in honor of the God of all ghosts, Giratina. Stacey felt her intrigue rise at the mention of the legend of all ghost pokemon and listened on for more of the tale. She explained how ritual sacrifices were performed, all the cases willing, happy to ascend into the nether realm to meet a greater fate. The humanity roared inside of Stacey, angry at the thought of such practice but she knew that a person chose their own fate and had seen much worse things befall a person along her travels. Along with the psychic legend Cressila, another God they worshiped the two pokemon seemed part of these people's religion, one in trune with life and the other with death and should one become corrupted by any means the other would provide nourishing purity. A temple had been erected in their honor not far off and Stacey silently hoped that she could visit it when she had the time.

Sitting down on a cold hard rock next to the cavern wall Stacey let all this new information sink in, her head tilting in that fashion when she was deep in thought. The next question rolled off her tongue easily before she had much time to contemplate it,

"Why were you in such a hurry to get away from the shrine?" she asked with a hint of bitterness lacing her voice though it was not intentional.

Ranjona seemed to ponder over her words before she spoke. She explained how being seen on that shrine, especially with how things were right now was a foolish idea and could lead to an untimely death. A new heir to the kingdom's throne had been chosen, King Amadoro and since his reign several people had one missing only to wind up dead days later. He had even sacrificed his own own father to Giratina apparantly in an effort to be crowned king. Stacey frowned, it sounded like some of their information was based on assumptions but she couldn't deny that it sounded suspicious. What sort of man would sacrifice their own father?

It seemed that recently the ritual sacrifices had become less optional and more enforced; with more people going missing only to be sacrificed soon after. Ranjona added with hatred lining her voice that he was meerly using this as a tool, a means to an end to eliminate all threats to the throne and instill fear into the hearts of his people. His soldiers lined the streets searching for someone, anyone that would rebel and snatch them for the sacrifice.

Ranjona's voice became small and she held back a sniffle as she told Stacey that her own father and brother had been sacrificed at that blood covered alter. Stacey felt her gaze soften as she placed a comforting hand on the older womans' shoulder, she knew exactly what it was like to have family murdered, she had lost her mother, father and brother in an arson attack years ago but the wounds still felt fresh and tore at her.

Ranjona composed herself and continued her story, telling her of a group that they had formed, a resistance called 'The Full Moon' with their main goal being to overthrow their corrupt king and ultimately summon Cressila to help free Giratina, ending her story there.

Stacey found her mind a swirl of thought and she ran one pale hand through her dark locks, making sense of all she had just been told. It seemed that she had somehow found herself drawn into the beginnings of an all out war between a corrupt king and his people. Did she really want to interfere with such matters? Was it her place to?

Interupting her thoughts Ranjona posed a question of her own to the young woman,

“We have a safely guarded hideout not too far from here. We could use all the help we can muster. Would you help our endeavor?” she asked with a hopeful voice.

Stacey's frown deepened wrinkling up her smooth skin, that was alot to ask a stranger. How did Ranjona know she could trust her? How did she knew that she was not a spy? Maybe she was the trusting kind or maybe Stacey just looked so different it was impossible for her to be anything other than a stranger. Stacey was not sure she wanted to get involved in all this, but dark though niggled at the back of her mind. To get back to the shore to leave on the cruise ship that would not return for many days she would have to return to the shrine, one that she now knew was guarded by soliders loyal to an evil, manipulative King intent on slaughtering anyone he could, that would be her fate should she chose that path.

On the other hand she was so very intrigued at the possibility of seeing two legends, especially Giratina. It was after all a ghost and knowing that it was 'captured' and being abused for such sickening purposes made her blood boil. She could not tolerate such actions towards her kin.

Locking eyes that shone with silent determination with Ranjona once more Stacey began to speak.

"You have spoke of so much to me in such a short space of time that I feel I am still processing it all. I am sorry to hear of what your king has done to you but, and forgive me for saying this, is it really my place to interfere? I am a stranger from an entirely different world, why should alter the course of action here? But, I have been brought here by some force I know nothing of, maybe this is fate, I cannot say ..."

Pausing as she thought over her next words she shook her hair loose from her hood and spoke once more.

"I will accompany you to your safehouse. I am intrigued to hear more about your situation and the plight of the legends of ghost and psychic pokemon. I feel that I need to be here for some reason and our meeting may be more than meer coincidence. I will offer what help I can in what way I feel is right." she says in a diplomatic fashion awaiting Ranjona's response.
