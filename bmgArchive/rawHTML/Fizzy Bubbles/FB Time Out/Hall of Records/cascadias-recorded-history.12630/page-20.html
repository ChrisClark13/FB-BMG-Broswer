What will you do?

CASCADIA​
Far off of the coasts of the mainland Fizzy Bubble zones, remote in the Silver Ocean, an island chain sits in basically, the middle of nowhere. These islands contain the some of the most diverse typing of Pokemon in the entirety of Fizzy Bubbles. All of these islands are actually volcanoes, but the majority of them have gone dormant. As many Pokemon spend all their lives here, many fossils of Pokemon and plants are buried in the depths of the volcanic ground, maybe some of these may be worth a look? You start out on the island that you first choose, via a ferry. In order to change areas afterward, Surf or Fly is a necessity.


Lily Island
With a totally different landscape from the first, Lily Island is the second largest of the archipelago. Completely covered with vegetation, this island was also formed by a volcano. However, erosion has taken this island down to almost flat, and giving many homes to all types of grass and bug Pokemon among others. Inside the dense jungle, which takes up about a half of the island, there is a very small lake. There are two different forests on the island, and you can tell when you've gone from one forest to another. Besides being divided by the Diamond River, some strange force causes them to have completely different climates as well...
The Silver Forest - The forest which takes up a bit more than half of the island, Silver Island is named for the silver veins the leaves are adorned with. A deciduous forest, you'll mainly see flying and bug types here, due to the lack of ground vegetation. There is a rest stop somewhere in the forest, helping trainers on their way.

Hugo and Krabby wander aimlessly through the large forest. Hugo seems entertained by the adventure, but Krabby seemed less than impressed, even a bit annoyed.

"Would you look at these trees? Have you ever seen trees like this before?" Hugo said to the crab Pokemon.

*click...click* responded Krabby unenthusiastically with it's large pincers.

"Look, you don't have to get an attitude," Hugo retorted.

*click*

"Oh, is that so?" Hugo argued.

Krabby seemed to be winning this battle of wits. Hugo knew Krabby and knew his sardonic attitude. Perhaps Krabby was just bored. Krabby wouldn't be all that enthused by trees to begin with. Why would he? Or maybe he was just hungry. Either way, Hugo tried not to take it personally. After a few minutes more of wondering, Hugo cleared a little brush off the ground and plopped down, pulling a sandwich from his bag. He ate the sandwich and tossed a bag of potato chips to Krabby. Krabby liked eating the chips almost as much as he liked tearing the bag to shreds.

Hugo looked up from his sandwich and gave a sly smirk to Krabby, who responded with a...smile...I guess. If Krabby could smile, that's what he was doing. The duo continued their lunch, taking a break from searching for adventure. They would let the adventure come to them.

In a cavern beneath Aduro Island's Geyser Park in the Cascadia archipelago, the young trainer known as William Hale hollers out encouragement to his Kingler Keo as the overgrown river crab continues to trade blows with their mysterious adversary's antagonistic Magmar. As Keo rushes in for a Crabhammer, the Spitfire Pokemon retaliates by belching out a thick cloud of black smoke which in such a tiny enclosed space, instantly envelops the battlefield, severly obscuring Will's vision and causing him to cough and splutter and cover his mouth with his forearm.

The din of combat echoes throughout the rocky catacomb however and as Will tries to avoid breathing in any more of the noxious smoke, he strains his ears trying to make sense of the sounds he hears and wonders how his cantankerous crustacean comrade is fairing. Just then, a flash of light catches the lad's eye followed closely by the sound of a deafening blow as it connects with its intended target before the room fades into total silence. All Will can do is hold his breath, hope for the best, and wait for the smoke to clear.

And clear it does. Moments later, the dark cloud dissipates and Will lets out a grateful sigh, relieved to see that Keo has emerged victorious and now stands above the fallen form of the mysterious man's Magmar. On the contrary, Will's opponent stands along the opposite wall, a look of complete astonishment on his face at this unexpected turn of events. Scowling, the man quickly recalls his fainted partner and makes a mad dash for the far end of the tunnel. Before Will can react and give chase however, the whole place gives one giant lurch and pieces of the stony wall and ceiling suddenly rain down to block the passageway and seal off the man's only escape route.

Turning to face Will, the man adopts a combat stance and shouts out a threat, before running straight for the lad, who bolts in shock at the man's aggressive attitude. Before the man can attack however, he trips over some of the jagged rubbles that are strewn about the ground and lands face first on the cavern's rocky floor where he lays without moving. Seeing the man's predicament, Will softens his face then turns to Keo and the two momentarily share a look. With a shrug, Will motions for Keo to follow him and the two friends trot over to help the man to his feet. Perhaps now, Will reasons, their foe will be a bit more civil...or at least willing to talk...

Storm Lake - A crescent moon shaped lake that surrounds the north and east sides of the Power Plant, Storm Lake is an electrically charged freshwater lake. Only a few Pokemon live inside the lake, but many electric types come to cool off.

CASCADIA​
Far off of the coasts of the mainland Fizzy Bubble zones, remote in the Silver Ocean, an island chain sits in basically, the middle of nowhere. These islands contain the some of the most diverse typing of Pokemon in the entirety of Fizzy Bubbles. All of these islands are actually volcanoes, but the majority of them have gone dormant. As many Pokemon spend all their lives here, many fossils of Pokemon and plants are buried in the depths of the volcanic ground, maybe some of these may be worth a look? You start out on the island that you first choose, via a ferry. In order to change areas afterward, Surf or Fly is a necessity.

Crater Island
Like the other islands of the archipelago, Crater Island is an inactive volcano. After what seems to be an excessively violent eruption, the empty crater filled with water, forming a lake. Many water types of many types live in the fresh water lake, and there are legends that some things lie in the depths of the lake...Possibly underwater caverns?

Iridi Beach - Formed by some strange mineral, Iridi Beach's sand is a very bright, sparkly white. Rather than black, like most volcanic sand, this is more of the sand you might find at a normal beach. A few sunbathing Pokemon who venture from the water may be found here, but mostly, this is just a tourist attraction.
Click to expand...
Bright sun, white sand and ocean as far as the eyes could see. This was Iridi Beach in all its summer glory! The beach was populated by tourists and Pokémon alike, all eager to soak in the sun and have a splash in the ocean. Noriko had seen this place in a television special and decided to have look for herself. The sun was shining as brightly as ever, but Noriko was stationed on the beach under a large umbrella. Lightly clothed, wearing sunglasses and sunblock and a book about Grass Pokémon in hand, Noriko was relaxing on a beach towel while her partner, a small Skitty, was rolling around in the sand.

"Skitty! Don't go too far!" Noriko kindly warned her partner. She'd already applied some sunblock on Skitty in fear of the Pokémon possibly getting sunburns. The Trainer herself got herself burned in the sun pretty easily, leading her to get an umbrella as protection against the offensive sunlight.

The duo was taking a breather from their journey, which Noriko had thought would be a good idea...until she found herself stuck on a beach, under the scorching sun, with nothing to do. On the other hand Skitty could entertain herself just fine. Her Trainer sighed beneath her umbrella, a little jealous of Skitty's ability to have a good time nearly anywhere. Setting her book aside, Noriko lay on her back on the blue towel. She was already trying to figure out what their next destination would be - on this island and beyond.

"Okay, we've been to the ice cream stand, the Pokémon Center, the Pokémart...is there anywhere else we should go before we move on?" Being a beginning Trainer set its limits on the places the two could travel to. For the time being, Noriko would have to try and make the best of Iridi Beach.

With Skitty strolling right by her side Noriko moved along the beach. The Crater Island was sitting in the distance, catching her attention. She recalled it to be a spot Fire Pokémon liked to hang around – definitely a place of interest for any Trainer or Specialist. Skitty started chasing her tail while waiting for Noriko to think of something to do. Two natures of these two seemed to be polar opposites, which could have been what drew them together in the first place, like magnets.

Dragged from her thoughts by a small sound, Noriko looked up at the Crater Island. The sound returned, a faint but painful sound, right before it was covered by a wave. After a bit of looking around, Noriko stopped something: a creature, big and blue, some distance away. Could it be a Pokémon? It wailed in pain again. Noriko looked at Skitty, who was looking back at the Trainer. Now what?

Skitty started jumping at Noriko’s hips – to be precise, at her Pokéball belt. After a few whacks Noriko understood her partner. “You’re right Skitty, this is the perfect opportunity to bring out Callias!” She picked Callias’ Pokéball and threw it at the water. A Finneon came out, calmly watching his surroundings. Noriko called Skitty back into her Pokéball before walking into the water over to Callias. “Hey there, you can hear that noise over there? That’s a Pokémon I want to help out, so could you use Surf and get me over there?”

It is said that a small freshwater lake is somewhere in this forest as well.

Karmas and Paine stood at the entrance of the dense forest, the pair of them not quite sure what to think of their surroundings. Until finally, thanks to the prodding from Paine, who was raring for an adventure, he finally left. And here he was, at Cascadia.

"I'm sorry Paine, I'm just trying to generate some atmosphere," said Karmas, walking into the Red Forest.
