Dialga's Gate - Final Singularity​

Echoes of Time
Primal Dialga Raid​


For weeks no, Dialga's Gate has been weakening. Since last month, the portal has been primarily closed, besides when it spits people out that were adventuring there. Every time that has happened, a loud, ear-piercing cry washes over all of Fizzytopia. This was always followed by a brilliant flash of orange and dark blue light. That was the only the first stage of oddities.

The land around New Fizz City has been corrupted, turning a sickening gray. Time around the portal has become distorted, and people who go near start to have flashes of both the past and the future. All possibilities that may have or will occur. This has created a sort of temporal lag within the region, where time will pass either incredibly slowly or quickly. There have been reports of a child going out to play and coming out an old woman. There have also been reports of an older couple getting sucked in, only to reappear back in their prime.

Something horrible was happening around the gate, and it was ripping through the very fabric of reality. That was until trainers all across Fizzytopia received the call. A portal appeared beneath them, and they were transported to a world that was completely still. Rocks hung still, there was no wind, and there was nothing in the sky but dark clouds. The planet itself was frozen within time. But, there was something that called them there. A plea, of sorts.

That's when they all saw it. Dialga, perched atop a broken down tower. Behind Dialga was a sight that elicited emotions that could not be described with words. It was something that went against the very nature of our reality. Something that would call one who peered into it for too long to go insane. Maddening whispers crawled through every trainer's mind. Here was the beginning of the end. Palkia's armor was orbiting a black hole.

Then, a faint shimmer of hope occurred. A pale, blue dot was on the horizon to only flicker then fade. This was the true power of Dialga being corrupted. This was Primal Dialga, the being created before Arceus at the very dawn of creation. And if the trainers did not stop it, this could be the very end of the world as they knew it. This was Dialga's Final Singularity. All possibilities led to this one end. Here, not even the echoes of time could be here. It was silent darkness. Darkness that would consume until nothing was left. It was time to fight.

Leo was sitting in his apartment when he heard the call. The place was quaint, a two bedroom apartment that was above his cafe. Of course, he still needed to actually finish up opening up the cafe, but it was fine. He only needed to a few more shipments to come in and he could open his doors.

But none of that really mattered here. He felt strange ever since he was so suddenly evicted from Diagla's Gate. Not even when he was exploring the Cradle or dealing with weird magic in the Arcane Realm had he felt this way. There was something fundamentally wrong here. And it just wasn't him that could feel it.

Custard was sitting next to him, the usually playful Togetic seeming despondent. The two have been together always, and Leo could tell when something was wrong. With everything they've been through, and all the years they've had, it was obvious this was weighing down on him.

That's when a big, orange fluff ball plopped itself on top of him. Leo let out a small yelp in shock, and that seemed to be enough to have Custard laughing a little bit. The Raichu was his roommate's, who he had recently met over the past few months and ended up deciding they'd rent an apartment together. Prices were cheap in the City of Pentacles, and the man seemed to have interest in conquering the places of power in the Arcane Realm.

"Lucas, good to see you! I guess you guys are back already then?" Leo asked the Raichu,

"Rai rai!" The Raichu responded with a wide happy grin before nuzzling him more.

"Sup," he heard his roommate. Turning his head, he was greeted with piercing, ethereal green eyes. The man put his bag down and sort of just slumped into the closest arm piece.

"Any thing interesting today Ethan?" Leo asked before getting hit with a bad migraine.

"Weaklings mostly. Though I heard that there's a Gigantamax Cinderace running amok near New Fizz City," the other boy responded with cold, distant words.

That was when the flash of Dialga's visage crossed Leo's mind. It was obvious enough that something wrong was happening with the gate. The Alolan teen jumped up, causing the Togetic and Raichu to react with shock.

"I'll go with you then," Leo muttered, going quickly to his room to grab his bag. A few of his Pokemon were lounging around, but he ignored the fairy-typed crew and rushed through getting his items. With a hop, skip, and a jump he was rushing Custard out the door.

Ethan followed behind, not understanding why his roommate was in such a hurry. The entire ride to New Fizz was quiet, and the two parted ways as soon as they arrived at the abandoned city.

That was when Leo was swallowed by the singularity, and saw the horros of what had actually happened. How long had Dialga suffered? Was being consumed by its temporal corruption. Enough to turn Palkia into a new big crunch. Others soon joined him, also answering the call of Dialga. This was truly a fight to the finish.

It had been but a day or two since Keith Masters returned from ancient Romma, and the time rift on the outskirts of New Fizz City slammed shut behind them... but things were not quite settling back to normal. The surrounding area had become corrupted somehow, with time moving very strangely. And things only seemed to be getting worse and worse. And so people were avoiding the area like the plague. Until one day, a number of conveniently-opened portals sent a number of Fizzytopia's finest tumbling into something they were not prepared for.

Among these Trainers were Keith Masters and Willa Carnet, who were helping each other up as they looked around. Moments before, they'd been on their way to the Sludge Wave Coast Pokémon Center, so that Willa could pick up the Pokémon she'd left there for Helena to heal following a particularly intense battle.

"...where are we?" Willa murmured, as she helped Keith back up.

"I..." Keith said, with no idea how he'd finish that sentence. Where were they, indeed? A dismal, grey world, where rocks hung still in midair, the wind blew not, and the sky above was a vast expanse of dark clouds. He shivered involuntarily. "I don't know, but it scares me," he said quietly.

Willa, in response, put an arm around her boyfriend in a comforting way. "It scares me, too," she said. "But we will be fine."

Keith smiled gratefully at Willa, but before this could lead to any sort of moment, the couple's attention was drawn to the top of a broken tower, where there stood a terrifying figure. A four-legged beast with a diamond set into its chest. This was the legendary Pokémon Dialga. But something was wrong- its body was too dark a shade of blue, and streams of pulsing orange energy, looking almost like lava, could be seen. And its diamond was blood red.

"...What are we looking at here?" Keith squeaked.

"I've heard stories," Willa murmured. "Terrible tales of a bleak and dismal future, a world brought to a halt, where time does not flow, because time itself is corrupted. And this world," she added, glancing around. "...it certainly fits the bill."

"I'd say so," Keith shuddered, glancing around once more.

"And in that case, then what we're seeing here..." Willa added, turning back to the terrifying legendary Pokémon before them. "...this is surely what's called Primal Dialga. But what's... what's behind it?" she murmured, trying to look around the corrupted embodiment of time.

Keith looked around Primal Dialga as well, and his stomach turned over at what he saw. He knew of the legendary Pokémon Palkia, and considering they were right in front of Dialga, he wouldn't have been too terribly surprised to see it at this point. But to see it in this capacity, that's not something a person can prepare for. "...what's left of Palkia orbiting a black hole," he replied shakily. "Willa, I think I'm gonna be sick..."

At this Willa pulled Keith close to her. "Take a deep breath and get ahold of yourself," she said, softly yet firmly. "It's obvious that we have to battle Primal Dialga, and from the looks of things, we aren't the only Trainers being brought here for this, either. Unfortunately, that portal had some exquisitely bad timing," she sighed. "So there's nothing I can do beyond supporting you. But you... you do have some of your Pokémon with you, right?" she asked.

"Yeah, yeah I do," Keith nodded, feeling around, nodding as he felt the balls on his belt. "And actually... I might just have the answer here," he added, plucking one sphere in particular off his belt. "They say to fight fire with fire... so why don't we fight this legendary Pokémon..." he clicked the button, causing the Violet Cyber Ball to grow to its full size with a whirring sound. "...with a legendary Pokémon?"

Willa looked confused, but only momentarily- she quickly realized what Keith was getting at, and smiled at him, before pulling him in for a quick kiss. "Now that's the Keith Masters I know and love," she smirked. "I would love to see this in action."

That encouragement was all Keith needed to hear. He turned his hat backwards and threw the Cyber Ball. "Here we go! Jack, let's do this!" Keith exclaimed. And in a flash of light, the ball split open, unleashing its purple blob of an occupant.

"Ditto dit- DITTO?!?!?" Jack squeaked, bubbling in surprise as he spotted the monstrosity before him.

"Jack," Keith said seriously. "There may well be a lot riding on this battle. So I figure our best shot is to use Primal Dialga's own power against it. You're our best shot. What do you say?"

"Diiit... Ditto!" nodded Jack, oozing forward determinedly. Keith and Willa looked at each other, each thinking the same thing- this could very well be the end. But if indeed it was, they were gonna go down fighting.

He had been caught off-guard by the ground vanishing beneath his feet, and had been in this Arceus-forsaken plain of oblivion for a couple of hours by now, hiding behind some debris while he continued to evade the vision of that which was in the midst of divine fury. With how chaotic the world had apparently become in her absence, it had apparently been pretty easy for the others to find an alcove for her to rest in, but that hadn’t really helped make her sleep any less restless. For some reason, she had felt like she was falling the entire time…

“Unfortunately, another complication arose while you were sleeping. It seems that despite the fact that whatever force that was governing the Gate claimed that it was closed, it isn’t quite done with you yet.” The young man motioned to Chroma, who was sitting a little ways away with an obviously strained look of focus dominating her expression. “Thankfully, she’s been proving her worth as the Grand Magus by holding off the portal that’s been trying to whisk you back away from us, but it’s clearly taking a toll on her.”

Ami immediately bolted up and rushed to her sister’s side at this revelation. “Are you okay, Chroma?!”

The stupid question recieved a surprisingly unsarcastic answer. “N-not really. Fighting deific powers is… incredibly draining…!”

“As soon as she lets go, willingly or otherwise, it looks like you’ll be thrown into a battle whose scope far beyond anything I had ever expected us to deal with.” Silic heaved a sigh that somehow oozed both resignation and disbelief simultaneously. “According to what little detail we can gather, the call leads right to Dialga, the very God of Time itself. We’re not talking about some physical avatar here, but the very essence of temporal divinity itself. Given the sheer number of similar occurrences that have been apparently occurring, it would seem that some force is trying to gather forces to beat it down in Raid-style combat.”

“That still sounds absolutely terrifying,” Ami mused, having found herself surprisingly unsurprised. Despite the fact that the Raid concept should’ve been completely foreign to her, the traveler seemed to recall participating in several of such confrontations despite having been in Romma during the time those events would have occurred. Had two timelines converged into one here?

Confusion aside, the mission briefing still had to continue. “‘Terrifying’ is certainly one way to put it. Fortunately, an... ally has been able to provide us with some valuable insight on your seemingly insurmountable foe. Apparently, Time Incarnate functions as a Dragon/Steel Type. Unfortunately, the same temporal distortion that gave us this unexpected opportunity also pulled away Dichromatia’s mawile, and being unable to access my Boxes means that my metagross is out of the question as well. All I have on me is a young and inexperienced aerodactyl, so you’re almost certainly better off using one of your own pokemon for thi-”

“Fontaine!” Ami cut him off by calling out her own metang as an idea came to her. “If we need a metagross for this situation, then we can take advantage of its line’s unique evolution methods by having this guy pluck a parallel version of themselves from their corresponding timestream to make one, right?”

“...That may actually be a decent, but risky plan.” Silic pondered for a moment before standing up and leading the way to the makeshift hideout’s entrance. “And the best one we currently have. Let’s hurry and get the ball rolling, shall we?”
