You could feel the tendrils of sleep wrap around you and take you in. Even so you asked your Elekid to stand watch for you in case things got weird, you didn't feel its pull on you. You just fell deeper and deeper into this peaceful sleep. The sound of the wind swaying across the branches, and the warmth of the sun only helped to aid you falling to sleep. There wasn't much you could do, and you were left adrift.

After a while, you wake up in a wooden room. The wood also had that white, ethereal glow that the tree had. The bed you were in was soft and comfortable, and it seemed to envelop you in its warmth whenever you moved your body. Looking around, you couldn't see your Elekid anywhere, but you felt at ease none the less.

"Oh, you're finally awake," a masculine voice rings through your head. "Well, awake isn't the proper term, you're still very much asleep."

Looking around, you soon spot the origin of the voice. At least, what you think is its origin. Two figures stand in a doorway and slowly enter the room. They look pretty much identical, with both of them wearing flowing, white kimonos with pink obi. Their silvery white hair shines in the room, and pink eyes seem to stare through. You could tell the one on the left was male, based on more sharper features and shorter hair, while the one on the right was female, based on softer features and longer hair.

"Don't worry, we're here to guide you through this place," the girl speaks, he voice angelic and soft on the ears.

"We are the spirits of the Great Tree. It is the heart of this orchard, but rarely shows itself to those it does not deem worthy," the boy chimes in, his voice more rougher, but has a warmth to it that you've never felt.

"It has been long since we have received a guest brother! Though, it is a shame we couldn't bring your friend along... This is a place of dreams after all," the girl sighs in disappointment. She sounded pretty excited about this too.

"It's okay, we still have a guest after all these years," he starts to try and cheer her up before looking at you. "I do want to warn you though, the Great Tree has great power. While we are the guides for the living, please do no stray too far from us. We can't save you if you drift too far."

Well, it seems that these mysterious twins are your ticket out of here, in the sense of waking up. Though, this all feels a bit too surreal. Surely if you just fall back asleep, you'd wake up underneath the tree. Though, it doesn't seem to be that easy now, does it?

What will you do?

His guide’s silver hair and pink eyes were striking, almost haunting. But surely, given their demeanor, they meant no harm, right? They could be trusted to see Hugo though his journey in this mysterious world.

The girl starts to speak after you start asking about this place, but her brother just places his ring finger on his mouth, and she silences herself. You walk through a white hallway filled with ethereal light. There seemed to be nothing in this hallway, but every so often you could see what seemed like white wisps briefly drift through the air, or a pulse of darker light go through the hallway. The twins don't stop at this, and just keep going. Seemed to be a normal occurrence in the tree.

After what felt like an hour, you're led into another room. This one is much smaller, but there is a window to your left. The two motion you towards it, and you go to look out it.

"What you're seeing now is the root system. it course through the entirety of the Great Tree, giving it life. We do recommend, though, to not go near it. Least of all you want to end up food," the boy speaks, before heading out of the room into the next hallway.

"The next room is where your first test will be," the girl tells you, waiting for you to go into the hallway before following you. "It's a bit more dangerous from here. Us encircling you is the safest for outsiders. Don't worry, we don't want to harm you."

That last line was a bit concerning, but there wasn't much time to think on it as you entered a room. It was plain, with a small table with a pink goblet in the center. The goblet was filled with some type of liquid, and a flower petal floated within.

"Drink," both of them said in unison. "How your mind reacts to this... Will determine your course through this tree."

What do you do?

Put-upon -- that's you. You don't entirely know how you got here, and you're not entirely sure what's going on, but now you've been tasked with saving the city from some sort of bug-driven apocalypse. You're either too nice or too weak willed to say no, so -- after taking a moment to survey the sights of the frankly beautiful city -- you set off .

You have a little bit of trouble navigating at first. Urban planning doesn't seem to be a concept that exists here, and though paved and well established, most of the streets have the tale-tell, chaotic twists and turns of roads that had cropped up organically over the years. There are almost no signs to help you get your bearings, which only makes things worse, but after a while -- and a fair amount of backtracking out of dead ends -- the buildings around you begin to think, until they're behind you and all that's left of civilization is the occasional farm. The countryside is rather idyllic, and... oddly familiar to you. Jaboca trees, having escaped their farms, stand near the road, casting a splotchy shade over you. Past a fence, in the distance, a Mudbray idles around a field. In a Jaboca tree to your right, a pair of Taillow watch you pass, seeming to glare at you. It seems they distrust your presence, in the way that prey Pokémon tend to do. They're actually pretty cute, with their surly little faces, and they hold your attention just long enough for you to walk into something.

Well, no, that's not quite accurate. It's more like... something flew into you. It pelts you right in the side of the head; the impact is powerful enough to sting, but not much else. Whatever struck you, however, goes reeling, spiralling to the ground with a constant, whining buzz. You almost could swear that the frequency of the buzzing itself fell too, as if the thing were producing its own sound effects; looking down, you see a very small, yellow creature with four thin limbs, a frightening-looking proboscis, and two oddly square eyes laying in the dirt of the path. After a moment its translucent wings kick to life, and it pulls itself airborne again. It turns its attention to you and begins buzzing angrily, seemingly upset that you dare got in its way, thrusting itself in your face in an utterly failed attempt to intimidate. The map in your hand briefly catches its attention, and it performs the most obvious, over-exaggerated double-take that you've ever seen, before swooping for it. Grasping it with all four limbs, it tugs with all its strength as it tries to abscond with it, but its tiny, lightweight body doesn't have the strength to so much as budge the scroll from your grasp.


What will you do?

To your right the structure you're on continues over the gorge to solid land a few yards away, and then onwards towards what appears to be a city in the distance. To the left the gorge -- and the structure -- span for a ways, before reaching the other side, which is at a slightly higher altitude than the one closer to you. The aqueduct inclines appropriately to bridge the distance, and the water flows downwards according to gravity. It would be a little difficult, with the current and the incline, but you could scale it if you really wanted to.
