Greca​


[ Raves ]​

You find yourself somewhere that you don't recognize. The architecture around you is marble, and the olive trees are in bloom. In front of you is a particularly stunning building, with a high ceiling and tall columns. Between you and it is a pristine reflecting pool, inside of which lily pads have begun to grow. Deciding it would be best to find another human being, you and Xigua make your way towards the opulent building, passing the pool on your way. Bubbles rise up from the bottom, attracting your attention; as you pause to see what it is, an arthropod rises to the surface, two of its thin, jointed legs clutching an air bubble trapping its head. It looks at you a moment, and then disappears under the water again; its body is wiry and lacking in substance, but you can just barely see it flit across the pool floor.

Just then, your attention is pulled away from the creature in the pond, by the sound of human voices. Two gentlemen, wearing what can only be described as 'period' clothing, descend the steps of the large building, talking loudly.

"This is the fault of the priestesses," one of them accuses. He has a dense, flaxen-colored beard. His companion is graying, beard a shortly-cropped salt-and-pepper mix.

"The priestesses have done their best," rebuts the graying man as they pass by you. He pauses to fix you with a strange look, but then pulls his attention away, returning to his conversation. "You shouldn't take it out on them..."

Well, you've found people, but they seem pre-occupied with something. Maybe you shouldn't interrupt them? You could check out that thing in the pool. Then again, you probably shouldn't be wandering around this place. It might be best to bite the bullet and ask these people what's going on, and how to get out of here. What was that they were talking about, though? Priestesses? If they're reenactors, they're definitely taking their roles seriously.

What will you do?

The change in scenery was a bit of a surprise for Rory, yet upon having another look around, things seemed a little more than extravagant for a reenactment. For one, those olive trees were in bloom, and that pool was glistening. So glistening in fact, he could see his reflection in it from a distance. The waters rippled, startling the teen lightly, before something approached from beneath the waters. The source of the disturbance was an unusual insect, wiry limbs supporting an orb of what seemed like air - no, water.

"A dewpider?" he drolled, scratching his head. "Last I checked, New Fizz didn't have any habitats suitable for many bugs around it, much less pond dwellers due to pollutants..." he continued, as Xigua watched the fellow water type dip below the surface and skitter away. A couple of voices then echoed, as the bug and boy whirled to face the source of noise. Two men, actors perhaps? But those were some really good period outfits they wore, and those beards...did they mention priestesses?

Rory racked his brains, deeply puzzled. "Xig...if this were a reenactment, then the water in that pond would still have some small traces of pollution, like little bits of mud, but that's clear as day. Those robes, too, look too good to be the work of hobbyists." The Wimpod chirruped in response. "It might be rude to interrupt them if they're rehearsing, but I think we should head inside the temple and look around. Let's be respectful though: no scavenging, no peeking under robes, and treat this place like a proper grecan reenactment, with old grecan greetings and no mentions of New Fizz until we find someone who's in charge here." Rory looked back at the pond before heading for the temple. That dewpider must be a trained pokemon, he mused.
